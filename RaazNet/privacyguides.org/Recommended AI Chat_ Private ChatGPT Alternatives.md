# AI Chat

Protects against the following threat(s):

- [Service Providers](https://www.privacyguides.org/en/basics/common-threats/#privacy-from-service-providers)

- [Censorship](https://www.privacyguides.org/en/basics/common-threats/#avoiding-censorship)

The use of **AI chat**, also known as Large Language Models (LLMs), has become increasingly common since the release of ChatGPT in 2022.

## Privacy Concerns About LLMs

Data used to train AI models includes a massive amount of publicly available data scraped from the web.

If you are concerned about these practices, you can either refuse to use AI, or use [truly open-source models](https://proton.me/blog/how-to-build-privacy-first-ai).

Alternatively, you can run AI models locally so that your data never leaves your device.

## AI Models

### Hardware for Local AI Models

Local models are also fairly accessible.

## AI Chat Clients

### Kobold.cpp

**Kobold.cpp** is an AI client that runs locally on your Windows, Mac, or Linux computer.

### Ollama (CLI)

**Ollama** is a command-line AI assistant that is available on macOS, Linux, and Windows.

### Llamafile

**Llamafile** is a lightweight, single-file executable that allows users to run LLMs locally on their own computers.

## Securely Downloading Models

We recommend downloading model files from Hugging Face since it provides several features to verify that your download is genuine and safe to use.

## Criteria

Please note we are not affiliated with any of the projects we recommend.

### Minimum Requirements

- Must be open source.
- Must not transmit personal data, including chat data.
- Must be multi-platform.
- Must not require a GPU.
- Must support GPU-powered, fast inference.
- Must not require an internet connection.
