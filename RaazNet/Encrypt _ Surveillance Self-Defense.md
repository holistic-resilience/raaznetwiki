# Encrypt

To scramble information or a message mathematically so that it seems meaningless, but can still be restored to its original form by a person or device that possesses a piece of [data ![](https://ssd.eff.org/assets/efforg/info-7ae1fb29e1e734b20023f3e3470316c82b84a7e4b9f1d1e2f49e399f1f8de2d3.png)](https://ssd.eff.org/zh-hans/glossary/data) that can unscramble it (a [key ![](https://ssd.eff.org/assets/efforg/info-7ae1fb29e1e734b20023f3e3470316c82b84a7e4b9f1d1e2f49e399f1f8de2d3.png)](https://ssd.eff.org/zh-hans/glossary/key).) This limits who can access the information or message because without the right key, it is nearly impossible to reverse the [encryption ![](https://ssd.eff.org/assets/efforg/info-7ae1fb29e1e734b20023f3e3470316c82b84a7e4b9f1d1e2f49e399f1f8de2d3.png)](https://ssd.eff.org/zh-hans/glossary/encryption) and recover the original information. Encryption is one of several technologies that make up the field called [cryptography ![](https://ssd.eff.org/assets/efforg/info-7ae1fb29e1e734b20023f3e3470316c82b84a7e4b9f1d1e2f49e399f1f8de2d3.png)](https://ssd.eff.org/zh-hans/glossary/cryptography).
