# Verizon Fined for Selling Location Data Without Consent

## The News

The U.S. Federal Communications Commission fined Verizon, alongside T-Mobile and AT&T, for selling customers' location data without consent through contracts with location aggregation companies. Verizon fought the fines, arguing that the law does not cover *device* location data, only *call* location data. The U.S. Court of Appeals for the 2nd Circuit disagreed and upheld the **$46.9 million fine** on the telecommunications giant for violations of the Communications Act.

**Source:** [Ars Technica - Court rejects Verizon claim that selling location data without consent is legal](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2025/09/court-rejects-verizon-claim-that-selling-location-data-without-consent-is-legal/)

## What You Can Do

### Understand How Your Phone Shares Location

Your phone is designed to give away your location—this is fundamental to how it works. Staying connected requires location information, and along the way, you leave records of these connections with third parties maintaining your connection to:

- Cellphone towers
- GPS satellites
- Bluetooth connections
- Wi-Fi networks

Note: Some features like [Apple's Find My](https://support.apple.com/en-za/guide/iphone/iph09b087eda/ios) can locate your device even when it's offline. While newer devices are improving privacy through [MAC address randomization](https://source.android.com/docs/core/connect/wifi-mac-randomization-behavior) on [both platforms](https://support.apple.com/guide/security/privacy-features-connecting-wireless-networks-secb9cb3140c/web), location exposure remains inherent to mobile connectivity.

### Tighten Your Location Settings

Location data brokers often obtain your location by embedding tracking code in third-party apps. Make their job harder:

1. Open your device's Settings app
2. Review location permissions for each app
3. Disable location access for apps that don't need it
4. Consider enabling location only when actively using specific apps

**Instructions:**
- **Android:** [Google's location permissions guide](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507?hl=en)
- **iPhone:** [Apple's location services guide](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102647)

### Uninstall Unneeded Apps

Perform regular app cleanups:

- Remove third-party apps you don't strictly need
- This eliminates apps with potentially problematic tracking code
- Reduces potential attack surface for malware
- You can always reinstall apps later when needed

### Disable Advertising IDs

Your device has unique identifiers designed for advertisers that enable location tracking. Disabling these adds another layer of protection.

**Learn how:** [Stop Ad-Based Location Tracking](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/stop-ad-based-location-tracking/)

### Continue Improving Your Mobile Security

While adjusting location settings, consider a broader review of your device security.

**Comprehensive guide:** [Your Smartphone and You: A Handbook for Modern Mobile Maintenance](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/your-smartphone-and-you-handbook-modern-mobile-maintenance/)
