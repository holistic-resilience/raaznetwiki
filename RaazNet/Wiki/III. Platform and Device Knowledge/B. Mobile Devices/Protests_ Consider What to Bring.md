# Protests: Consider What to Bring

What you carry to a protest can either help you respond to emergencies or become a pretext for harassment. Make these decisions only after completing your context and risk analysis, and coordinate with your affinity group to distribute resources according to each person's role.

## What Not to Bring

**Electronic devices** without first reviewing their contents. Consider:
- Is there sensitive information stored on the device?
- Your mobile phone can track your location—this has been used to identify and harass peaceful protesters

**Items that could cause legal trouble:**
- Anything that could be considered a weapon
- Illegal drugs or alcohol

## What to Bring

### Essential Items
- Any special medication you need
- Cash for emergencies
- Comfortable clothing that allows easy movement

### Communication
- If bringing a phone, consider a new SIM card and burner phone
- Top up airtime credit in advance
- Bring a spare battery
- Write emergency contacts on your arm or leg

### Health and Safety
- First aid kit
- Toilet paper and sanitary products
- High-energy food
- Water for drinking and washing wounds
- Pack supplies to last longer than the planned protest duration

### Protection
- Swimming goggles and bandana or scarf for tear gas
- Antacid solution for treating tear gas burns
- Camera for documenting abuses (optional)

### Identification
The decision to carry ID depends on your location:
- In some countries, lacking ID is grounds for arrest
- In others, there's no obligation to carry it and ID may be used against you
- Coordinate with your affinity group on whether to bring ID and what type

## Prepare for Being Searched

Authorities commonly stop and search protesters, ostensibly to prevent weapons or illegal items from entering protests. However, searches are also used for intimidation or harassment.

**Before the protest:**
- Consult a lawyer about legal constraints on police searches
- Understand both the legal rules and practical realities in your context
- Prepare and practice verbal resistance to searches (where legally permitted)
- Ensure you carry nothing illegal

**During a search:**
- Alert your affinity group immediately so everyone stays aware
