# Privacy-Respecting Health and Wellness Apps

Keep track of your health and fitness goals with these apps. Unlike mainstream alternatives, your personal health information remains private and under your control.

## Menstrual Cycle Tracking

Popular menstrual trackers like [Flo](https://techcrunch.com/2021/01/13/flo-gets-ftc-slap-for-sharing-user-data-when-it-promised-privacy) are notorious for collecting and sharing user data. Depending on your jurisdiction, this may lead to [legal consequences](https://forbes.com/sites/abigaildubiniecki/2024/11/14/post-roe-your-period-app-data-could-be-used-against-you) affecting reproductive autonomy.

### Drip

**Drip** is a gender-inclusive, open-source menstrual cycle tracker available on all mobile platforms. It uses the sympto-thermal method to predict ovulation. All user data is stored locally and can be password-protected.

- **Platforms:** Android (Google Play, F-Droid), iOS
- [Homepage](https://bloodyhealth.gitlab.io/) | [Privacy Policy](https://bloodyhealth.gitlab.io/privacy-policy.html) | [Source Code](https://gitlab.com/bloodyhealth/drip)

### Euki

**Euki** is a nonprofit-backed menstrual cycle tracker that doubles as a medication tracker and sexual wellness knowledge base. It allows scheduled automatic deletion of personal data. All user data is stored locally and can be password-protected.

- **Platforms:** Android, iOS
- [Homepage](https://eukiapp.org/) | [Privacy Policy](https://eukiapp.org/privacy-policy) | [Source Code](https://github.com/Euki-Inc/Euki-Android)

## Fitness Trackers

General-purpose apps for counting steps, tracking sleep, and measuring heart rate.

### Apple Fitness (iOS)

**Apple Fitness** is the default fitness app for iOS. It uses end-to-end encryption when syncing across devices, and almost all measured data is processed on-device.

- **Platform:** iOS only
- [Privacy Policy](https://apple.com/legal/privacy/consumer-health-personal-data/en-ww)

### Gadgetbridge (Android)

**Gadgetbridge** is an open-source Android application that allows you to pair and manage Bluetooth fitness devices without relying on vendor applications. When paired with a compatible smartwatch, it provides health and wellness functionality without third-party data collection.

- **Platform:** Android (F-Droid)
- [Homepage](https://gadgetbridge.org/) | [Source Code](https://codeberg.org/Freeyourgadget/Gadgetbridge)

> **Note:** Review the [compatibility list](https://gadgetbridge.org/gadgets) before purchasing a device. Some devices require initial connection to vendor servers before Gadgetbridge can be used.

## Health Records

Apps for collecting, managing, and sharing personal health data with providers.

### Apple Health Records (iOS)

**Apple Health Records** is a built-in feature within Apple Health that allows you to view, store, and share health records. It includes end-to-end encryption and on-device processing.

- **Platform:** iOS only
- [Homepage](https://apple.com/health) | [Privacy Policy](https://apple.com/legal/privacy/consumer-health-personal-data/en-ww)

### CommonHealth (Android)

**CommonHealth** is a privacy-respecting Android app for accessing electronic health records and securely sharing them with providers. All health data is stored on-device with passcode or biometric protection.

- **Platform:** Android (US only)
- [Homepage](https://commonhealth.org/) | [Privacy Policy](https://commonhealth.org/privacy)

> **Note:** CommonHealth is only available in the United States. The app is closed source, but the [developer SDK is open source](https://github.com/the-commons-project).

## Selection Criteria

These recommendations follow Privacy Guides' standard criteria with additional requirements:

### Minimum Requirements

- Must support automatic updates
- Must not store unencrypted data outside the device
- Must function offline
