# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Instagram

This guide covers essential security and privacy practices for Instagram users. Since Instagram is owned by Meta, also review the companion guides on [Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) for comprehensive protection across Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration**: Commercial platforms may not share your values and could remove content they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand local contexts, particularly if they don't operate in your language.

---

## Understanding Instagram's Policies

### How Instagram Uses Your Information

Before sharing data on any platform, understand how it will be used:

- Review [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/219) for a plain-language summary
- Read [Instagram's privacy policy](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy)
- Review the [terms of use](https://help.instagram.com/581066165581870/) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

**Tip**: Install the [Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see policy summaries for sites you visit.

### Government and Law Enforcement Access

Understand what Instagram may share with authorities:

- [Meta's law enforcement request policy](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Transparency report on government data requests](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [Content restrictions based on local law](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [All Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

## Creating a New Account

### Identity Considerations

**Using real vs. fake names:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for personal and professional/activist activities
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency so trusted contacts can verify your identity

### Setting Up Securely

**Use a fresh email address:**
- Create a new email on a [privacy-friendly service](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- Set it up while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)

**Avoid linking your phone number:**
- Instagram allows account creation with only an email address
- Phone numbers can easily identify you and may be accessible to law enforcement

**Skip "Find Friends" features:**
- Decline requests to access your contacts or connect other social media accounts
- Don't share employer or educational background during setup
- Connect only with people you know and trust

### Account Succession Planning

Instagram won't give others access to your account, but you can prepare for emergencies:

- Designate someone to [memorialize your account](https://help.instagram.com/231764660354188) if needed
- Verified family members can [request account removal](https://help.instagram.com/264154560391256#removing-the-account)
- Share login credentials with a trusted person via [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- For detained colleagues, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact)

### Data Portability

Test [downloading your Instagram data](https://help.instagram.com/181231772500920) before storing important content. Understand what format it provides and how to use it elsewhere.

## Protecting Your Account

### Recovery Information

- [Verify your recovery email and phone](https://help.instagram.com/583107688369069) are current and secure
- Change recovery information immediately if you lose access to either
- Secure your recovery email account—attackers may target it to gain access

### Strong Passwords

Use a [strong, unique password](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords) for Instagram. Anyone accessing your account gains access to information about you and your entire network.

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

[Set up 2FA on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/566810106808145) using these methods (in order of preference):

1. **Security key** (most secure)
2. **Authenticator app** (recommended)
3. **SMS** (least secure—avoid if possible, as mobile carriers can access these codes)

[Learn more about 2FA](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa).

### Backup Codes

[Get recovery codes](https://help.instagram.com/1006568999411025) and store them securely:
- Save in your password manager
- Print and keep in a locked safe
- Have these ready before you need them

### Advanced Protection

If Instagram identifies your account as vulnerable, you may be invited to enroll in [Advanced Protection](https://help.instagram.com/945221763907783). Consider enrolling promptly if offered—you may be locked out if you don't respond in time.

### Recognizing Phishing

- [Learn to identify phishing attempts](https://help.instagram.com/670309656726033)
- [Verify emails from Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/emails/emails_sent/) in your account settings
- Never click links in security emails—log in directly instead

## Using Instagram More Securely

**Browser vs. App:**
- Use a browser instead of the app when possible for browsing and basic posting
- The app is required for some features like Stories

**Anonymous usage:**
- Create accounts through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser) or VPN
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use an [anonymously-created email address](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Monitoring Account Security

### Check for Suspicious Access

Regularly review which devices have logged into your account. If you see unfamiliar activity:

1. Immediately change your password to a [new, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
3. Review the Password and Security section in Accounts Center

### Login Notifications

Enable alerts for logins from unfamiliar devices or locations to catch unauthorized access quickly.

### If Your Account Is Hacked

1. Follow [Instagram's recovery steps](https://help.instagram.com/149494825257596)
2. If unsuccessful, use [the Digital First Aid Kit troubleshooter](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

## Managing What You Share

### General Privacy Settings

- [Consider making your account private](https://help.instagram.com/517073653436611)
- Review [Instagram's privacy tools](https://help.instagram.com/811572406418223/)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**Never post:**
- Your home address or identifiable images of where you live
- Sensitive documents or identification

**Consider carefully before sharing:**
- Details about family members
- Information about sexual orientation or activities
- Email addresses (use separate accounts for sensitive vs. public use)

**Best practices:**
- Get consent before posting about others
- Establish guidelines with your network about what can be shared
- Remember that content can easily be copied and spread

### Cross-Posting and Connected Accounts

- [Turn off automatic sharing to Facebook](https://help.instagram.com/797182937605779/#sharing-your-posts-automatically-to-facebook)
- [Unlink from other social networks](https://help.instagram.com/536741816349927)
- Be cautious—you may be anonymous on one platform but exposed on another

### Location Privacy

- [Understand how location works on Instagram](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/guide/location/)
- Turn off location services: [iPhone/iPad](https://help.instagram.com/171821142968851) | [Android](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507)

### Photo and Video Safety

**Before posting, check for:**
- Background details revealing your location
- Identifiable information about yourself or others
- Faces of people who haven't consented

**Technical steps:**
- [Remove metadata](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files) before posting
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555) for personal photos

### Other Privacy Considerations

- **Likes**: [Always visible, even on private accounts](https://help.instagram.com/281388201973414)—use caution in sensitive contexts
- **Birthday**: Use a fake date but remember it for account recovery; Instagram doesn't share it publicly

## Controlling Who Sees Your Content

### Managing Your Audience

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://help.instagram.com/413012278753813) (they won't be notified)
- [Block accounts](https://help.instagram.com/426700567389543)
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555)
- [Remove content from search engines](https://help.instagram.com/147542625391305)

### Comments and Replies

- [Turn off comments](https://help.instagram.com/1766818986917552)
- [Remove comments](https://help.instagram.com/289098941190483)
- [Delete multiple comments at once](https://help.instagram.com/289376615404536) (app only)

### Connections

- [Unfollow people](https://help.instagram.com/286340048138725)
- [Deny follow requests](https://help.instagram.com/207917546007234)

Consider that your connections reveal information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in legal cases.

### Mentions and Tags

[Control who can mention and tag you](https://help.instagram.com/345505572238391). Tags could be used to make false accusations about your activities.

### Direct Messages

- [Choose who can message you](https://help.instagram.com/296884305019942/)
- [Block accounts](https://help.instagram.com/454180787965921)

### Advertising

Governments or police may purchase advertising data to target you:

- [De-select all ad topics](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/245100253430454)
- [Opt out of partner data for ads](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/2885653514995517)
- Review [Facebook advertising settings](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook/#manage-advertising) (shared with Instagram)

## Leaving No Trace

### Shared or Public Devices

- Avoid logging into Instagram on shared computers
- Never save passwords on public machines
- Delete browsing history after use
- Change passwords from your own device afterward

### Search History

[Clear your Instagram search history](https://help.instagram.com/354860134605952) regularly. If your account is compromised, this information could be used against you.
