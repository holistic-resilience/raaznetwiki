# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

We use social media platforms to connect with others and spread important messages. However, the companies managing these platforms often profit from our communications and contacts. They share information about us—including buying habits, social connections, location, and identifying details—with third parties, often without our full awareness.

When you use their services, you agree to terms that allow them to share or sell this information. Sometimes it's unclear just how public that data will become.

## Why This Matters

Social media platforms are actively monitored by governments and law enforcement agencies. Authorities collect publicly available data and gather metadata that we may not consider—such as when a photo was taken or who is connected to whom. They may also request private information from social media companies about individuals of interest, often including human rights defenders or activists.

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Private Group Communication

- **Small groups**: Move to [secure chat apps](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications) like [Signal](https://signal.org/) or [Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/)
- **Larger private groups**: Set up a [Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/) or [Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/) instance

### For Public Outreach

- **Microblogging**: Explore [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about) or [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) as alternatives to X. See [our blog post on leaving X](https://securityinabox.org/en/blog/x-odus) for more options
- **Blogging**: Create a blog on [Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/), [Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/), [Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html), or run your own website
- **Mailing lists and newsletters**: Use [Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists) or [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp

While Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and spreading messages, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure—the companies behind them have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you use these apps, read our guides on using [Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram) or [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) more securely.
