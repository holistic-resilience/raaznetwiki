# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Facebook

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Facebook account. Also see guides on [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other platforms belonging to Meta.

> **Important Consideration**: Think carefully about whether relying on commercial, corporate products is secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Facebook's Policies

### Learn How Facebook Uses Your Information

- Review a clarification of Facebook's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/182)
- [Learn about Facebook's privacy policy](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/explanation/)
- Review Facebook's [terms of service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms/preview) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

**Why this matters**: It is often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their terms of service and privacy policy helps you understand the risks.

> **Tip**: Install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see an overview of terms for each commercial service you use.

### Learn What Facebook Will Turn Over to Governments

- Review [Meta's policy on law enforcement requests](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- Check [Meta's transparency report on government requests for user data](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- See [Meta's transparency reports page](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/) for comprehensive information

**Why this matters**: Social media sites may provide your information—including information you tried to keep private—to governments or law enforcement if requested.

## Don't Use the Mobile App

**Recommendation**: Do not use the Facebook app on your mobile device. Connect using a browser instead.

**Why this matters**: An app that constantly gathers information can violate your privacy in many ways, even when you deny permissions for geolocation, microphone, etc. Accessing Facebook through a browser ensures Facebook isn't constantly connected to a device you carry everywhere.

## Create a New Account

You can [create a Facebook account](https://www.facebook.com/help/188157731232424) for free.

### Decide on Name and Identity Strategy

**Consider these factors**:
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network connection and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider using separate accounts for different campaigns and activities
- Keep personal and work accounts separate at minimum
- The key to safe social networking is trusting your connections—establish ways to validate identities without necessarily using real names

**Be aware**: Facebook's [name policy](https://www.facebook.com/help/1090831264320592) allows them to remove accounts using names not used in everyday life. If choosing an alternative name, select one that Facebook's algorithms will recognize as plausible.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a [privacy-friendly server](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- If protecting your identity, use [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address) when creating the email

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number

If possible, do not enter your phone number when setting up your account. Facebook allows account creation with only an email address.

**Why this matters**: Your phone number can easily be used to identify you. Consider whether providing it would increase your risk—could law enforcement or someone seeking to harass you use it to find information about you?

### Skip "Find Friends"

- Dismiss Facebook's requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- Select contacts carefully afterward
- Only connect to people you know and trust
- Avoid sharing employer or educational background, as Facebook may use this to share your profile unexpectedly

**Why this matters**: Social media uses contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous when you want to keep contacts hidden. Consider what might happen if law enforcement accessed these contact lists.

### Designate an Account Manager

- Facebook will not give others access to your account, but allows a designated person to close and ["memorialize" your account](https://www.facebook.com/help/275013292838654/)
- For emergency access, arrange to share login information using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- [Learn about removing accounts for medically incapacitated persons](https://www.facebook.com/help/480409628639043)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test downloading your data from Facebook. Start the download process, then review what data is provided, in what format, and how you can use it.

**Why this matters**: Before storing data on a large platform, understand what it takes to move your data out. Complete deletion from servers may take considerable time.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Recovery Information

- [Check your recovery email](https://www.facebook.com/help/162801153783275/)
- [Check your recovery phone number](https://www.facebook.com/help/124895950923762/)
- Change this information immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters**: Attackers may change recovery information to gain control later. Secure your recovery email and phone number, as an adversary could hack them to change your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who accesses your Facebook account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

- Use a security key, authenticator app, or security codes
- Avoid SMS or phone calls if possible—mobile companies have full access to these communications
- [Learn how 2FA works on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/148233965247823/)
- Complete the ["How to keep your account secure" checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)

**Learn more**: See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa).

### Save Recovery Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://www.facebook.com/help/148104135383285) for signing in when your usual 2FA method is unavailable
- Print codes and store them securely (like a locked safe)

### Consider Advanced Protection

[Learn about Advanced Protection](https://www.facebook.com/help/1052552578831700)—this feature helps particularly visible people adopt stronger security protections, including monitoring for potential hacking threats.

**Note**: Advanced Protection is only available for people selected by Facebook. If eligible, you'll see prompts to enroll.

### Watch for Phishing Attempts

- [Learn about suspicious emails and messages](https://www.facebook.com/help/225602007465207)
- [Check if Facebook emailed you recently](https://www.facebook.com/recent_emails/security)

**Why this matters**: Phishing messages try to trick you into giving account access. Never click links in security emails—log in directly to your account to verify messages.

### Create an Alternative Identity

If using Facebook without connecting to your real identity, create your account through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser) and [never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address). Create the associated email address using Tor or a VPN.

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Sessions and Security Events

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

**If you see suspicious activity**:
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Use a passphrase not used for any other accounts
3. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

Review security issues in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Enable Login Notifications

Get alerts when someone tries logging in from an unusual device or location.

## If You Think Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Perform a security checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/review/?review_id=573933453011661)
2. [Follow steps for securing a hacked account](https://www.facebook.com/hacked) or use the [step-by-step walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/help/1306725409382822)
3. If standard procedures don't work, check [the Digital First Aid Kit troubleshooter](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

### Review Connected Apps and Sites

- Avoid using Facebook to log into other sites—use unique passwords saved in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- Be careful when connecting accounts—you may be anonymous on one site but exposed on another
- [Manage privacy settings for connected apps and websites](https://www.facebook.com/help/218345114850283/)

**Why this matters**: Connected sites and apps can be exploited by hackers to access your accounts.

## Decide What Information to Share

- [Choose who you share content with](https://www.facebook.com/help/325807937506242#choose-who-you-share-content-with)
- Complete the [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264)
- [Set up regular Privacy Checkup reminders](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264#set-up-privacy-checkup-reminders)
- [Manage what others can see about you](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/dialog/how-to-clean-your-profile?helpref=privacycenter)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**Personally identifying information including**:
- Information revealing where you live (e.g., pictures of your house)
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Email address (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional considerations**:
- Even if you trust your network, information can easily be copied and spread
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared for safety
- Think about what you may reveal about friends that they wouldn't want shared

**Why this matters**: The more information you reveal, the easier it becomes to identify and monitor you. For example, "liking" a page opposing your government's position could make you a target for surveillance or persecution.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location services work on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509/)
- Turn off location services if using the mobile app
- [Don't add location to your posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509#adding-or-sharing-your-location)

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Never post images that include**:
- Sensitive locations or identifying backgrounds
- Information that could identify others without consent

**Photo management**:
- [Delete uploaded photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/208547132518386/)
- [Control who can see shared albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/410653545719324)
- [Delete albums with all photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/152153388188974)
- [Remove contributors from shared albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/589394671112683)
- [Remove metadata before posting](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

**Why this matters**: Photos can reveal information unintentionally, and cameras embed hidden data (metadata/EXIF tags) about location, date, time, and camera used.

### Manage "Likes" Visibility

- [Change "Who can see your social interactions alongside ads"](https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences/ad_settings) to "Only Me"
- [Learn how video interactions are seen by others](https://www.facebook.com/help/410629549531206)

**Why this matters**: In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when leaving reactions.

### Don't Share Your Birthday

You can register with a different birthday and change it later. Keep track of what you enter in case you need it to regain access.

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share to Select People

- Complete the ["Who can see what you share" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- Set everything under "Profile information" to "Only me"
- Consider "Limit past posts" to restrict visibility of older content
- [Check your activity log](https://www.facebook.com/settings/)
- [Change who can see apps you use](https://www.facebook.com/help/201750739897196)

### Manage Replies and Comments

- [Choose who can comment on your Page's posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/369765040737128)
- [Hide comments from posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/297845860255949)
- [Stop people from posting on your profile](https://www.facebook.com/help/115469971891543)
- [Control what visitors can post on your Page](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/356113237741414)

**Why this matters**: Replies have been used to build false claims about associations with certain people, and can be used for harassment.

### Manage Group Membership and Connections

- [Control who can add you as a friend](https://www.facebook.com/help/217125868312360)
- [Choose who can follow you](https://www.facebook.com/help/201148673283205)

- [Learn what information apps can access in groups](https://www.facebook.com/help/261149227954100)
- [Leave a group](https://www.facebook.com/help/172944012764072)
- [Remove or ban group members](https://www.facebook.com/help/211909018842184)

- [Turn off post comments in groups](https://www.facebook.com/help/987143528046827)
