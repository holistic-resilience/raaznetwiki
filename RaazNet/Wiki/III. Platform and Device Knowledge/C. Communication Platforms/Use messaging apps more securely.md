# Use Messaging Apps More Securely

Messaging apps are widely used for informal and real-time communications, enabling immediate exchange of text and voice messages, file sharing, voice and video calls, and group chats.

Despite their popularity, many messaging apps have privacy and security issues that can expose your communications to risks—messages could be tampered with or accessed by unauthorized third parties. It's essential to reflect on your needs before deciding whether to use a messaging app for specific information and which apps to use for sensitive details.

This guide provides questions to help you make informed decisions about real-time communication tools, along with recommendations for choosing and using apps safely.

> **Related Reading**: To assess communication sensitivity and choose appropriate channels, see the [guide on protecting online communication privacy](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/private-communication/).

## Assess Your Needs

Answer these questions to determine which messaging apps suit your communication requirements.

### Communication Features

**What do you need to exchange?**
While some apps only support text chat, most also enable voice messages, files, images, videos, emoji, stickers, and audio/video calls. Choose tools based on your communication needs.

**Do you need individual or group communication?**
For sensitive information with individuals, most messaging tools work well. For group communications, look for apps supporting group messaging and check maximum group sizes.

### Technical Requirements

**Can you and your contacts access the app on all devices and operating systems?**

- If you can install software, ensure you trust the app and provider before installation
- If you can't install apps or don't trust them, check if a web interface is available and use it through a [secure browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)

### Registration and Identity

**Does the app require registration? Are you willing to provide an email or phone number?**

If you need to hide your identity:
- Choose tools that don't require phone numbers or email addresses
- Prefer tools allowing email registration over phone numbers
- Create a separate email for registration
- Privilege decentralized tools when possible

> **Learn more**: [How to chat anonymously](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

### Encryption Requirements

**Do you need end-to-end encryption?**

For sensitive information (without self-hosting):
- Ensure messages are protected by default with end-to-end encryption, or that it can be enabled
- Verify group chats can also be encrypted
- Confirm voice/video calls support end-to-end encryption

**Can conversations disappear automatically?**

For sensitive exchanges, ensure the app allows automatic deletion on all receiving devices after a set period. This protects information if a device is lost, stolen, or seized.

### Evaluating App Trustworthiness

Consider these factors when assessing a messaging app:

| Factor | Key Questions |
|--------|---------------|
| **Mission** | Was it created for secure communications or commercial purposes? What's the business model? |
| **Data Storage** | What data is stored and for how long? Check privacy policies and terms of service for logging practices. |
| **Legal History** | Have they received government data requests? How did they respond? |
| **Jurisdiction** | Where are headquarters and servers located? Will they comply with your country's authorities? Do they respect human rights? |
| **Encryption Software** | What encryption does the app use? Is the software mature? |
| **Open Source** | Are both app and server based on free and open-source software? |
| **Security Audits** | Has the app undergone audits by reputable, independent third parties? Were servers also audited? When was the last audit? |

### Group Chat Considerations

- **Size**: How many participants do you need? Group limits vary by app.
- **Access Control**: Should participants register, or join without registration?
- **Moderation**: Do you need to control who can write to the group?
- **Purpose**: Announcements only, or full conversations with file sharing and meetings?
- **Security**: Does the app offer end-to-end encryption and auto-delete for groups?
- **Calls**: Do you need video call capabilities?
- **Organization**: Can you subdivide conversations by topic?

## Use Messaging Tools Safely

Follow these recommendations regardless of which app you choose.

### Register with a Separate Identity

- Avoid using your phone number to create accounts when possible
- [Learn how to choose a username](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/#choose-a-name)
- [Create and protect multiple online identities](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/)
- [Chat anonymously](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

> **Why this matters**: Your phone number is typically connected to your official identity. Keeping messaging apps separate from your usual contact information lets you control who can reach you directly.

### Enable End-to-End Encryption

- Use apps that protect conversations with end-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
- If encryption isn't enabled by default, activate it for sensitive conversations

> **Why this matters**: Anyone with server access can read unencrypted communications. End-to-end encryption prevents unauthorized access to sensitive information.

### Verify Contact Identities

Always verify contacts' identities through a second, safer channel—preferably face-to-face, or through encrypted email.

### Manage Message History

- Use apps with automatic message deletion for sensitive information
- Set messages to disappear after an appropriate time period
- Regularly delete chat history manually if automatic deletion isn't available

> **Why this matters**: Chat apps keep records of everything shared by default. Disappearing messages limit information stored on your phone.

### Handle Links and Files Carefully

- **Suspicious links**: Avoid opening unexpected links
- **Unexpected files**: Contact the sender through a different secure channel to confirm before opening
- **File sharing**: Consider uploading files to secure storage and sending download links instead

> **Why this matters**: Many malware and spyware infections occur through clicking malicious links or downloading files with malicious code.

## Use Group Chats Safely

### Control Group Access

- Assign admin rights only to [verified contacts](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat/#verify-your-contacts-identities)
- Know who you're inviting to join
- If removing someone doesn't work, create an entirely new group
- Double-check contact information before sending invitations
- Verify each person's identity through a different trusted channel

### Review Member Privileges

Check group settings to determine whether privileges should be admin-only or available to everyone:

- Writing and sending voice messages
- Adding or removing members
- Viewing member names and information

> **Why this matters**: Depending on assigned rights, you can treat a group as a peer chat room or an announcement channel for wider audiences.
