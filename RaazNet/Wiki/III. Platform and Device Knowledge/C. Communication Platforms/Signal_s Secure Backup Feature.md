# Signal's Secure Backup Feature

Losing your phone or deleting the Signal app used to mean saying goodbye to your important messages. Signal has announced a new feature called **Secure Backups** that changes this. This optional feature allows you to back up and recover your messages if you lose your phone or need to reinstall Signal. As always, backups are end-to-end encrypted, meaning the service operator cannot read your messages.

## Storage Options

### Free Tier
- 100MB of storage for text messages
- Last 45 days of messages and media
- Signal suggests this covers the needs of "even heavy Signal users"

### Paid Tier ($1.99/month)
- 100GB of storage
- Extended message and media retention

### Local Backup
- Password-protected backup stored directly on your device
- No cloud storage required

### Getting Started on Android
1. [Sign up for Signal Beta](https://support.signal.org/hc/en-us/articles/360007318471-Signal-Beta) to access the feature now
2. Open Signal and tap the circle icon at the top left to access settings
3. Navigate to **Settings > Backups**
4. Choose between Secure Backup (cloud) or on-device backup

### Protect Your Recovery Key

> **Important:** Signal does not store a copy of your backup encryption keys—only you have them.

Signal provides a **recovery key** to unlock your backup archive. Store this key securely:
- Use a [password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)
- Keep a secure physical copy in a safe location
- **Warning:** If you lose this key, you cannot recover your backup
