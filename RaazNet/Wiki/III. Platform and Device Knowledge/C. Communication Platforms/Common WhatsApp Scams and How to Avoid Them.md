# Common WhatsApp Scams and How to Avoid Them

WhatsApp messenger, with over two billion users worldwide, is considered a potential tool for many common scams. Attackers are learning new methods every day and updating their techniques to deceive users.

## Why is WhatsApp a target for scammers?

- **Easy access:** Anyone who knows your phone number can send you a message
- **User diversity:** People of different ages and awareness levels use this messenger
- **Data leaks:** In December 2022, information of over 500 million WhatsApp accounts was put for sale on the dark web

> **Important Note:** Scammers often do not look for specific users and use their strategies randomly.

---

## Common Types of Scams

### 1. Verification Code Scam

**Execution Method:**

1. An SMS containing a verification code (seemingly from WhatsApp) is sent to you
2. Then you receive a message on WhatsApp from an unknown contact
3. The scammer claims the code was sent to your number by mistake and needs it urgently

**Goal:** Access to your WhatsApp account

---

### 2. Impersonation Scam

**Execution Method:**

- The scammer pretends to be your child or a family member
- Claims to have a new phone number
- Requests money urgently

**Main Target:** Parents and elderly people

---

### 3. Fake Surveys and Services

**Common Types:**

- **Fake Bank Messages:** Warnings about fraud and requests to verify personal information
- **Fake Postal SMS:** Requests to confirm package delivery details (DHL, UPS)
- **Fake Campaigns:** Imitating special offers on occasions like Black Friday

**Signs of Detection:** Poor grammar, strange links

---

### 4. Charity Scams

**Execution Method:**

- Using emotional photos and videos
- Creating fake charity websites
- Claiming to help victims of natural disasters or diseases
- Misusing the names of reputable charitable institutions

**Prevention:** Before donating, contact the charity directly and verify the authenticity of the request.

---

### 5. Catfishing (Romance Scams)

**Execution Method:**

1. Meeting on dating sites
2. Exchanging phone numbers
3. Creating conditions where meeting in person is not possible (another city or country, military service)
4. Telling a pitiful story and asking for financial help

---

## Ways to Protect Yourself

### Security Settings

- ✅ Enable two-step verification
- ✅ Keep the operating system and apps updated
- ✅ Only use official stores (Google Play, App Store) for updates
- ✅ Use reputable security solutions for mobile

### Safe Behaviors

- ❌ Do not share the verification code with anyone
- ❌ Do not click on random links
- ❌ Do not open unknown attachments
- ❌ Do not send personal information on messengers

### Identity Verification

- 📞 Before transferring money, verify the authenticity of the request via phone call
- 📞 If you receive a suspicious message from friends, verify via another channel

> **Remember:** Banks will never message you on WhatsApp or ask for personal information.
