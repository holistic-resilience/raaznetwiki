# Protect Your Data When Using TikTok

Read this guide to increase your privacy and security when sharing and viewing videos or streaming content on TikTok.

> **Important Consideration**: Before relying on commercial, corporate products, assess whether they can adequately protect your information. Commercial corporations may not share your values and might remove or filter content they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

---

## Understand TikTok's Policies

### How TikTok Uses Your Information

Before sharing information on any platform, understand what they do with your data:

- Review TikTok's terms of service summary on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/1448)
- Read [TikTok's privacy policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/eea/privacy-policy/en) (select your region using the button above the title)
- Review their [Community Principles](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/community-principles) and [Community Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en)

**Why this matters**: It's often unclear what service providers do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to profile you? Is it sold to companies that may share it without your consent? You can install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see terms summaries for services you use.

### What TikTok Shares with Authorities

Understand what information TikTok may provide to governments or law enforcement:

- [TikTok's law enforcement guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/global/law-enforcement/en)
- [Government removal requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/government-removal-requests-2024-1)
- [Information requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/es/information-requests-2024-1)
- [TikTok's transparency center](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/)

**Why this matters**: Social media sites may provide your information—including private data—to governments or law enforcement when requested. Understanding these conditions helps you make informed decisions about what to share.

## Create a New Account Securely

TikTok accounts are free. While TikTok suggests using their app, [using a web browser on a computer is more secure](#use-tiktok-more-securely).

**To create an account via web browser:**
1. Go to the [TikTok home page](https://www.tiktok.com/)
2. Click **Login**, then **Sign up**
3. Choose **Use phone or email**
4. Select **Sign up with email** to avoid using a phone number

If using the app, see [TikTok's account creation guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/getting-started/creating-an-account/creating-an-account).

### Choose Your Identity Carefully

**Consider using a pseudonym:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use separate accounts for different campaigns, activities, or to separate personal and work content
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency so trusted contacts can verify your identity

**Why this matters**: Many people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with less risk to their safety or liberty.

### Use a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Choose from [privacy-friendly email providers](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Avoid Using Your Phone Number

When setting up your account, skip entering your phone number. TikTok allows account creation with only an email address.

**Why this matters**: Phone numbers can easily identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could use your number to find your activities, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Don't Grant Contact Access

- **Skip** or **dismiss** requests to access contacts, import contacts, connect with Facebook friends, or find connections
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share employer or educational background in your bio—TikTok may use this to share your profile unexpectedly

**Why this matters**: Social media use your contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you need to keep contacts hidden. Law enforcement might use these lists to build cases, or the platform might reveal your associations publicly.

### Plan for Account Emergencies

- TikTok doesn't accept requests from family members to delete or memorialize deceased users' accounts
- Share login information with a trusted person using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication) for emergencies
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance. See also the [Digital First Aid Kit](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test downloading your data from TikTok. Understand what format it's in and how you can use it elsewhere before committing to the platform.

## Protect Your Account

Review [TikTok's account security recommendations](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety).

### Verify Recovery Options

- [Check your recovery email and phone number](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/email-and-phone-number)
- Update immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters**: Attackers may change recovery information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising them could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your TikTok account gains access to information about you and your connections.

- Follow [best practices for creating strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)
- If prompted to create a passkey, read [recommendations on using passkeys](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords/#consider-how-and-when-to-use-passkeys)

### Enable Two-Step Verification

- Use an **authenticator app** and **email** for verification
- **Avoid SMS** if possible—mobile carriers have access to these messages, and SMS is easier to compromise
- See [TikTok's 2-step verification guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety)

Learn more in our [two-factor authentication guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa).

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- Learn to identify [phishing messages claiming to be from TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/avoid-fraudulent-message-attacks-on-tiktok)
- For business accounts, see [how to avoid phishing attempts](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/how-to-avoid-phishing-attempts)

**If you receive a suspicious security email or text**: Don't click any links or provide your password. Instead, log in directly to your account and check your settings.

## Use TikTok More Securely

- **Prefer browsers over the app** for browsing profiles, publishing posts, and chatting
- **For anonymous use**:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use an [email created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)
  - [Don't add your phone number](#avoid-using-your-phone-number)

## Monitor for Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Don't reuse this password elsewhere
3. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

### If Your Account Has Been Hacked

- [Reset your password and remove suspicious devices](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/my-account-has-been-hacked)
- If locked out, try [recovering via friend verification](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety#3)

## Control Your Information Sharing

### Set Account Privacy

- [Make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Change video visibility settings for specific audiences](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/video-visibility)

### Avoid Sharing Sensitive Information

**Never share:**
- Information revealing where you live (videos shot in or near your home)
- Personally identifying information

**Consider carefully before sharing:**
- Family member details
- Sexual orientation or activities
- Email addresses (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional precautions:**
- Remember that trusted contacts can easily copy and spread your information
- Agree with your network on what can be shared for safety
- Be sensitive about revealing information about friends—ask them to reciprocate

**Why this matters**: The more you reveal online, the easier it becomes to identify and monitor you. In some countries, "liking" posts opposing government positions has led to targeting and persecution.

### Disable Location Sharing

- [Understand how location works on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok)
- [Turn off location services](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#3)
- [Delete stored location data](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#4)

### Share Videos Safely

**Before posting, consider what's visible:**
- Background details that could identify locations
- People who haven't consented to being filmed
- Sensitive information visible in the frame

**Best practices:**
- Seek consent before posting about others
- Set guidelines with colleagues about public sharing
- [Remove metadata from videos shot outside TikTok](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)
- For private-only content, [make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)

### Hide Your Likes

Your liked videos are hidden by default, but verify this setting:
- [Ensure liked videos are visible only to you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/exploring-videos/liking#4)

**Why this matters**: In some countries, "likes" have been used as evidence in legal cases.

## Control Who Sees Your Content

### Manage Followers

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking them](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/blocking-the-users)
- [Make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)

### Control Comments

- [Choose who can comment on your videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#4) (select "No one" to disable all comments)
- [Turn off comments for specific videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#5)
- [Filter comments requiring approval](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#3)
- [Delete comments](https://support.tiktok.org/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#6)

**Why this matters**: Replies have been used to falsely claim associations between human rights defenders and others. Disabling comments can reduce harassment and stress.

### Review Your Connections

- [Unfollow people](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/following-and-unfollowing)
- [Remove followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers)

**Why this matters**: Your connections reveal information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in court cases.

### Manage Mentions and Hashtags

- [Control who can mention you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/messaging-and-notifications/mentions-on-tiktok#3): Everyone, People you follow, Friends, or No one
- [Report inappropriate hashtags](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/report-a-problem/report-a-hashtag)

### Limit Direct Messages

- [Learn about DM controls](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/controlling-your-dms)
- [Choose who can message you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message)
- [Filter, hide, report messages, or block senders](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message#3)

### Disable Personalized Advertising

- [Understand TikTok's ad personalization](https://www.tiktok.com/privacy/ads-and-your-data/)
- [Turn off personalized ads](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/personalized-ads-and-data/personalization-and-data)

**Why this matters**: Governments or police may purchase advertising data to target you with disinformation or locate you.

## Leave No Trace

### On Shared or Public Devices

- Avoid logging into TikTok on shared devices (internet cafes, others' devices)
- Never save passwords on public machines
- Delete browsing history after use
- Change passwords for any accessed accounts as soon as possible using your own device

### Clear Your History

- [Turn off post view history](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/tiktok-post-view-history)
- [Turn off profile view history](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/tiktok-profile-visit-history)
- [Delete watch history](https://www
