# TikTok Security Considerations for Newsrooms

By the end of 2021, TikTok reached one billion monthly users, becoming one of the most popular social media platforms globally. In the United States, the platform was one of the few that significantly increased its share of users who regularly use the platform to get their news.

Whether you're a journalist new to the platform or part of a long-time TikToking newsroom, understanding how to mitigate the potential dangers of the platform is key. This guide focuses on the most important security considerations—like account hijacking, data breaches and privacy—and provides recommendations for navigating the platform securely.

## Understanding Concerns About the Platform

The success of TikTok isn't random. As with many other social media platforms, the algorithm that personalizes your experience is powered by data collection, analysis of interests and interactions, and profiling of user behavior. In late 2022, TikTok admitted they targeted journalists using the app to investigate their sources. All of this raises concerns among U.S. lawmakers, as the company has been vague and evasive in explaining what specific information is collected about users and for what purposes.

### Data Access and Storage Concerns

TikTok is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance. While the platform has stated that none of its data centers are subject to Chinese law and that content isn't removed based on sensitivities related to China, leaked internal information revealed in 2022 that U.S. user data has been repeatedly accessed from China. The company has confirmed that personal data on TikTok users who sign up to make money through the app is stored in China.

While TikTok began negotiating a security deal with the U.S. government in 2022, some former national security officials and other experts believe users' personal data would still be exposed to hacking and espionage.

### App Behavior Concerns

- **Clipboard Access**: A 2020 Apple update revealed that the TikTok app was constantly accessing the user's clipboard content on iOS
- **In-App Browser Monitoring**: In 2022, research discovered TikTok's in-app browser includes code that can monitor user keystrokes on websites

> **Recommendation**: Use a trusted browser installed on your device (Chrome, Firefox, or Brave) instead of the in-app browser.

### Content Creation Awareness

When creating content for TikTok, be mindful of sharing information that you don't want public or that could put you or others at risk, including:
- Information about your daily routine
- Personal data about yourself or your loved ones
- Source information

## Securing Your TikTok Account

### Use a Unique and Strong Password

Many people use short, predictable passwords, which introduces unnecessary risk to account safety. Regardless of whether it's a personal or institutional account, it must be protected by a unique and strong password.

**Recommendations:**
- Use passphrases (a sequence of random words or other types of text)—they are harder to guess and easier to remember
- Use a password manager to generate and securely store your passphrases while having access to them from multiple devices

**To change your password:**
- **Android**: Open TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password → Enter code → Enter new password

TikTok will send you a 6-digit code to validate your identity before you can set your new password.

### Adding Two-Step Verification

While TikTok doesn't support two-factor authentication methods like physical security keys or authentication apps, activating two-step verification adds an extra layer of security. Once configured, you will be prompted for a 6-digit one-time code at login, right after validating your password.

**To enable two-step verification:**
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification → Choose phone, email, or additional password (must select 2) → Select "Turn on"

You must select at least two of the available methods (SMS, email, password) and click "Turn on." Enter your password and click next to confirm.

### Law Enforcement Data Requests

Even if your TikTok account is secured with a strong, unique password and two-step verification, your data is still available to the company.

**Important considerations:**
- Direct messages are **not** end-to-end encrypted
- TikTok's law enforcement guidelines specify that it can disclose user data to government agencies, including direct message content

> **Recommendation**: If you need to discuss sensitive topics that could put you or others at risk, don't use TikTok. Move the conversation to apps that use end-to-end encryption, like Signal or WhatsApp.

## Privacy Settings to Consider

Account security is essential, but it's also important to weigh the available privacy options with how you want to use the app. A newsroom account may focus on maximizing reach, while a freelance journalist's account may want more control over interactions.

### App Permissions

Many apps require multiple permissions to access information such as location, storage, and contacts. When you launch TikTok for the first time, it will ask for permission to send notifications and access your contacts.

If you don't feel comfortable letting TikTok access your contact list to recommend accounts, you can deny or remove this permission:
- **Android** (may vary by version): Settings → Privacy → Permission manager
- **iOS**: Settings app → Apps → TikTok

### Public vs. Private Accounts

**Business Accounts:**
- Provide access to advanced statistics, post scheduler, and auto-messaging
- Must be public
- **To switch**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Switch to Business Account

**Private Accounts:**
- Not viewable by other TikTok users
- **To enable**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Private account (toggle on)

### Controlling Interactions

Access privacy options at: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy

**Available settings include:**

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Activity status** | Shows when you are active or were last active. Consider disabling if you don't want others to see when you're using the app. |
| **Suggest your account to others** | Controls how TikTok recommends your account. Even if disabled, TikTok will still suggest your account to people you follow. |
| **Comments** | Set who can comment on your videos and stories; filter comments containing specific keywords, spam, or offensive content. |
| **Mentions and tags** | Control who can mention or tag you in videos, descriptions, stickers, comments, and stories. |
| **Direct messages** | Decide who can message you; set read receipts; filter messages from suspicious accounts. |
| **Following list and liked videos** | Set visibility of your following list and liked videos. |
| **Downloads, Duets, and Stitch** | Control whether others can download your videos or use them in their posts. Disabling may reduce reach and engagement. |

### Account Deactivation and Deletion

You can temporarily or permanently deactivate your TikTok account so that no one can see it or its content.

**To deactivate or delete:**
- TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account

> **Note**: If you permanently delete your account, you'll have a 30-day waiting period during which you can reverse the request and fully restore your account.
