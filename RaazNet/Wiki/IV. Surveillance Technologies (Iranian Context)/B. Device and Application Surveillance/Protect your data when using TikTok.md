# Protect Your Data When Using TikTok

*Updated 3 April 2025*

Read this guide to increase your privacy and security when sharing and viewing videos or when streaming content on TikTok.

> **Important Consideration**: Before relying on commercial, corporate products, assess whether they are secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About TikTok's Policies

### Learn How TikTok Uses Your Information

Before using any platform, understand what happens to your data:

- Review TikTok's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/1448)
- Read [TikTok's privacy policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/eea/privacy-policy/en) (select your region using the button above the title)
- Review their [Community Principles](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/community-principles) and [Community Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en)

**Why this matters**: It is often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Your data may be combined with other information to infer things about you, or sold to other companies. You can also install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see an overview of terms for each service you use.

### Learn What TikTok Will Turn Over to Governments

Understanding TikTok's relationship with authorities helps you assess your risk:

- [TikTok's law enforcement guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/global/law-enforcement/en)
- [Government removal requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/government-removal-requests-2024-1)
- [Information requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/es/information-requests-2024-1)
- [TikTok's transparency center](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/)

**Why this matters**: Social media sites may provide your information—including data you intended to keep private—to governments or law enforcement when requested.

## Create a New Account

You can create a TikTok account for free. While TikTok suggests using their app, **using a web browser on a computer is more secure**.

**To create an account via web browser:**
1. Go to the [TikTok home page](https://www.tiktok.com/)
2. Click the Login button
3. Click Sign up at the bottom of the form
4. Choose "Use phone or email"
5. Select "Sign up with email" to avoid using a phone number

If you must use the app, see [TikTok's account creation guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/getting-started/creating-an-account/creating-an-account).

### Decide Whether to Use a Real or Fake Name

**Key considerations:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider using separate accounts for different campaigns, activities, or to separate personal and work content
- If using pseudonyms, use them consistently so trusted contacts can verify your identity

**Why this matters**: Some people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with others while protecting their free speech, safety, or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a privacy-friendly server while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Find suggested privacy-friendly mail services in [the communication tools section](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number with Your Account

When setting up a new account, avoid entering your phone number. TikTok allows account creation with only an email address, which is harder to associate with your identity.

**Why this matters**: Your phone number can easily be used to identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could request information from service providers using your phone number, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Don't Allow TikTok to Access Your Contacts

- Skip or dismiss requests to "allow access" to contacts, import contacts, connect with Facebook friends, or find more connections
- Select contacts carefully afterward
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share your employer or educational background in your bio

**Why this matters**: Social media platforms use your contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you want to keep your contacts hidden—law enforcement might use these lists to build cases, or the platform might reveal your associations publicly.

### Designate Someone to Manage Your Account in Emergencies

- TikTok does not accept requests from family members to delete or memorialize deceased users' accounts
- If someone may need emergency access to your account, share login information via [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know has been arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- See [Digital First Aid Kit: Someone I Know Has Been Arrested](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test how to download your data from TikTok. Start the download process and assess what data is provided, in what format, and how you can use it.

**Why this matters**: Before storing data on any commercial platform, understand what it takes to move your data out. Complete deletion from large platforms may take considerable time.

## Protect Your Account

Read [TikTok's recommendations on keeping your account secure](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety).

### Check Your Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Verify your recovery email and phone number](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/email-and-phone-number)
- Change this information immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters**: Recovery methods help you regain account access, but attackers may change them to take control later. Secure your recovery email and phone number, as compromising either could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your TikTok account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See [how to create and maintain strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)
- Read [recommendations on passkeys](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords/#consider-how-and-when-to-use-passkeys) if TikTok prompts you to create one

### Set Up 2-Step Verification (2SV)

- Use an authenticator app and your email for two-step verification
- Avoid SMS if possible—your mobile provider has access to these communications and they're easier to compromise
- Learn how to enable 2SV in [TikTok's account safety support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety)

See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for more details.

### Be Careful with Messages Claiming to Be from TikTok

- Learn [what to do if you receive a potential phishing message](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/avoid-fraudulent-message-attacks-on-tiktok)
- [Avoid phishing attempts for TikTok Business accounts](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/how-to-avoid-phishing-attempts)

**Why this matters**: Phishing messages try to trick you into giving others access to your account. If you receive a security email or text, don't click links or provide your password. Instead, log in directly to your account and verify the message's legitimacy.

## Use TikTok More Securely

- TikTok requires the app for many activities, but for browsing profiles, publishing posts, and chatting, consider using a browser instead
- To use TikTok without connecting to your real identity:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use an [email address created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)
  - [Avoid adding your phone number](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/tiktok/#dont-associate-your-phone-number-with-your-account)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Account Activity

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) not used elsewhere
2. Save this in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

**Why this matters**: Adversaries may log into your account from their devices. You can often detect this in the Security & permissions section of your account settings.

## If You Think Your Account Has Been Hacked

- Reset your password and remove suspicious devices, [as TikTok suggests](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/my-account-has-been-hacked)
- If you can't log in or reset your password, try [recovering your account through friend verification](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety#3)

## Decide What Information to Share

- Consider [making your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- Restrict post visibility individually by [changing video settings for specific audiences](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/video-visibility)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**Never share:**
- Personally identifying information
- Content revealing where you live (videos shot in or near your home)

**Consider carefully before sharing:**
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional precautions:**
- Remember that contacts can easily copy and spread your information beyond your intended audience
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared for safety
- Be sensitive about what you reveal about friends, and ask them to reciprocate

**Why this matters**: The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. "Likes" on posts opposing government positions, for example, could lead to targeting for surveillance or persecution. Family members of activists abroad may be targeted because of publicly accessible online content.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location works on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok)
- [Turn location services off](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#3)
- [Delete data TikTok received from location services](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#4)

**Why this matters**: Stopping accounts from storing location information protects against location tracking. Turning off location services also improves battery life.

### Share Videos More Safely

**Before posting videos, consider what's visible:**
- Background details that could identify locations
- Other people who may not want to be identified
- Sensitive information visible on screens or documents

**Best practices:**
- If posting only private videos, [make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Remove metadata before posting videos shot outside TikTok](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

**Why this matters**: Videos can unintentionally reveal information through backgrounds. Many cameras embed hidden metadata (EXIF tags) about location, date, time, and camera used. Social media may publish this when you upload.

### Change Who Can See Your "Likes"

Liked videos are hidden by default, but this can change. [Ensure your liked videos are visible only to you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/exploring-videos/liking#4).

**Why this matters**: "Likes" and reactions are sometimes shared in unclear ways. In some countries, even seemingly harmless interactions have been used in legal cases.

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share to Select People

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking them](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/blocking-the-users)
- Consider [making your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)

### Manage Who Can Reply to Your Posts

- [Choose who can comment on your videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#4) — select "No one" to disable comments on all videos
- [Turn comments off for specific videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#5)
- [Filter comments for all videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#3) — comments stay hidden until you approve them
- [Delete comments](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#6)
