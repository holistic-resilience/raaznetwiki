# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Instagram

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Instagram account, protect your privacy, and handle abuse on the platform.

> **Note:** Also see guides on [Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

Consider whether relying on commercial, corporate products is secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that they believe violates their policies. They may also have difficulty understanding your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Instagram's Policies

### Learn How Instagram Uses Your Information

- Review Instagram's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/219)
- [Learn about Instagram's privacy policy](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy)
- Review Instagram's [terms of use](https://help.instagram.com/581066165581870/) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

> **Why this matters:** It is often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to guess things about you? Is it sold to other companies? Read their terms of use and privacy policy to find out. You can also install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see an overview of terms for each service you use.

### Learn What Instagram Will Turn Over to Governments

- See [Meta's policy on law enforcement requests](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- Review [Meta's transparency report on government requests for user data](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- Check [Meta's transparency report on content restrictions based on local law](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- Explore [Meta's full transparency reports page](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

> **Why this matters:** Social media sites may give your information to governments or law enforcement if requested. This includes information you were trying to keep private.

## Create a New Account

You can [create an Instagram account](https://help.instagram.com/155940534568753) for free.

### Decide Whether to Use a Real or Fake Name

- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider using separate accounts for different campaigns and activities
- Keep personal and work accounts separate at minimum
- If using a fake name, use it consistently so trusted contacts can verify your identity

> **Why this matters:** Some people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with others with less risk to their safety or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a privacy-friendly server while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- See [suggested privacy-friendly mail services](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number with Your Account

- When setting up a new account, do not enter your phone number if possible
- Instagram allows account setup with only an email address

> **Why this matters:** Your phone number can be easily used to identify you. Local law enforcement could request information from service providers using your phone number, or someone seeking to harass you might use it to locate you.

### Skip "Find Friends"

- Dismiss Instagram's requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect with other social media accounts
- Only connect to people you know and trust
- Do not share your employer or educational background, as this information may be used to share your profile unexpectedly

> **Why this matters:** Social media uses your contact lists to recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you want to keep your contacts hidden. Law enforcement might use these contact lists to build a case against you.

### Designate Someone to Manage Your Account in Emergencies

- Instagram will not give others access to your account, but can ["memorialize" your account](https://help.instagram.com/231764660354188) with permission
- Verified immediate family members can [request removal of a deceased user's account](https://help.instagram.com/264154560391256#removing-the-account)
- For emergency access, arrange to share login information using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know has been arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance

### Know How to Move Your Data Out

- Test how to download your data from Instagram
- Assess what data is provided, in what format, and how you can use it

> **Why this matters:** Before storing data on a large commercial platform, understand what it takes to move your data out. Complete deletion from servers may take time.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Your Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Check your recovery email and phone number](https://help.instagram.com/583107688369069)
- Change this information immediately if you lose access to your email or phone
- [Read more on keeping your Instagram account secure](https://help.instagram.com/369001149843369/)

> **Why this matters:** Ensure an attacker has not changed your recovery information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone number, as an adversary could hack them to change your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who gets into your Instagram account will gain access to information about you and everyone you're connected to.

- See the guide on [how to create and maintain strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

- Use a security key, authenticator app, or security codes for 2FA
- **Avoid SMS or phone calls** — your mobile phone company has full access to these communications and they are easier to hack
- [Learn how two-factor authentication works on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/566810106808145)
- See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for more information

### Download and Save Backup Codes

- [Get recovery codes for Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/1006568999411025)
- Print these codes and keep them somewhere secure, like a locked safe

> **Why this matters:** Having verification codes safely stored gives you another way to access your account if you lose your 2FA method.

### Consider Enrolling in Advanced Protection

- If Instagram identifies your account as vulnerable, you may be invited to enroll in the [Advanced Protection program](https://help.instagram.com/945221763907783)
- Consider enrollment prompts promptly, as you may be locked out if you don't enroll in time

### Be Careful with Emails Claiming to Be from Instagram

- [Learn how to recognize phishing emails and messages](https://help.instagram.com/670309656726033)
- [Verify emails by checking your account settings](https://www.instagram.com/emails/emails_sent/)

> **Why this matters:** Phishing messages try to trick you into giving someone else access to your account. Never click links in security emails — log into your account directly to verify.

## Use Instagram More Securely

- Consider using a browser instead of the app for browsing profiles, publishing posts, and chatting
- To use Instagram anonymously:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use [an email address created using Tor or a VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Account Activity

- Check which devices have recently logged in to your account
- Does every login look familiar?
- If you see suspicious activity, immediately change your password to [a new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) and save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

> **Why this matters:** Adversaries may find ways to log into your account from their devices. You can review security issues in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Get Notified About Logins

- Enable alerts for login attempts from unusual devices or locations

## Decide What Information to Share

- [Consider making your account private](https://help.instagram.com/517073653436611)
- [Learn about privacy tools on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/811572406418223/)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

- Personally identifying information
- Information revealing where you live (e.g., pictures of your house)
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Best practices:**
- Seek consent before posting about others
- Work with colleagues to set guidelines for what you will and won't share publicly
- Think about what you may be revealing about friends that they may not want shared

> **Why this matters:** The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. In some countries, sharing or "liking" posts opposing government positions has led to targeting for surveillance or persecution.

### Check Automatic Cross-Posting

- [Consider turning off automatic sharing to Facebook](https://help.instagram.com/797182937605779/#sharing-your-posts-automatically-to-facebook)
- [Unlink your Instagram account from other social networks](https://help.instagram.com/536741816349927)

> **Why this matters:** You may be anonymous on one site but exposed on another. When other sites have access, hackers can also use them to access your accounts.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location works across Facebook and Instagram](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/guide/location/)
- [Turn location off on iPhone or iPad](https://help.instagram.com/171821142968851)
- For Android, see ["Stop an app from using your device's location"](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507)

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

- Consider what is visible in photos before posting
- Never post images that could identify sensitive locations or people at risk
- If posting private pictures, [make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555)
- [Remove metadata before posting photos and videos](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

> **Why this matters:** Photos and videos can reveal information unintentionally through background details or embedded metadata (location, date, time, camera information).

### Be Aware of "Likes" Visibility

- [Your likes are always visible, even if your account is private](https://help.instagram.com/281388201973414)

> **Why this matters:** In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when leaving reactions depending on your legal situation.

### Don't Share Your Real Birthday

- You can register with a different birthday and change it later
- Keep track of what you enter in case you need it for account recovery
- Instagram does not share your birthday publicly — it's used for age verification

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share to Select People

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://help.instagram.com/413012278753813) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking them](https://help.instagram.com/426700567389543)
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555)
- [Remove public photos from search engine results](https://help.instagram.com/147542625391305)
- Note: If you share private posts to other platforms, [anyone with that link can see it](https://help.instagram.com/442837725762581)

### Manage Who Can Reply

- [Turn off comments](https://help.instagram.com/1766818986917552)
- [Remove comments on your posts](https://help.instagram.com/289098941190483)
- [Delete multiple comments at once](https://help.instagram.com/289376615404536) (app only)

> **Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims of association with people you don't actually know. Disabling comments can reduce harassment and stress.

### Think About Who You Connect With

- [Unfollow people](https://help.instagram.com/286340048138725)
- [Deny follow requests](https://help.instagram.com/207917546007234)

> **Why this matters:** Your connections reveal something about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used in court to make cases against people.

### Control Mentions and Tags

- [Control mentions and tags](https://help.instagram.com/345505572238391)

> **Why this matters:** Tags could be used to make false accusations about your activities.

### Limit Who Can Contact You

- [Choose who can send direct messages and add you to group conversations](https://help.instagram.com/296884305019942/)
- [Block an account](https://help.instagram.com/454180787965921)

### Manage Advertising

- [De-select all ad topics](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/245100253430454)
- [Tell Instagram not to use partner data for ads](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/2885653514995517)
- Check [managing advertising on Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook/#manage-advertising)

> **Why this matters:** Governments or police may buy advertising data to target you with disinformation or locate you.

## Leave No Trace

### Precautions on Public or Shared Devices

- Avoid logging into Instagram from shared devices (internet cafes, others' devices)
- Never save passwords and delete browsing history on public machines
- Change passwords of any accounts accessed from shared devices as soon as possible

### Clear Your Search History

- [Clear your search history](https://help.instagram.com/354860134605952)

> **Why this matters:** Social media platforms track your searches. If your account is compromised or device seized, this information could be used against you.
