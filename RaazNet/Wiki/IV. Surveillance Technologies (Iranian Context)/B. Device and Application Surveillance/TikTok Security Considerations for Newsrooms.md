# TikTok Security Considerations for Newsrooms

By the end of 2021, TikTok reached one billion monthly users, becoming one of the most popular social media platforms globally. In the United States, the platform was one of the few that significantly increased its share of users who said they regularly use the platform to get their news.

Whether you're a journalist new to the platform or part of a long-time TikToking newsroom, understanding how to mitigate the potential dangers of the platform is key. This guide focuses on the most important security considerations—like account hijacking, data breaches, and privacy—and provides recommendations for navigating the platform securely.

## Understanding Concerns About the Platform

The success of TikTok isn't random. As with many other social media platforms, the algorithm that personalizes your experience is powered by data collection, analysis of interests and interactions, and profiling of user behavior. In late 2022, TikTok admitted they targeted journalists using the app to investigate their sources. All of this raises concerns among U.S. lawmakers, as the company has in the past been vague and evasive in explaining what specific information is collected about users and for what purposes.

### Data Access and Storage Concerns

TikTok is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance, but the platform has stated that none of its data centers are subject to Chinese law and that the platform doesn't remove content based on sensitivities related to China. However, leaked internal information revealed in 2022 that U.S. user data has been repeatedly accessed from China. In a letter to the U.S. Senate, the company confirmed personal data on TikTok users who sign up to make money through the app is stored in China.

While TikTok began negotiating a security deal with the U.S. government in 2022, some former national security officials and other experts believe users' personal data would still be exposed to hacking and espionage.

### Additional Technical Concerns

- **Clipboard Access**: A 2020 Apple update revealed that the TikTok app was constantly accessing the user's clipboard content on iOS
- **In-App Browser Monitoring**: In 2022, research discovered its in-app browser includes code that can be used to monitor user keystrokes on websites

**Recommendation**: Use a trusted browser installed on your device like Chrome, Firefox, or Brave instead of the in-app browser.

When creating content for TikTok, be mindful of sharing information that you don't want public or that could put you or others at risk. This includes information about your daily routine, personal data about yourself or your loved ones, or source information.

## Securing Your TikTok Account

### Use a Unique and Strong Password

We know from publicly-available data breaches that many people use short, predictable passwords, and this introduces unnecessary risk to account safety. Regardless of whether it's a personal or institutional account, it must be protected by a unique and strong password.

**Recommendations:**
- Use passphrases (a sequence of random words or other types of text), as they are harder to guess and easier to remember
- Use a password manager to generate and securely store your passphrases while having access to them from multiple devices

**To change your password:**
- **Android**: Open TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password → Enter code → Enter new password

TikTok will send you a 6-digit code to enter in the app to validate your identity. After that, log your new password.

### Adding Two-Step Verification to Your Account

While TikTok doesn't support two-factor authentication methods like physical security keys or authentication apps, we still recommend activating two-step verification on your TikTok account, which will add an extra layer of security. Once configured, you will be prompted for a 6-digit one-time code at login, right after validating your password.

**To enable two-step verification:**
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification → Choose phone, email, or additional password (must select 2) → Select "Turn on"

You will have to select at least two of the available methods (SMS, email, password) and click "Turn on." Enter your password and click next to confirm.

### Be Aware of Vulnerability to Law Enforcement Data Requests

Even if your TikTok account is secured with a strong, unique password and two-step verification, your data is still available to the company.

**Important**: Direct messages aren't end-to-end encrypted and the company specifies in its law enforcement guidelines that it can disclose user data to government agencies, including direct message content.

**Recommendation**: If you need to talk about sensitive topics that could put you or others at risk, don't use TikTok. Move the conversation to apps that use end-to-end encryption, like Signal or WhatsApp.

## Privacy Settings to Consider

Account security is essential but it's also important to weigh the available privacy options with how you want to use the app. A newsroom account may focus on maximizing the reach of its TikTok content, while a freelance journalist's account may want to have more control over the interactions.

### Review App Permissions

Many apps require multiple permissions to access information, such as location, storage, and contacts. When you launch TikTok for the first time, it will ask you for permission to send notifications and access your contacts.

If you don't feel comfortable letting TikTok access your contact list to recommend TikTok accounts of people you have in your directory, you can deny this permission or remove it.

**To manage permissions:**
- **Android** (may vary by version): Settings → Privacy → Permission manager
- **iOS**: Settings app → Apps → TikTok

### Private vs. Public Account

**Business Accounts**: For institutional accounts, using a Business Account gives access to more features like advanced statistics, post scheduler, and auto-messaging, but Business Accounts need to be public.

**To switch to Business Account:**
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Switch to Business Account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Switch to business account

**To make your account private:**
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Private account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Toggle private account on

### Controlling Interactions with Your Account and Posts

Access privacy settings at: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy

**Key settings to review:**

| Setting | Description | Recommendation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Activity status** | Shows when you and mutual followers are active on TikTok | Consider disabling if you don't want others to see when you're using the app |
| **Suggest your account to others** | Allows people to find you through mutual connections and shared links | Disable if you want more control over discoverability (note: TikTok will still suggest your account to people you follow) |
| **Comments** | Control who can comment on your videos and stories | Enable filters for spam and offensive comments |
| **Mentions and tags** | Control who can mention or tag you in content | Restrict based on your comfort level |
| **Direct messages** | Decide who can message you | Enable filtering for suspicious accounts |
| **Following list and liked videos** | Control visibility of your activity | Set based on privacy preferences |
| **Downloads, Duets, and Stitch** | Allow others to download or remix your content | Note: Disabling may reduce reach and engagement |

### Deactivating or Deleting Your Account

If needed, you can temporarily or permanently deactivate your TikTok account so that no one can see it or its content.

**To deactivate or delete:**
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account

**Note**: If you decide to permanently delete your account, you'll have a 30-day waiting period in which you can reverse the request and fully restore your account.
