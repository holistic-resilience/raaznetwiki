# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Before Recording

Ensure you are in a physically safe position before taking pictures or filming. Ideally, have another person recording you while you film, and vice versa, in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries. Plan this tactic in advance with your affinity group.

## Recommended Recording Tools

If you're using a smartphone, several apps can help you record safely:

| App | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **[ObscuraCam](https://guardianproject.info/apps/obscuracam/)** | Blur faces to protect identities |
| **[CameraV](https://guardianproject.info/apps/camerav)** | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| **[eyeWitness to Atrocities](http://www.eyewitnessproject.org/)** | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| **[Mobile Martus](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.martus.android)** | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |

## Secure Transmission

When sending files:

- **Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted
- **Use end-to-end encrypted messengers** like [Signal](https://whispersystems.org/)
- **Use GPG-encrypted email** via [Thunderbird with GPG](https://securityinabox.org/en/guide/thunderbird/windows)
- **Upload anonymously** using [Orbot](https://guardianproject.info/apps/orbot) on Android

If storing content on an SD card, consider encrypting your phone or passing the card to someone who can take it to safety.

## Publishing and Sharing

Before publishing recorded violations, take measures to protect your identity and others in the footage.

### Remove or Manage Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data that can identify where, when, and by whom they were taken. Remove this metadata using:

- [Metanull](https://securityinabox.org/en/lgbti-mena/metanull/windows)
- [Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)](https://mat.boum.org/)
- CameraV (can also add metadata useful for verification)

### Cover Your Tracks

- Use the [Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/) or Orbot for Android
- Use a VPN service to anonymize your connection and access blocked websites

### Seek Expert Guidance

If you're concerned about safety or legal implications of sharing information:

- Consult lawyers and human rights organizations
- Keep secure backups of original materials
- Decide whether to retain non-anonymized materials and how to store them securely
