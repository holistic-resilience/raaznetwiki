# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

Social media platforms help us connect with others and spread important messages. However, understanding the privacy implications of using these services is essential for protecting yourself and your data.

## The Privacy Challenge

Social media companies typically operate on a business model that monetizes user data. When you use their services, you agree to terms that often allow them to:

- Share information about your buying habits, social connections, and location with third parties
- Collect and use your birthday and other identifying information
- Make your data available in ways that may not be immediately clear to you

### Government and Law Enforcement Monitoring

Social media platforms are actively monitored by authorities who:

- Collect publicly available data from your profiles
- Gather metadata (such as when photos were taken or who you're connected to)
- Request private information from social media companies about individuals of interest, including human rights defenders and activists

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Small Group Organizing

- **[Signal](https://signal.org/)** or **[Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/)** — Secure chat apps ideal for carefully chosen groups
- **[Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/)** or **[Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/)** — Self-hosted options for larger private groups

### For Public Communication

- **[Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about)** or **[Bluesky](https://bsky.app/)** — Decentralized alternatives to X/Twitter
- **Blogs** — Platforms like [Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/), [Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/), or [Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html) for sharing news, images, and longer content

### For Email-Based Organizing

- **Mailing lists** — Available through [Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists) or [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)
- **Newsletters** — For reaching wider audiences with your messages

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp

While Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and spreading messages, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure. Both companies have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you choose to use them, consult the security guides for [Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp).
