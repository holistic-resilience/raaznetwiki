# Information at Rest

Information "at rest" refers to digital files and hard-copies that are being *stored* on devices, paper, or other formats—as opposed to information actively being transmitted.

## Digital Information at Rest

Digital information at rest includes all files stored on devices whenever they are not being transferred or communicated. This encompasses:

- **Documents**: Reports, evidence, proposals, contact lists, accounting records
- **Communications**: Emails, attachments, text messages
- **Metadata**: Hidden information about your files (creation dates, authors, locations)

### Common Storage Locations

- Computers and laptops
- Smartphones and tablets
- External hard drives and USB drives
- SD cards and CDs
- Cloud storage services
- Email provider servers

> **Note**: Portable devices like smartphones contain data at rest even while the device itself is in motion.

## Physical Information at Rest

Hard copies of sensitive data may be stored in:

- Filing cabinets and vaults
- Office desks and shelves
- Wallets and purses
- Books and ledgers

## Mapping Your Information

To protect your information effectively, consider these key questions for each piece of data:

1. **Location**: Where is it stored?
2. **Access**: Who can access it?
3. **Sensitivity**: How sensitive is the content to you, your organisation, or people mentioned?
4. **Necessity**: How important is it to keep?
5. **Retention**: How long should it be kept?

### Information Mapping Template

Create a table to catalog your most important information:

| Information Type | Location | Who Has Access | Sensitivity Level | Retention Period |
|------------------|----------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| *Example: Witness statements* | *Encrypted drive* | *Project lead only* | *High* | *Until case closed* |

Once you have mapped your critical information, you can identify concrete protection strategies for each category.

## Next Steps

After cataloging your information at rest, the next consideration is protecting **information in motion**—data that is actively being transferred or communicated between devices and people.
