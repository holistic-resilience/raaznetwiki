# Secure Communication

We'd probably object if someone put a microphone and camera in our room and could listen to our conversations at any time. Yet, whether through ordinary phone calls or messages on social media, we often use chat tools that enable the service provider to eavesdrop on our conversations.

For added privacy and security, we recommend using chat tools that are **end-to-end encrypted**—meaning only the participants in the conversation can read the calls and messages. No uninvited guests, not even the service provider, should be able to listen in.

## Encrypted Messaging Apps

### Signal

**Signal** is one of the best end-to-end encrypted messengers available, enabling voice, video, and messaging with other Signal users. Signal differentiates itself by minimizing the amount of information it stores about conversations, such as who spoke to whom and when.

**Related guides:**
- **Signal for Beginners** — Setting up and using Signal for Android and iOS, with safety considerations
- **Locking Down Signal** — Advanced settings to harden the app further and minimize risk
- **Taking Private Photos with Signal** — Using Signal to take ephemeral photos that aren't saved to your camera roll

### WhatsApp

With over two billion users, **WhatsApp** is one of the most popular chat services in the world. WhatsApp offers end-to-end encryption, but its parent company Meta collects significant metadata about users and contacts. Many useful security features are not enabled by default.

**Related guide:**
- **Upgrading WhatsApp Security** — Enabling stronger security settings and minimizing risk

### Keybase

**Keybase** is a powerful yet fairly simple end-to-end encrypted file-sharing and chat service.

**Related guide:**
- **Keybase for Beginners** — Setup, usage, and safety tips

## Encrypted Email

### Proton Mail

Since the 1990s, PGP has been the standard for encrypting messages and files, including email. However, it is notoriously tricky to use. **Proton Mail** is an email client designed to make PGP easier, particularly when messaging other Proton Mail users.

**Related guide:**
- **Proton Mail like a Pro** — Getting started with encrypted email

### Mailvelope

**Mailvelope** offers PGP email encryption through a browser add-on, enabling encrypted messaging with popular email clients including Gmail and Microsoft Outlook.

**Related guide:**
- **Encrypting Email with Mailvelope** — Setup and usage guide

## Key Takeaways

- **End-to-end encryption** ensures only conversation participants can read messages
- **Signal** offers strong privacy with minimal metadata collection
- **WhatsApp** provides encryption but requires manual security hardening
- **Encrypted email** options like Proton Mail and Mailvelope make PGP more accessible
