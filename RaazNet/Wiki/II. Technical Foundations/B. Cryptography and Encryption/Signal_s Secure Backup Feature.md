# Signal's Secure Backup Feature

## Overview

Losing your phone or deleting the Signal app used to mean losing all your important messages permanently. Signal has now introduced **Secure Backups**, an optional feature that allows you to back up and recover your messages while maintaining end-to-end encryption. This means the service operator cannot read your messages, even in backup form.

## Storage Options

### Free Tier
- **100MB** of storage for text messages
- Last **45 days** of messages and media
- Sufficient for most users, including heavy Signal users

### Paid Tier ($1.99/month)
- **100GB** of storage
- Extended message and media retention

### Local Backup
- Password-protected backup stored directly on your device
- No cloud storage required

## How to Enable Secure Backups

> **Note:** This feature is currently rolling out on Android first. iOS and desktop support are coming soon.

### For Android Users

1. **Join the Beta** (for immediate access): [Sign up for Signal Beta](https://support.signal.org/hc/en-us/articles/360007318471-Signal-Beta) to access the feature now, or wait for the stable release
2. **Open Signal** and tap the circle icon at the top left to access Settings
3. **Navigate to Settings > Backups**
4. **Choose your backup method:**
   - Secure Backup (cloud-based, encrypted)
   - On-device backup (local storage)

## Critical: Protecting Your Recovery Key

Signal does not retain a copy of your encryption keys. When you create a backup, Signal provides you with a **recovery key** that unlocks your backup archive.

**Important considerations:**
- Store your recovery key in a secure location, such as a [password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)
- If you lose this key, you **cannot** recover your backup
- Do not share this key with anyone
