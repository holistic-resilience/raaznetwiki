# Online Account Security

Data breaches involving passwords occur frequently, and journalists are particularly at risk as frequent targets of digital attacks. However, most online attacks aren't sophisticated. Attackers typically try to access accounts by:

- Guessing short, predictable passwords
- Sending **phishing** emails designed to trick you into entering credentials on malicious login pages

Fortunately, the defenses are straightforward. This guide provides resources to help protect your online accounts.

## Key Security Topics

### 1. Phishing Prevention and Email Hygiene

One of the most common security threats is phishing—convincing you to enter real credentials in a fake login form. Learn to identify phishing hints and implement simple defenses.

**Resource:** [Phishing Prevention and Email Hygiene](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/email-security-tips/)

### 2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

One of the best and easiest ways to strengthen login security is enabling two-factor authentication—requiring a second piece of information beyond your password. This covers:

- The strategy behind 2FA
- Common 2FA methods
- Step-by-step setup instructions

**Resource:** [Two-Factor Authentication for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/)

### 3. Creating Strong Passphrases

Publicly available breach data shows many people use short, predictable passwords, introducing unnecessary risk. Learn three ways to create secure, hard-to-predict **passphrases**—passwords composed of memorable codes and random words.

**Resource:** [Three Types of Passphrases](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/three-types-of-passphrases/)

### 4. Password Managers

Most people reuse passwords across multiple websites. If one site suffers a breach, hackers can reuse your password elsewhere. Password managers solve this by:

- Generating long, unique passwords for every website
- Isolating breaches to single accounts
- Auto-filling credentials on browsers and smartphones

**Resource:** [Choosing a Password Manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)

### 5. Passkeys (Passwordless Authentication)

You can't get phished if you don't type your password. Passkeys allow you to log into services by proving you have an authorized device. While not yet universally supported, set them up wherever available.

**Resource:** [Passkeys: Passwordless Logins for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/passkeys-beginners/)
