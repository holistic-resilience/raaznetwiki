# Recover from Possible Account Compromise

While it is rarely possible to know for certain if your account has been compromised, there are clear warning signs to watch for and immediate steps you can take to regain control.

## Signs Your Account May Have Been Compromised

Look for these indicators that someone may have accessed your account:

- **Unexpected changes** to content or account settings you didn't make
- **Messages sent** from your account that you didn't write
- **Login failures** despite entering correct credentials
- **Missing messages** that contacts confirm they sent
- **Information leaks** where a third party knows details shared exclusively through this account
- **Password change notifications** you didn't request (successful or not)
- **Lost or stolen device** that was signed into the account
- **Suspicious activity logs** showing connections from unfamiliar times or locations
- **Security alerts** from your service provider about login attempts

## Immediate Recovery Steps

### 1. Stop Using the Account for Sensitive Information

Pause all sensitive communications through this account until you understand what happened.

### 2. Change Your Password Immediately

Create a new, strong password. See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords).

### 3. Update Passwords on Related Accounts

If you used the same or similar passwords elsewhere, change them immediately with unique passwords for each account.

### 4. Review Connected Device Security

Audit all devices that:
- Have accessed this account
- Store the password for this account
- Hold your 2-factor authentication method

See guides on [protecting phones and computers from attacks](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers).

### 5. Check Linked Services

Many services allow login through Facebook, Google, Apple, or Microsoft accounts.

- Search your inbox for "verify your email" or "account confirmation" to find linked accounts
- Unlink services where possible
- Prioritize financial and sensitive communication accounts

### 6. Log Out of All Sessions

Most email and communication providers let you view active sessions:
- Review all logged-in locations
- Log out of unrecognized connections
- Re-authenticate on your trusted devices

See [basic security guides for phones and computers](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers) for platform-specific instructions.

### 7. Follow Platform-Specific Recovery Steps

Check the [social media guides](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/social-media/) for instructions under "If you think your account has been hacked" or "Protect your account."

## If You Cannot Log In

1. **Try standard password recovery** through the service's reset procedure
2. **Use the Digital First Aid Kit** troubleshooter: [I lost access to my account](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)
3. **Seek expert help** if recovery attempts fail
