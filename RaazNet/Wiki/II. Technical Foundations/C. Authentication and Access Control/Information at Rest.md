# Information at Rest

Information "at rest" refers to digital files and hard-copies that are being *stored* on devices, paper, or other formats—as opposed to information actively being transferred or communicated.

## Digital Information at Rest

Digital information at rest includes:

- **Documents**: Reports, evidence, proposals, contact information, accounting records
- **Communications**: Emails, attachments, text messages
- **Metadata**: Information about your files (creation dates, authors, locations)

This information may be stored on:

- Computers and laptops
- Phones and tablets
- External hard drives and USB drives
- SD cards and CDs
- Cloud servers and email provider servers

> **Note**: Portable devices like smartphones contain data "at rest" even while the device itself is in motion.

## Physical Information at Rest

Hard copies of sensitive data may be stored in:

- Filing cabinets and vaults
- Office desks and shelves
- Wallets and purses
- Books and ledgers

## Key Questions for Assessment

When cataloguing your information at rest, consider:

- **Location**: Where is the information stored?
- **Access**: Who has access to it?
- **Sensitivity**: How sensitive is the content to you, your organisation, or people mentioned (e.g., witness or victim statements)?
- **Importance**: How critical is it to retain this information?
- **Retention**: How long should it be kept?

## Mapping Your Information

Use a structured approach to map your information at rest. Create a table that documents:

| Information Type | Location | Access Level | Sensitivity | Retention Period |
|------------------|----------|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| *Example entry*  | *Where stored* | *Who can access* | *High/Medium/Low* | *Duration* |

Once you have catalogued your most important information and its locations, you can identify concrete protection strategies.

## Next Steps
