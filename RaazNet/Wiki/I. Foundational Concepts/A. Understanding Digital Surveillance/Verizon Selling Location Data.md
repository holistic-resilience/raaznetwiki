# Verizon Fined for Selling Location Data Without Consent

The U.S. Federal Communications Commission fined Verizon, alongside T-Mobile and AT&T, for selling customers' location data without consent through contracts with location aggregation companies. 

Verizon fought the fines, arguing that the law does not cover *device* location data, only *call* location data. The U.S. Court of Appeals for the 2nd Circuit disagreed and upheld the $46.9 million fine on the telecommunications giant for violations of the Communications Act.

**Source:** [Ars Technica: Court rejects Verizon claim that selling location data without consent is legal](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2025/09/court-rejects-verizon-claim-that-selling-location-data-without-consent-is-legal/)

## What You Can Do

### Understand How Your Phone Shares Location

Your phone is designed to give away your location—this is how it maintains connections to cellphone towers, GPS, and other data connections like Bluetooth or Wi-Fi. Along the way, you leave records of these connections with third parties.

In some circumstances (such as using [Apple's Find My feature](https://support.apple.com/en-za/guide/iphone/iph09b087eda/ios)), your device can even be found when it's offline. However, there are ways to reduce your exposure.

### Tighten Your Location Settings

Location data brokers often get your location by embedding tracking code in third-party apps. Make their job harder by adjusting your permissions:

- **Android users:** [Follow Google's location permissions guide](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507?hl=en)
- **iPhone users:** [Follow Apple's location services guide](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102647)

Many apps don't need location access—turn permissions off entirely or enable them only when needed.

### Uninstall Unneeded Apps

Remove third-party apps you don't strictly need. This reduces:
- Apps with potentially problematic tracking code
- Potential entry points for malware

You can always reinstall apps later as needed.

### Disable Advertising IDs

Remove location tracking based on device advertising identifiers. See Freedom of the Press Foundation's guide: [Stop Ad-Based Location Tracking](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/stop-ad-based-location-tracking/)

### Review Other Mobile Settings

While adjusting location settings, consider a broader security review. See: [Your Smartphone and You: A Handbook for Modern Mobile Maintenance](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/your-smartphone-and-you-handbook-modern-mobile-maintenance/)
