# Overall Framework for Context Analysis

Carrying out a systematic analysis of our context underpins our ability to design and maintain accurate security strategies. As we and our environment change, so too does our security situation.

## Evolving Threats

The threats we face are constantly changing, and our security strategy will only be effective if we acknowledge this fact and plan accordingly. If we take security precautions out of habit or based on hearsay, changing circumstances may render them ineffective.

While this manual follows the analysis process once, these tools should be used iteratively in accordance with your own planning processes to remain current.

## Analysis and Perception

The process outlined here helps us be as objective as possible in diagnosing our security situation. However, security remains fundamentally a question of perception.

Our perception of threats may be challenged, limited, or flawed. We may have:

- **Unrecognised threats** — dangers we cannot identify due to an unfamiliar environment or because opponents successfully conceal them (such as electronic surveillance)
- **Unfounded fears** — things that pose no actual danger but which we identify as threats due to misinformation from opponents or our own anxious emotional reactions, possibly related to past traumatic experiences

![Perception of Threats Diagram](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/ckeditor_assets/pictures/23/content_perceptionthreats.png)

## Improving Threat Perception

Getting a realistic idea of our threat level is possible, though challenging, if we keep these factors in mind. The goal is reducing unrecognised threats and unfounded fears so that our security practices are as effective as possible.

We can improve perception accuracy by:

- Undertaking systematic research and analysis
- Checking our perceptions with trusted friends or colleagues
