# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Facebook

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Facebook account, protect your privacy, and handle potential abuse on the platform.

Also see related guides on [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration**: Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Facebook's Policies

### Learn How Facebook Uses Your Information

Before sharing data on Facebook, understand what they do with your information:

- Review Facebook's terms on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/182)
- [Learn about Facebook's privacy policy](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/explanation/)
- Review their [terms of service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms/preview) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

> **Why this matters**: It's often unclear what online service providers do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these risks.

**Tip**: Install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see an overview of terms for each service you use.

### Learn What Facebook Will Turn Over to Governments

Social media sites may provide your information to governments or law enforcement if requested:

- [Meta's policy on law enforcement requests](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Transparency report on government data requests](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [Transparency report on content restrictions](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [All Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

## Don't Use the Mobile App

**Recommendation**: Access Facebook through a browser instead of the mobile app.

> **Why this matters**: Mobile apps can constantly gather information about you, even when you deny permissions for location, microphone, etc. Using a browser ensures Facebook isn't constantly connected to a device you carry everywhere.

## Create a New Account

You can [create a Facebook account](https://www.facebook.com/help/188157731232424) for free.

### Decide on Your Identity Strategy

**Real vs. Fake Name Considerations**:

- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for different activities (personal vs. work at minimum)
- If using a pseudonym, choose one that's consistent and that Facebook's algorithms will recognize as plausible
- Be aware of Facebook's [name policy](https://www.facebook.com/help/1090831264320592) — they may remove accounts with names not used in everyday life

> **Why this matters**: Many people maintain accounts with pseudonyms to organize and connect with others while protecting their free speech, safety, or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email on a [privacy-friendly service](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- If protecting your identity, use [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address) when creating the email

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number

If possible, set up your account with only an email address.

> **Why this matters**: Phone numbers can easily identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could use your number to find activities associated with you, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Skip "Find Friends"

- Dismiss requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share employer or educational background initially

> **Why this matters**: Social media uses your contact lists to recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you want to keep contacts hidden. Law enforcement could use these lists to build cases, or platforms might reveal your associations publicly.

### Designate an Account Manager

For emergencies:

- Facebook won't give others access to your account, but can ["memorialize" it](https://www.facebook.com/help/275013292838654/)
- Share login information with trusted contacts using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact)
- [Remove accounts for medically incapacitated persons](https://www.facebook.com/help/480409628639043)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test downloading your data before relying on the platform. Understand what format it comes in and how to use it elsewhere.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Recovery Information

- [Verify your recovery email](https://www.facebook.com/help/162801153783275/)
- [Verify your recovery phone number](https://www.facebook.com/help/124895950923762/)
- Update immediately if you lose access to either

> **Why this matters**: Attackers may change recovery information to gain control later. Secure your recovery email and phone, as adversaries may hack them to change your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your Facebook account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Recommended methods** (in order of preference):
1. Security key
2. Authenticator app
3. Security codes

**Avoid**: SMS or phone calls — your mobile carrier has full access to these, and they're easier to compromise.

- [How 2FA works on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/148233965247823/)
- Complete the ["How to keep your account secure" checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa)

### Save Recovery Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://www.facebook.com/help/148104135383285) for when you can't use your usual 2FA method
- Print codes and store them securely (e.g., locked safe)

### Consider Advanced Protection

[Advanced Protection](https://www.facebook.com/help/1052552578831700) provides stronger security for particularly visible people. It's only available to people selected by Facebook — if eligible, you'll see prompts to enroll.

### Watch for Phishing

- [Learn about suspicious emails and messages](https://www.facebook.com/help/225602007465207)
- [Check if Facebook actually emailed you](https://www.facebook.com/recent_emails/security)
- Never click links in security emails — log in directly instead

### Create an Alternative Identity

For anonymous use:
- Create your account through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use an [email created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Devices

Check which devices have recently logged in. If anything looks unfamiliar:
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

Find this in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Enable Login Notifications

Get alerts when someone tries logging in from an unusual device or location.

## If You Think Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Perform a security checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/review/?review_id=573933453011661)
2. Follow steps on [securing a hacked account](https://www.facebook.com/hacked) or use the [step-by-step walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/help/1306725409382822)
3. If standard procedures don't work, see [Digital First Aid Kit troubleshooter](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

### Review Connected Apps and Sites

- **Avoid** using Facebook to log into other sites — use unique passwords saved in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- Being anonymous on one site doesn't help if you're exposed on a connected one
- [Manage privacy settings for connected apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/218345114850283/)

## Decide What Information to Share

### Privacy Settings

- [Choose who sees your content](https://www.facebook.com/help/325807937506242#choose-who-you-share-content-with)
- Complete [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264)
- [Set up regular Privacy Checkup reminders](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264#set-up-privacy-checkup-reminders)
- [Manage what others see about you](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/dialog/how-to-clean-your-profile?helpref=privacycenter)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**High-risk information**:
- Personally identifying details
- Images revealing your home location
- Family member details
- Sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider separate accounts for sensitive communications)

**Remember**:
- Even trusted contacts can accidentally spread your information
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared
- Consider what you reveal about friends without their consent

> **Why this matters**: The more you reveal online, the easier it becomes to identify and monitor you. "Likes" on pages opposing government positions have led to surveillance and persecution in some countries.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [How location services work on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509/)
- If using the app, turn off location services
- [Don't add location to posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509#adding-or-sharing-your-location)

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Never post images showing**:
- Sensitive locations
- Identifiable individuals without consent
- Background details that reveal location

**Photo management**:
- [Delete uploaded photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/208547132518386/)
- [Control shared album visibility](https://www.facebook.com/help/410653545719324)
- [Delete albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/152153388188974)
- [Remove shared album contributors](https://www.facebook.com/help/589394671112683)
- [Remove metadata before posting](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

> **Why this matters**: Photos and videos can reveal information unintentionally. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and device.

### Manage "Likes" Visibility

- [Change "Who can see your social interactions alongside ads"](https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences/ad_settings) to "Only Me"
- [How video interactions are visible](https://www.facebook.com/help/410629549531206)

> **Why this matters**: In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when reacting to others' content.

### Don't Share Your Real Birthday

You can register with a different birthday and change it later. Keep track of what you enter for account recovery purposes.

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share Selectively

- Complete the ["Who can see what you share" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- Set Profile information to "Only me"
- Consider "Limit past posts"
- [Check your activity log](https://www.facebook.com/settings/)
- [Change app visibility](https://www.facebook.com/help/201750739897196)

### Manage Replies and Comments

- [Choose who can comment on Page posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/369765040737128)
- [Hide comments](https://www.facebook.com/help/297845860255949)
- [Stop profile posts from others](https://www.facebook.com/help/115469971891543)
- [Control Page visitor posts](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/356113237741414)

> **Why this matters**: Replies have been used to build false claims about associations. They can also be used for harassment.

### Manage Connections and Groups

- [Control friend requests](https://www.facebook.com/help/217125868312360)
- [Choose who can follow you](https://www.facebook.com/help/201148673283205)
- Check if groups are [public or private](https://www.facebook.com/help/2580077045343981)
- [What apps can access in groups](https://www.facebook.com/help/261149227954100)
- [Leave a group](https://www.facebook.com/help/172944012764072)
- [Remove/ban group members](https://www.facebook.com/help/211909018842184)
- [Delete group posts/comments](https://www.facebook.com/help/887936158224251)
- [Turn off group comments](https://www.facebook.com/help/987143528046827)

> **Why this matters**: Group membership reveals information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used in court. If adversaries might join groups to identify you, choose connections carefully.

### Manage Tags

- [Review tags on your posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/247746261926036)
- [Manage Page tags/mentions](https://www.facebook.com/help/www/1679637135584406) — set "Who can see posts you're tagged in" to "Only me"

### Limit Who Can Contact You

Complete the ["How people can find you" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/).
