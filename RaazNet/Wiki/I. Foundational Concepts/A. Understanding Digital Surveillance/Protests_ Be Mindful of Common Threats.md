# Protests: Be Mindful of Common Threats

Certain threats and tactics are regularly used against activists during protests. Being prepared for them—and sharing observations with your affinity group or the wider protest—can help everyone stay safer.

## Common Crowd Control Tactics

### Tear Gas

Tear gas is one of the most common "crowd control" measures and is increasingly used to suppress protests. The chemicals used vary by region and change over time, which means effective remedies also change.

**Preparation:**
- Research which type of tear gas authorities use in your area
- Obtain a gas mask if possible (avoid old masks, as they may be harmful)
- Swimming goggles can help protect your eyes

**If exposed:**
- Eyes can often be treated with water (avoid warm or hot water with CS gas, as it increases pain)
- Antacids diluted with water may help
- Diphoterine solution (a first aid product for chemical splashes) is also effective

### Water Cannons

Water cannons are commonly used against protesters and can be extremely dangerous at close range.

**Safety guidance:**
- If police prepare to use water cannons near you, move away as quickly as possible
- Maintain distance whenever possible

### Mounted Police

Police horses are generally well-trained but can be unpredictable and could cause serious injury.

**Safety guidance:**
- Never approach horses from behind
- Avoid standing or crouching directly in front of them

## Monitor and Share Security Indicators

The security situation at a protest can change rapidly. Sharing and analyzing anything unusual that might affect safety helps everyone make informed decisions early.

**Strategies for collective awareness:**
- Maintain awareness of your surroundings at all times
- Create a dedicated security group within the protest
- Designate a security person in your affinity group to monitor indicators and coordinate responses
- Share observations about changes in police behavior or positioning

For more on monitoring and analyzing security indicators, see [Security Indicators](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/chapters/explore/2-7-security-indicators.html).

## Take Care of Fellow Protesters

Everyone has an equal right to participate in protests—including children, elderly people, and people with disabilities. While protests carry risks for everyone, some individuals face particular challenges.

**Collective responsibility:**
- Offer help or protection to those who may be at greater risk
- Support anyone who wishes to peacefully protest
- Look out for people who may need assistance during rapidly changing situations
