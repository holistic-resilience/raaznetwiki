# Protect Your Data When Using TikTok

*Updated 3 April 2025*

Read this guide to increase your privacy and security when sharing and viewing videos or streaming content on TikTok.

> **Important Consideration:** Before relying on commercial, corporate products, assess whether they are secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

---

## Inform Yourself About TikTok's Policies

### Learn How TikTok Uses Your Information

- Review TikTok's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/1448)
- Read [TikTok's privacy policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/eea/privacy-policy/en) (select your region using the button above the title)
- Review TikTok's [Community Principles](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/community-principles) and [Community Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en)

**Why this matters:** It's often unclear what service providers do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Understanding these policies helps you make informed decisions. Consider installing [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see service overviews as you browse.

### Learn What TikTok Shares with Governments

- Review [TikTok's law enforcement guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/global/law-enforcement/en)
- Check [government removal requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/government-removal-requests-2024-1)
- Review [information requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/es/information-requests-2024-1)
- Explore [TikTok's transparency center](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/)

**Why this matters:** Social media sites may provide your information to governments or law enforcement when requested, including information you intended to keep private.

## Create a New Account

You can create a TikTok account for free. While TikTok suggests using their app, [using a web browser on a computer is more secure](#use-tiktok-more-securely).

**To create an account via web browser:**
1. Go to the [TikTok home page](https://www.tiktok.com/)
2. Click the Login button
3. Click "Sign up" at the bottom of the form
4. Choose "Use phone or email"
5. Select "Sign up with email" to avoid using a phone number

If you must use the app, see [TikTok's account creation guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/getting-started/creating-an-account/creating-an-account).

### Consider Using a Pseudonym

- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for different campaigns, activities, or to separate personal and work content
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency and establish ways for trusted contacts to verify your identity

**Why this matters:** Some people maintain accounts with pseudonyms to organize and connect with others while protecting their free speech, safety, or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a privacy-friendly server while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- See suggested [privacy-friendly email services](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number

When setting up a new account, avoid entering your phone number. TikTok allows account creation with only an email address.

**Why this matters:** Your phone number can easily be used to identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could request information from service providers using your phone number, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Don't Allow Contact Access

- Skip or dismiss TikTok's requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect with Facebook friends
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- If connecting with unfamiliar communities, [carefully consider what information to share](#decide-what-information-to-share)
- Avoid sharing employer or educational background in your bio

**Why this matters:** Social media platforms use your contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you need to keep your contacts hidden. Law enforcement might use these lists to build cases, or the platform might reveal your associations publicly.

### Plan for Account Access Emergencies

- TikTok does not accept requests from family members to delete or memorialize deceased users' accounts
- If needed, share login information with a trusted person using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- Review the [Digital First Aid Kit](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/) for additional guidance

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test how to download your data from TikTok. Review what data is provided, its format, and how you can use it.

**Why this matters:** Before storing data on a commercial platform, understand what it takes to move your data elsewhere. Complete deletion from large platforms may take considerable time.

## Protect Your Account

Review [TikTok's account security recommendations](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety).

### Verify Recovery Information

- [Check your recovery email and phone number](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/email-and-phone-number)
- Update immediately if you lose access to your recovery email or phone

**Why this matters:** Attackers may change recovery information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising them could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who accesses your TikTok account gains access to information about you and your connections.

- See [how to create and maintain strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)
- If prompted to create a passkey, read [recommendations on passkey usage](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords/#consider-how-and-when-to-use-passkeys)

### Enable Two-Step Verification (2SV)

- Use an authenticator app and email for verification
- Avoid SMS verification when possible—mobile carriers have access to these communications, and SMS is easier to compromise
- See [TikTok's 2-step verification guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety)
- Learn more in our [two-factor authentication guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa)

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- Learn [how to identify phishing messages claiming to be from TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/avoid-fraudulent-message-attacks-on-tiktok)
- For business accounts, see [how to avoid phishing attempts](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/how-to-avoid-phishing-attempts)

**Why this matters:** Phishing messages try to trick you into giving others access to your account. Never click links in security emails or provide your password. Instead, log in directly to your account to verify any alerts.

## Use TikTok More Securely

- While TikTok requires the app for many features, you can browse profiles, publish posts, and chat using a browser instead
- For anonymous use:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use [an email address created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)
  - [Avoid adding your phone number](#dont-associate-your-phone-number)

## Monitor Account Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

If you see suspicious activity:
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) not used elsewhere
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

**Why this matters:** If adversaries log into your account from their devices, you may be able to detect this in your Security & permissions settings.

### If Your Account Has Been Hacked

- [Reset your password and remove suspicious devices](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/my-account-has-been-hacked)
- If you can't log in, try [recovering your account through friend verification](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety#3)

## Control Your Information

### Manage Account Visibility

- [Consider making your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Change video settings to select specific audiences](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/video-visibility)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

Consider avoiding:
- **Personally identifying information**, including:
  - Content revealing where you live (videos shot in or near your home)
  - Details about family members
  - Information about sexual orientation or activities
  - Email addresses (consider maintaining separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional considerations:**
- Even trusted network members can easily copy and spread your information
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared
- Be sensitive about what you reveal about friends; ask them to reciprocate

**Why this matters:** The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. For example, sharing a post opposing your government's position could result in surveillance or persecution. This affects people globally—activists' families in their home countries may be targeted based on the activist's public online content.

### Disable Location Sharing

- [Learn how location works on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok)
- [Turn off location services](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#3)
- [Delete previously collected location data](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#4)

**Why this matters:** If you're concerned about location tracking, prevent your accounts from storing this information. Turning off device location services also improves battery life.

### Share Videos Safely

**Never post videos showing:**
- Sensitive locations
- Identifying information about yourself or others without consent

**Best practices:**
- If posting only private videos, [make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Remove metadata before posting videos shot outside TikTok](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)
- Seek consent before posting about others
- Work with colleagues to establish sharing guidelines

**Why this matters:** Videos can unintentionally reveal information, particularly in backgrounds. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and camera used.

### Hide Your Likes

Your liked videos are hidden by default, but verify this setting. [Ensure liked videos are visible only to you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/exploring-videos/liking#4).

**Why this matters:** "Likes" are sometimes shared in unclear ways. In some countries, "likes" have been used as evidence in legal cases.

## Control Who Sees What

### Manage Your Followers

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking problematic users](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/blocking-the-users)
- [Make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)

### Control Comments

- [Choose who can comment on your videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#4)—select "No one" to disable all comments
- [Turn off comments for specific videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#5)
- [Filter comments requiring your approval](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#3)
- [Delete comments](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#6)

**Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims about human rights defenders' associations. Comments can also be used for harassment. Disabling them can reduce stress.

### Curate Your Connections

- [Unfollow people](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/following-and-unfollowing)
- [Remove followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers)

**Why this matters:** Your connections reveal information about you. People may assume you support what your connections say or do. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in legal cases.

### Control Mentions

- [Control who can mention you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/messaging-and-notifications/mentions-on-tiktok#3): Everyone, People you follow, Friends, or No one
- [Report inappropriate hashtags](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/report-a-problem/report-a-hashtag)

**Why this matters:** Like comments and connections, mentions and hashtags could be used to make false accusations about your activities.

### Limit Direct Messages

- [Learn how to control direct messages](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/controlling-your-dms)
- [Choose who can send you messages](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message)
- [Filter, hide, report messages, or block senders](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message#3)

**Why this matters:** Limiting contact options reduces the likelihood of being found when trying to maintain privacy, or being targeted by those seeking to gain your trust
