# Device Searches at US Border Hit Record Numbers

## Overview

According to U.S. Customs and Border Protection, the number of device searches conducted between April and June reached an all-time high. CBP searched **14,899 devices** during this quarter.

### Search Types

- **Basic searches (~93%)**: A border official manually examines a device
- **Advanced searches (~7%)**: Officials use forensic tools designed to copy and process data on mobile devices

This quarterly figure tops the previous record of 12,766 devices (January–March 2022) by **16.7 percent**.

**Source**: [Wired: Phone Searches at the US Border Hit a Record High](https://www.wired.com/story/phone-searches-at-the-us-border-hit-a-record-high/)

## What You Can Do

### Prepare Your Devices for Search

Americans returning from abroad cannot be denied entry, but you may still be asked to unlock your devices. Risks include:

- Delays and extended detention
- Device seizure for search

**Non-U.S. citizens face elevated risks**, including potential denial of entry or deportation.

📖 **Read**: [FPF Guide on Preparing Devices for Border Crossings](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/border-security/)

### Consider Your Online Presence

Your online posts and reporting may be scrutinized before arrival. Australian journalist Alistair Kitchen was pulled from a customs line, with a federal officer explicitly stating: *"It's because of what you wrote online about the protests at Columbia University."*

Depending on your citizenship, visa status, and reporting focus, this may represent elevated risk.

📖 **Read**: [U.S. Press Freedom Tracker Report on This Incident](https://pressfreedomtracker.us/all-incidents/australian-writer-deported-after-interrogation-over-reporting-phone-search/)
