# TikTok Security Considerations for Newsrooms

By the end of 2021, TikTok reached one billion monthly users, becoming one of the most popular social media platforms globally. In the United States, the platform significantly increased its share of users who regularly use it for news consumption.

Whether you're a journalist new to the platform or part of a long-time TikToking newsroom, understanding how to mitigate potential dangers is essential. This guide covers the most important security considerations—including account hijacking, data breaches, and privacy—with recommendations for navigating the platform securely.

## Understanding Concerns About the Platform

### Data Collection and Profiling

TikTok's algorithm personalizes your experience through extensive data collection, analysis of interests and interactions, and profiling of user behavior. In late 2022, TikTok admitted they targeted journalists using the app to investigate their sources, raising significant concerns among U.S. lawmakers about what specific information is collected and for what purposes.

### Chinese Ownership and Data Access

TikTok is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance. While the platform has stated that none of its data centers are subject to Chinese law and that content isn't removed based on sensitivities related to China, leaked internal information revealed that U.S. user data has been repeatedly accessed from China. The company has confirmed that personal data on TikTok users who sign up to make money through the app is stored in China.

### Additional Security Concerns

- **Clipboard Access**: A 2020 Apple update revealed the TikTok app was constantly accessing user clipboard content on iOS
- **In-App Browser Monitoring**: Research in 2022 discovered its in-app browser includes code that can monitor user keystrokes on websites

**Recommendation**: Use a trusted browser installed on your device (Chrome, Firefox, or Brave) instead of TikTok's in-app browser.

### Content Awareness

When creating content for TikTok, be mindful of sharing information that you don't want public or that could put you or others at risk, including:
- Information about your daily routine
- Personal data about yourself or loved ones
- Source information

## Securing Your TikTok Account

### Use a Unique and Strong Password

Many people use short, predictable passwords, introducing unnecessary risk to account safety. Regardless of whether it's a personal or institutional account, protect it with a unique and strong password.

**Recommendations**:
- Use passphrases (a sequence of random words or other text)—they're harder to guess and easier to remember
- Use a password manager to generate and securely store passphrases with access from multiple devices

**To change your password**:
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Password*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Password > Enter code > Enter new password*

### Adding Two-Step Verification

While TikTok doesn't support physical security keys or authentication apps, activating two-step verification adds an extra layer of security. Once configured, you'll be prompted for a 6-digit one-time code at login after validating your password.

**To enable two-step verification**:
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Security > 2-step verification*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Security > 2-step verification > Choose phone, email, or additional password (must select 2) > Select "Turn on"*

Select at least two available methods (SMS, email, password) and click *Turn on*. Enter your password and click next to confirm.

### Law Enforcement Data Requests

Even with a strong password and two-step verification, your data remains available to the company. Key considerations:

- Direct messages are **not end-to-end encrypted**
- TikTok's law enforcement guidelines specify they can disclose user data to government agencies, including direct message content

**Recommendation**: For sensitive topics that could put you or others at risk, don't use TikTok. Move conversations to apps with end-to-end encryption like Signal or WhatsApp.

## Privacy Settings to Consider

Account security is essential, but it's also important to weigh available privacy options with how you want to use the app. A newsroom account may focus on maximizing reach, while a freelance journalist may want more control over interactions.

### App Permissions

When you launch the app for the first time, it will ask for permission to send notifications and access your contacts. If you're uncomfortable letting TikTok access your contact list, deny this permission.

**To manage permissions**:
- **Android**: *Settings > Privacy > Permission manager*
- **iOS**: *Settings app > Apps > TikTok*

### Private vs. Public Accounts

**Business Accounts** provide access to advanced statistics, post scheduler, and auto-messaging, but must be public.

**To switch to Business Account**:
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Switch to Business Account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Switch to business account*

**To make your account private**:
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy > Private account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy > Toggle private account on*

### Controlling Interactions

Access privacy settings at: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy*

Available options include:

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Activity status** | Controls whether others see when you're active. Consider disabling if you don't want others to know when you're using the app. |
| **Suggest your account to others** | Controls how TikTok suggests your account. Even if disabled, TikTok still suggests your account to people you follow. |
| **Comments** | Set who can comment on your videos and stories; filter comments containing specific keywords, spam, or offensive content. |
| **Mentions and tags** | Control who can mention or tag you in videos, descriptions, stickers, comments, and stories. |
| **Direct messages** | Decide who can message you; set read receipts; filter suspicious messages. |
| **Following list and liked videos** | Set visibility of your following list and liked videos. |
| **Downloads and sharing** | Control whether others can download your videos or use them in Duets and Stitch. Note: disabling may reduce reach and engagement. |

### Account Deactivation or Deletion

You can temporarily or permanently deactivate your TikTok account so no one can see it or its content. Permanent deletion includes a 30-day waiting period during which you can reverse the request.

**To deactivate or delete**:
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Deactivate or delete account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Deactivate or delete account*
