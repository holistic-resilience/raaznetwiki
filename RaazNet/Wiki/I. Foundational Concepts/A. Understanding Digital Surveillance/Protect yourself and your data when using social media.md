# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

*Updated 11 March 2025*

## Why Social Media Privacy Matters

We use social media platforms to connect with others and spread important messages. However, the companies managing these platforms often have business models based on monetizing our communications and contacts. They share information about us with third parties—including our buying habits, social connections, location, birthday, and other identifying details—often without us realizing it.

When you use their services, you agree to terms that allow them to share or sell this information. Sometimes it's unclear just how public that information becomes.

### Government Surveillance Concerns

Social media platforms are actively monitored by governments and law enforcement agencies. Authorities:

- Collect publicly available data
- Gather "metadata" we may not think about (like when a photo was taken or who is connected to whom)
- Request private information from social media companies about individuals of interest, often including human rights defenders and activists

## Securing Your Social Media Accounts

While this guide covers specific platforms, most social networking services behave similarly and offer comparable security options. We recommend reviewing your account settings on each platform you use.

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Private Group Communication

- **Small groups**: Use [secure chat apps](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications) like [Signal](https://signal.org/) or [Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/)
- **Larger private groups**: Set up a [Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/) or [Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/) instance

### For Public Messaging

- **Microblogging**: Explore [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about) or [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) as alternatives to X. See [our blog post on leaving X](https://securityinabox.org/en/blog/x-odus) for more options
- **Blogging platforms**: [Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/), [Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/), or [Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html)
- **Mailing lists**: [Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists) or [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)
- **Newsletters**: [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp

While Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and spreading messages, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure—their parent companies have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you use them, read our guides on using [Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram) or [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) more securely.
