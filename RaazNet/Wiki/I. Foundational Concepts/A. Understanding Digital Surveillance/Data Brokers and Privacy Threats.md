# Data Brokers and Privacy Threats

## What Are Data Brokers?

Data brokers are companies that collect and sell consumers' personal information. They obtain this data by:
- Purchasing information from other businesses
- Scraping public records
- Aggregating data from various online sources

That unsettled feeling you get when served an all-too-accurate advertisement? That's data brokers at work.

## The Government Connection

While many Americans feel uncomfortable with this relentless tracking, the relationship between data brokers and government remains problematic:

- **Federal purchases**: The government has long been purchasing location data from brokers
- **Fourth Amendment concerns**: This practice likely violates constitutional protections against unreasonable searches
- **Journalist and source protection**: This data broker loophole directly threatens journalists and whistleblowers

### Recent Developments

**Setbacks:**
- Much-needed regulation continues to be weakened or eliminated
- The CFPB quietly killed rules designed to shield Americans from data brokers

**Progress:**
- Some states are making progress toward closing the law enforcement data broker loophole (Montana became the first)
- Congressional pressure on brokers caught hiding their opt-out pages from search engines

## How to Protect Yourself

### 1. Understand the Landscape
Learn how your data is tracked, bought, and sold. Knowing what happens behind the scenes helps you right-size your defenses.

### 2. Use an Opt-Out Service
**Paid options:**
- Abine's DeleteMe (yearly subscription)
- Consumer Reports published a [helpful comparison guide](https://innovation.consumerreports.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Data-Defense_-Evaluating-People-Search-Site-Removal-Services-.pdf) to find a service that fits your needs and budget

### 3. DIY Opt-Out Approach
For those who prefer a hands-on approach:
- Use Yael Grauer's [Big Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List](https://github.com/yaelwrites/Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List)
- Cross-reference this list against reports from any opt-out service you use to identify coverage gaps

## Why This Matters for Journalists

The data broker ecosystem poses particular risks for journalists and their sources:
- Location data can reveal meetings with confidential sources
- Personal information can be used to identify and target whistleblowers
- Aggregated data creates detailed profiles that compromise operational security

Taking steps to remove your information from data broker databases is an essential part of source protection and personal security hygiene.
