# Brainstorming New Capacities

![Strategise detail 03](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/media/exercises/images/default/strategise_detail_03.jpg%3F1465900908)

## Purpose & Outputs

This exercise helps you identify new capacities needed to maintain your well-being and security. By examining each prioritized threat alongside your existing capacities and vulnerabilities, you can develop targeted strategies to reduce risk and strengthen your overall security posture.

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this exercise, you should have completed:

- **Exercise 2.5b**: Threat identification and prioritization
- **Exercise 3.5a**: Capacity assessment
- **Exercise 3.5b**: Vulnerability assessment

## Format & Steps

Reflect on the threats you face and your existing capacities and vulnerabilities identified in previous exercises. Document your answers using the format provided in Appendix A, or create your own structured template.

For each prioritized threat, work through the following questions:

### 1. Whom or What is Under Threat?

What new capacities should the person, people, or assets under threat build to reduce the likelihood or impact of the identified threat?

### 2. Who is Behind the Threat?

- How might you influence the cost-benefit analysis of the people or institution behind the threat?
- Can you improve their tolerance or acceptance of your work?
- Are there ways to deter them from acting against you?

### 3. How Could the Threat Be Carried Out?

- What information would be necessary for adversaries to carry out an attack?
- How can you better protect sensitive information about your work?
- What measures can prevent critical information from falling into the wrong hands?

### 4. Where Could the Threat Occur?

How can you increase security capacities in your physical spaces (buildings, vehicles, private property) to make this threat less likely or less damaging?

### 5. Psychological, Emotional, and Health Considerations

In the context of this threat, what practices can you build to:

- Reduce stress and tiredness
- Maintain awareness
- React more creatively to emerging threats
