# TikTok Security Considerations for Newsrooms

By the end of 2021, TikTok reached one billion monthly users, becoming one of the most popular social media platforms globally. In the United States, the platform significantly increased its share of users who regularly use it for news consumption.

Whether you're a journalist new to the platform or part of a long-time TikToking newsroom, understanding how to mitigate the potential dangers of the platform is key. This guide focuses on the most important security considerations—like account hijacking, data breaches, and privacy—and provides recommendations for navigating the platform securely.

## Understanding Concerns About the Platform

The success of TikTok isn't random. Like other social media platforms, the algorithm that personalizes your experience is powered by data collection, analysis of interests and interactions, and profiling of user behavior. In late 2022, TikTok admitted they targeted journalists using the app to investigate their sources. All of this raises concerns among U.S. lawmakers, as the company has been vague and evasive in explaining what specific information is collected about users and for what purposes.

### Data Access and Storage Concerns

TikTok is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance. While the platform has stated that none of its data centers are subject to Chinese law and that content isn't removed based on sensitivities related to China, leaked internal information revealed in 2022 that U.S. user data has been repeatedly accessed from China. In a letter to the U.S. Senate, the company confirmed that personal data on TikTok users who sign up to make money through the app is stored in China.

Some former national security officials and other experts believe users' personal data would still be exposed to hacking and espionage, even with proposed security deals.

### App Behavior Concerns

- **Clipboard Access**: A 2020 Apple update revealed that the TikTok app was constantly accessing the user's clipboard content on iOS
- **Keystroke Monitoring**: In 2022, research discovered its in-app browser includes code that can monitor user keystrokes on websites

**Recommendation**: Use a trusted browser installed on your device (Chrome, Firefox, or Brave) instead of TikTok's in-app browser.

### Content Creation Awareness

When creating content for TikTok, be mindful of sharing information that you don't want public or that could put you or others at risk. This includes:
- Information about your daily routine
- Personal data about yourself or loved ones
- Source information

## Securing Your TikTok Account

### Use a Unique and Strong Password

Publicly-available data breaches show that many people use short, predictable passwords, introducing unnecessary risk to account safety. Regardless of whether it's a personal or institutional account, it must be protected by a unique and strong password.

**Recommendations**:
- Use passphrases (a sequence of random words or other types of text)—they are harder to guess and easier to remember
- Use a password manager to generate and securely store your passphrases while having access to them from multiple devices

**To change your TikTok password**:
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Password → Enter code → Enter new password

TikTok will send you a 6-digit code to validate your identity before allowing the password change.

### Adding Two-Step Verification

While TikTok doesn't support two-factor authentication methods like physical security keys or authentication apps, we still recommend activating two-step verification. Once configured, you will be prompted for a 6-digit one-time code at login, right after validating your password.

**To enable two-step verification**:
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Security → 2-step verification → Choose phone, email, or additional password (must select 2) → Select "Turn on"

You must select at least two of the available methods (SMS, email, password) and click "Turn on." Enter your password and click next to confirm.

### Law Enforcement Data Requests

Even if your TikTok account is secured with a strong, unique password and two-step verification, your data is still available to the company.

**Important**: Direct messages aren't end-to-end encrypted, and the company specifies in its law enforcement guidelines that it can disclose user data to government agencies, including direct message content.

**Recommendation**: If you need to discuss sensitive topics that could put you or others at risk, don't use TikTok. Move the conversation to apps that use end-to-end encryption, like Signal or WhatsApp.

## Privacy Settings to Consider

Account security is essential, but it's also important to weigh the available privacy options with how you want to use the app. A newsroom account may focus on maximizing reach, while a freelance journalist's account may want more control over interactions.

### App Permissions

Many apps require multiple permissions to access information such as location, storage, and contacts. When you launch TikTok for the first time, it will ask for permission to send notifications and access your contacts.

If you don't feel comfortable letting TikTok access your contact list to recommend accounts of people in your directory, you can deny or remove this permission:
- **Android** (may vary by version): Settings → Privacy → Permission manager
- **iOS**: Settings app → Apps → TikTok

### Private vs. Public Accounts

**Business Accounts**: Using a Business Account provides access to advanced features like statistics, post scheduler, and auto-messaging, but Business Accounts must be public.

**To switch to a Business Account**:
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Switch to Business Account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Switch to business account

**To make your account private**:
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Private account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Toggle private account on

### Controlling Interactions

Access privacy options at: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Privacy

**Key settings to configure**:

| Setting | Description | Recommendation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Activity status** | Shows when you're active on TikTok to mutual followers | Consider disabling if you don't want others to see when you're using the app |
| **Suggest your account to others** | Allows TikTok to recommend your account based on mutual connections and shared links | Disable if you want more control over discoverability (note: TikTok will still suggest your account to people you follow) |
| **Comments** | Control who can comment on your videos and stories; filter comments for keywords, spam, or offensive content | Configure based on your moderation capacity |
| **Mentions and tags** | Control who can mention or tag you in videos, descriptions, stickers, comments, and stories | Restrict if you want to limit unsolicited tagging |
| **Direct messages** | Set who can message you; filter suspicious accounts; control read receipts | Restrict to limit unwanted contact |
| **Following list and liked videos** | Control visibility of your following list and liked content | Consider restricting for privacy |
| **Downloads, Duets, and Stitch** | Allow others to download your videos or use them in their content | Note: Disabling may reduce reach and engagement |

### Account Deactivation and Deletion

You can temporarily or permanently deactivate your TikTok account so that no one can see it or its content.

**To deactivate or delete your account**:
- **Android**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account
- **iOS**: TikTok → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account

**Note**: If you permanently delete your account, you'll have a 30-day waiting period during which you can reverse the request and fully restore your account.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** and a password manager
2. **Enable two-step verification** with at least two methods
3. **Avoid sensitive conversations** on TikTok—use end-to-end encrypted apps instead
4. **Review app permissions** and remove unnecessary access
5. **Configure privacy settings** based on your account's purpose
6. **Use external browsers** instead of TikTok's in-app browser
7. **Be mindful of content** that could expose you, sources, or others to risk
