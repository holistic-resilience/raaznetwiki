# A Journalist's Guide to Digital Security Risk Assessment

A risk assessment (or "threat model") helps journalists and filmmakers identify potential digital security challenges and develop strategies to mitigate them. It's a systematic process for evaluating the digital assets you own, create, or engage with.

This tool is extensible—you can work through this process repeatedly, perhaps as a first step for covering a new story, or during each phase of an investigation. Risk will change based on the work you are trying to do and where you are trying to do it.

## My Assets: What Am I Protecting?

- **Devices**: cellphones, computers, tablets
- **Communications**: email, texts, social media posts, direct messages
- **Documents**: tips from sources, field notes, drafts
- **Footage**: interviews and b-roll
- **Browsing data**: search history

From morning until night, if you are using a device, you are generating an asset of some kind. Start your risk assessment by tracking everything you do in a day with your phone, computer, and anything else that might store your information.

Once you've tracked your activities, look for patterns in the data you're building. This can narrow the field to assets you'd like to protect, prioritizing those most important to you (e.g., passwords to accounts containing personal information or contact information of sensitive sources). Identifying high-priority assets will help you address risks efficiently.

## My Adversary: Who Am I Protecting My Assets From?

### Governments
Foreign and domestic governments collect and use data in various ways. They gain access to privileged information through networked devices, sometimes using [targeted exploits](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/04/02/us/politics/nso-contract-us-spy.html) aimed at specific individuals or groups. Governments also employ [dragnet surveillance policies](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2024/apr/16/house-fisa-government-surveillance-senate) that impact nearly everyone online.

### Corporations
Companies increasingly buy and sell data for [targeted advertising](https://www.newamerica.org/oti/reports/special-delivery/the-role-of-data-in-the-targeted-advertising-industry/) and [financial gain](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/data-brokers-are-running-wild-and-only-congress-can-rein-them). Personal information can also [become an asset](https://themarkup.org/privacy/2024/02/23/what-happens-to-your-sensitive-data-when-a-data-broker-goes-bankrupt) when companies are bought and sold.

### Hackers
Hackers exploit system vulnerabilities for [ransomware](https://techcrunch.com/2024/10/31/2024-looks-set-to-be-another-record-breaking-year-for-ransomware-and-its-likely-going-to-get-worse/) or data exfiltration. They often work in groups and, in [certain](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/03/08/microsoft-hack-email-russia/) [cases](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2024/11/02/china-typhoon-hack-us-election-trump/), are sponsored by nation-states.

### Scammers
Scammers engage in [social engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_(security)) through psychological manipulation to extract confidential information or [beneficial behavior](https://www.thecut.com/article/amazon-scam-call-ftc-arrest-warrants.html). Attack venues include phishing emails, phone calls, text messages, and [evolving new tactics](https://hbr.org/2024/10/phishing-attacks-are-evolving-heres-how-to-resist-them).

### Trolls
Trolls engage in [provocative behaviors](https://datasociety.net/library/source-hacking-media-manipulation-in-practice/) online, including sharing personal information (addresses, phone numbers) to unleash [threats](https://pressfreedomtracker.us/all-incidents/five-members-neo-nazi-group-arrested-alleged-targeted-harassment-journalists/) that transcend the digital world.

While journalists are a high-risk group, these adversaries affect most everyone online. There are many steps you can take to protect yourself.

## The Adversary's Resources: What Might They Be Capable Of?

### Legal Requests
Government agencies worldwide can [legally request](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/one-nation-under-risaa/) user information from service providers (Meta, Google, your ISP). This data is collected for national security purposes or as evidence in criminal cases. In the United States, journalists have [legal protections](https://www.justice.gov/archives/jm/criminal-resource-manual-661-privacy-protection-act-1980) for information collected during newsgathering.

### Illegal Activities
Some adversaries engage in illegal activities like hacking networks and systems for unlawful access to sensitive information. They may extort organizations and individuals or attempt to trick you into disclosing private information.

**Important**: These actors do not distinguish between professional and personal accounts, and often rely on intimidation tactics like online harassment and physical threats.

## Likelihood: How Big of a Risk Is This to Me Now?

The most important question in risk assessment is how likely you are to be targeted. We regularly hear about scary new cybersecurity threats while using technologies we aren't meant to fully understand. This makes it difficult to gauge the likelihood of any given threat.

Your identity is a factor worth considering. For example, female journalists are [far more likely](https://www.theguardian.com/media/article/2024/may/06/female-journalists-under-attack-as-press-freedom-falters) to experience online harassment than their male peers.

Focus on account security:
- [Writing solid passwords](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/three-types-of-passphrases/)
- [Using a password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)
- [Choosing strong two-factor authentication](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/)
- [Staying aware of phishing tactics](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/email-security-tips/)

### Safeguard Your Communications
Use encrypted channels like Signal and WhatsApp:
- Signal [primer](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/signal-beginners/) and [deep dive](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/locking-down-signal/)
- [Upgrading WhatsApp security](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/upgrading-whatsapp-security/)
- [Encrypting](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/encrypting-email-mailvelope-guide/) your [email messages](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/protonmail-pro/)

### Protect Browsing Habits
- [Choosing a browser](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/-depth-guide-choosing-web-browser/)
- Understanding [VPN use cases](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-vpn/)

### Protect Source Communications
- [Setting up a tipline](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/security-confidential-tip-pages/)
