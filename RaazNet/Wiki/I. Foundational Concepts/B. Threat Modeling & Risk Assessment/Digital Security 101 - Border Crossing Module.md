# Digital Security 101: Border Crossing Module

This module focuses on preparing journalists and others for traversing the U.S. southern border, with emphasis on protecting digital devices and data during border crossings.

## Overview

Unlike standard digital security modules, this curriculum emphasizes:
- **Individual risk assessment** based on personal attributes (nationality, profession)
- **Destination-specific considerations** when traveling between countries
- **Practical interview preparation** for U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) encounters

This module was developed in collaboration with the [Electronic Frontier Foundation](https://www.eff.org/), the University of Texas at El Paso [Multimedia Journalism Program](https://www.utep.edu/liberalarts/communication/academic-programs/undergraduate/ba-multimedia-journalism.html), and [Borderzine](https://borderzine.com/).

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, participants will be able to:

1. **Identify** information assets and physical devices requiring protection at borders
2. **Develop** strategies for minimizing risk to data stored on devices
3. **Recognize** the most pressing risks to data from border officials
4. **Create** a plan for speaking with CBP officials if detained

## Why This Matters

Reporters living and working near the U.S. southern border routinely experience elevated surveillance levels. Learning practical protective steps for key moments is essential for journalists in this region.

## Session Structure

**Estimated Time:** 80-90 minutes

### Topics Covered

1. Risk assessment for border travel
2. Preparing devices for potential search
3. Coordinating with collaborators
4. Minimizing risk during border screening
5. Simulated CBP interview practice

## Pre-Class Homework

**Required:**
- Read CBP's "[Border Search of Electronic Devices](https://www.cbp.gov/sites/default/files/assets/documents/2023-Oct/Border%20Search%20of%20Electronic%20Devices%20Tearsheet.pdf)" handout

**Optional:**
- "[Researching the Mexico-US border: a tale of dataveillance](https://shura.shu.ac.uk/33041/3/Meneses-Gutierrez-ResearchingTheMexico-US%28VoR%29.pdf)" by Mitxy Mabel Meneses Gutierrez
- NBC News: "[U.S. officials made list of reporters, lawyers, activists to question at border](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/immigration/u-s-officials-made-list-reporters-lawyers-activists-question-border-n980301)"

## Post-Class Assignment

**Optional:** Learn to encrypt portable USB devices using BitLocker To Go, Disk Utility, or VeraCrypt via the "[Encryption toolkit for media makers](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/introduction/)"

## Class Activities

1. **Case Study Analysis:** Examine why performer Olivia Rodrigo may have been pulled into secondary screening
2. **Asset Identification Game:** Family Feud-style activity identifying physical objects and data assets needing protection
3. **Interview Simulation:** Practice screening conversations between CBP officers and reporters

## Discussion Questions

- What data would you want to remove from your device before crossing?
- What types of data would be impractical to remove? Why?
