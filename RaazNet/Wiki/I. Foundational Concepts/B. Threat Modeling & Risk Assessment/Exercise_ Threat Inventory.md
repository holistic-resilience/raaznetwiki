# Threat Inventory Exercise

## Purpose

This exercise helps you prioritize threats and analyze their causes, ramifications, sources, required resources, existing actions, and possible next steps.

**Output:** A detailed inventory of your prioritized threats, which will be used in [Chapter 3.1: Responding to Threats](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/chapters/strategise/3-1-responding-to-threats.html) to create actionable response plans.

---

## Required Materials

**Input from Previous Exercises:**
- Actor and relational maps
- Information ecosystem analysis
- Security indicators
- Impact/Likelihood matrix
- Threat brainstorm results

**Supplies:**
- Pen and paper
- Flip-chart (for group work)

---

## Exercise Steps

### Step 1: Prioritize Threats

Review your threat brainstorm from the previous exercise. Evaluate each threat based on:
- **Likelihood** — How probable is this threat?
- **Impact** — How severe would the consequences be?

Select the threats that score highest on both dimensions for detailed analysis.

> **Tip:** Consider organizing threats by activity type (e.g., protest-related threats vs. daily office operations) to make analysis more manageable.

### Step 2: Document Each Threat

For each prioritized threat, record:
- **Title** — A clear, descriptive name
- **Summary** — Brief description of the threat

### Step 3: Break Down Complex Threats

If a threat is complex, divide it into sub-threats for separate analysis. 

**Example:** An "office raid" threat might be broken into:
- Arrests of staff
- Confiscation of devices
- Judicial harassment
- Loss of sensitive documents

Use separate rows in your template for each sub-threat.

### Step 4: Analyze Each Threat

Work through the following questions for each threat or sub-threat:

#### What Happens?

Describe the consequences if the threat is carried out:
- Impact on you personally
- Impact on your organization
- Impact on allies and partners
- Physical damages (property, space)
- Human costs (stress, trauma, safety)
- Information compromise

#### Who Is Behind It?

Identify the adversary using your actor map:
- What are their capabilities?
- What are their limitations?
- Are there neutral parties or allies who could influence them?
- Is there a history of similar actions against you or allies?

#### Who or What Is Targeted?

Identify potential targets:
- Specific information at risk
- Specific people under threat (physical, emotional, financial)
- Material resources (property, equipment)

#### How Would It Happen?

Consider the information requirements:
- What information does the adversary need to carry out this threat?
- Where might they obtain this information?

#### Where Would It Occur?

Analyze the location:
- Does the attacker need physical proximity?
- What are the security characteristics of this location?
- What makes this location vulnerable or protective?

---

## Reflection Questions

After completing your threat analysis, consider these additional factors:

1. **Mindset Impact:** How might your current mental state affect your planning and contingency measures?

2. **Activity Context:** Does this threat occur during a particular activity? What mental or physical state are you typically in during such activities? What practices could protect you, and what might increase vulnerability?

3. **Behavioral Factors:** What elements of your behavior or state of mind might increase the probability of this threat occurring, or worsen its impact?
