# Situational Monitoring: PESTLE Analysis Exercise

## Purpose

This exercise helps you systematically analyze external factors that may affect your security. By examining developments across six key domains, you can identify emerging threats, anticipate changes, and strengthen your security planning.

**Output:** A documented assessment of current situational factors and their potential security implications.

## Materials Needed

- Paper and pens (or digital note-taking tool)
- Access to your regular news and information sources

## Exercise Steps

### Step 1: Assess Your Current Monitoring Practices

Reflect on how you currently track developments in your environment:

- What methods do you use for situational monitoring and analysis?
- What spaces exist for discussing ongoing developments with colleagues or peers?
- How frequently do you conduct this type of analysis?

### Step 2: Evaluate Your Information Sources

List your primary sources of information and assess each one:

| Source | Strengths | Weaknesses | Objective or Biased? |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
|        |           |            |                     |

Consider: Are you relying too heavily on any single source? Are there gaps in your information gathering?

### Step 3: Analyze PESTLE Factors

Review significant developments from the past 12 months in each category:

- **Political:** Government changes, policy shifts, elections, political tensions
- **Economic:** Funding changes, economic instability, resource availability
- **Social:** Public attitudes, community dynamics, social movements
- **Technological:** New tools, platform changes, surveillance capabilities
- **Legal:** New laws, regulatory changes, legal precedents
- **Environmental:** Climate events, infrastructure changes, geographic factors

### Step 4: Connect Developments to Security Impact

For each significant development identified:

1. Could this impact your security directly or indirectly?
2. How might it affect your work, movement, or communications?
3. Did you experience any security incidents in the past year? How do they relate to these developments?

## Follow-Up Actions

- Identify gaps in your monitoring practices
- Establish regular intervals for repeating this analysis
- Share relevant findings with your team or network
