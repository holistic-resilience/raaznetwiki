# What to Do When You Risk Being Arrested

In some regions, prominent human rights defenders, activists, journalists, and lawyers must choose between going into exile or risking arrest and legal prosecution, often under unfair conditions. This guide, developed by the [Front Line Defenders Digital Protection team](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/en/programme/digital-protection), provides guidelines to help protect your activities, information, and contact networks by making it harder to extract data from personal and work devices.

> **Related resource:** If you are preparing to go into exile or travel in riskier situations, read the [guide on protecting your information and devices while traveling](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel).

---

## Assess Your Situation

### Talk to a Lawyer

- Assess your legal situation and likelihood of detention
- Know your rights, particularly regarding:
  - Whether you can be compelled to provide passwords
  - What authorities can legally access
  - Your rights during interrogation

> **Important:** If violent interrogation or torture are possible scenarios, it is better to have empty devices so you can provide passwords without exposing yourself or others to higher risks.

### Map Your Data

Take an inventory of important information across all your devices (phones, computers, external hard drives, USB sticks, SD cards) and online platforms (cloud storage, email accounts).

- Follow recommendations in [the Holistic Security Manual](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/exercises/explore/information-ecosystem.html)
- Create a digital inventory of sensitive information: pictures, documents, PDFs, chats, attachments, and login credentials

---

## Prepare for Potential Detention

Follow these steps in order:

1. **Delete** what you don't need
2. **Back up** what you want to keep
3. **Delete** backed-up content from your devices
4. **Disconnect** devices from online storage and email containing sensitive information
5. **Uninstall** apps for online services (access through browser only when possible)

### Back Up Important Information

- [Learn secure backup methods](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/)
- Back up to an encrypted external storage device and/or secure online platform
- If you continue producing content, repeat backups regularly and delete information afterward

### Minimize Data in Your Devices

- Delete all data (or at least the most sensitive) after completing backups
- Review the [safer travel guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel/) for device cleanup recommendations
- Consider keeping all data in a [secure cloud storage service](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/#encrypted-cloud-services)
- If you must keep files locally, store them in a secure, encrypted app

### Protect Against Malware

If you suspect an ongoing investigation, protect your devices against spyware surveillance.

- [Read the malware prevention guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/malware/)

## Prepare Your Phone

### For All Phones

- Avoid linking your phone to personal Google or iCloud accounts; create new accounts if needed
- **Disable biometrics** for unlocking—use at least a 6-digit PIN or 8+ character password
- Enable automatic updates
- Remove apps containing private information or connecting to sensitive online services
- Use your phone only for less sensitive purposes
- Read security guides for [Android](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/android/) or [iOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/ios/)
- **iPhone:** [Enable Lockdown Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/guide/iphone/iph049680987/ios)
- **Android:** Enroll in [Google's Advanced Protection program](https://support.google.com/android/answer/16339980)

### Recommended: Install GrapheneOS

For enhanced security:

- Purchase a Google Pixel (ideally [8a](https://store.google.com/us/product/pixel_8a) or newer)
- Have a trusted technologist install [GrapheneOS](https://grapheneos.org/)
- Create separate profiles (admin, work, bank, social media)
- Use GrapheneOS's built-in browser and PDF reader

## Prepare Your Computer

Follow security guides for [macOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/mac/), [Windows](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/windows/), or [Linux](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/linux/).

If keeping information on your computer, [encrypt your hard drive](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/secure-file-storage/#consider-encrypting-your-whole-device).

### Recommended: Use Tails

[Tails](https://tails.net/about/index.en.html) is an operating system that runs from a USB stick or SD card. When you shut down, no traces remain on the hard drive or RAM. Tails routes all internet traffic through [the Tor network](http://torproject.org/) for anonymity.

- [Install Tails](https://tails.net/install/index.en.html)
- [Get started with Tails](https://tails.net/doc/first_steps/index.en.html)
- [Create persistent storage](https://tails.net/doc/persistent_storage/index.en.html) for encrypted file storage

### Optional: Install Qubes OS

For protection against online attacks, consider [Qubes OS](https://www.qubes-os.org/), which isolates different compartments (qubes) within your computer. Configure it using the [SecureDrop guide for journalists and human rights workers](https://github.com/freedomofpress/securedrop-workstation).

> **Note:** Qubes OS protects against remote hacking but may not protect data from forensic tools during physical device searches.

## Review Your Password Strategy

Passwords are the weakest point of any encryption or secure service.

- [Use strong, unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
- [Use a secure password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- [Enable 2-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for all accounts
- Back up 2FA codes securely (e.g., [Proton Authenticator backups](https://proton.me/support/back-up-2fa-codes))

---

## Secure Your Communications

- [Read secure messaging recommendations](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat)
- Use apps with end-to-end encryption by default, ideally [Signal or similar](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications)

### Protect Your Connections

- [Browse the web more securely](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)
- Connect only through [a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/circumvention/#choose-a-vpn-service)

### Minimize App Usage

Access sensitive email and cloud services through a browser, logging out when finished. Avoid using dedicated apps to reduce traces of your activities on devices.

---

## Emergency Planning

### Choose Trusted Contacts

- Securely share passwords to sensitive accounts (including Google/iCloud) with trusted contacts
- Ask them to suspend social media accounts and remotely wipe your phone if arrested
- Compile a list of digital security helplines for your contacts

### Set Up Emergency Apps

- **Android:** Configure the [Personal Safety app](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9319337) for emergency actions (calling for help, sharing location, recording video)
- **iPhone:** Use [Emergency SOS](https://support.apple.com/guide/personal-safety/emergency-call-text-iphone-apple-watch-ips4f0cd709b) to alert contacts and share live video or photos

---

## During Detention

- Keep all devices **off** if possible
- Only turn on your phone to call trusted contacts (if permitted), then switch it off again
- **Do not unlock** your devices

---

## After Release

- **Do not use** returned devices until a forensics expert confirms they are clean from malware
- Be prepared to replace devices entirely—confirming infection may be impossible
