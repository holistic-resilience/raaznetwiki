# Protests: Report Emergencies Accurately

During protests or demonstrations, you may witness human rights abuses by authorities or opposition groups. Coordinating an effective response requires accurate reporting—something that becomes difficult when you're scared or distressed.

## Essential Questions for Emergency Reporting

When reporting an incident, answer these questions clearly:

- **Who are you, and where are you?**
- **What happened?**
- **Where did it happen?**
- **When did it happen?**
- **Who was affected?**
- **Who was the perpetrator, and how do you know?**
- **What do you need?**
- **How and when can you be contacted?**

> **Tip:** Practice these scenarios before the protest if you consider emergencies sufficiently likely.

## Responding to Arrests

If someone is arrested during the protest:

1. **Document immediately:** Record the place, time, arresting officer information, and any vehicles involved. Names or ID numbers are usually printed on police uniforms.

2. **Disseminate information:** Share details with affinity groups, human rights organizations, and media contacts.

3. **Conduct a roll call:** If the arrested person was part of your group, confirm everyone else is safe.

4. **Follow up on custody:** Confirm the detained person's location, medical condition, and any charges filed.

5. **Regroup and adapt:** Reassign duties and adjust your plan if you intend to continue the action.

## Preparation Is Key

If arrest seems likely, develop a comprehensive strategy before the protest to demand immediate release. Ideally, involve national and/or international human rights groups in your planning.
