# Protests: Be Mindful of Common Threats

Certain threats and tactics are regularly used against activists during protests. Being prepared for them—and staying aware of your surroundings—can help you and others stay safe. Share any indicators of changing conditions (such as shifts in police behavior) with your affinity group or the wider protest.

## Common Crowd Control Tactics

### Tear Gas

Tear gas is one of the most common "crowd control" methods used to suppress protests. The chemicals used vary by region and change over time, so effective remedies also vary.

**Preparation:**
- Research which type of tear gas authorities use in your area
- If possible, obtain a gas mask (avoid old masks, as they may be harmful)
- Swimming goggles can help protect your eyes

**Treatment if affected:**
- Rinse eyes with water (avoid warm or hot water with CS gas, as heat increases pain)
- Cigarette smoke may provide temporary relief in some cases
- Antacids diluted with water can help neutralize effects
- Diphoterine solution (a first aid product for chemical splashes) is also effective

### Water Cannons

Water cannons are frequently used against protesters and can cause serious injury at close range.

**Safety measures:**
- Stay alert for signs that water cannons are being deployed
- Move away quickly if police prepare to use them near you
- Maintain distance from the front lines if water cannons are present

### Mounted Police

Police horses are generally well-trained but can be unpredictable and may cause serious injury.

**Safety measures:**
- Never approach horses from behind
- Avoid standing or crouching directly in front of them
- Give mounted officers a wide berth when possible

## Monitor and Share Security Indicators

The security situation at a protest can change rapidly. Sharing and analyzing anything unusual that may affect safety helps everyone make informed decisions early.

**Best practices:**
- Maintain awareness of your surroundings at all times
- Designate a security person within your affinity group to monitor conditions
- Consider forming a broader security group within the protest to coordinate observations
- Communicate changes in police behavior, crowd movements, or other warning signs promptly

## Take Care of Fellow Protesters

Everyone has an equal right to participate in peaceful protests, including children, elderly people, and people with disabilities. While protests carry risks for everyone, some participants face additional challenges.

**How to help:**
- Offer assistance to those who may be at greater risk of harm
- Be aware of people around you who might need support
- Respect everyone's right to participate while recognizing different needs
