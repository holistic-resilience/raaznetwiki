# Exercise: Reflecting on Perceiving Threats

![Explore detail 08](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/media/exercises/images/default/explore_detail_08.jpg%3F1465900782)

## Purpose

This exercise helps you improve your recognition and analysis of threats to respond more effectively. You will learn to:

- Recognize your own blind spots in threat identification
- Identify missing processes for detecting threats
- Create strategies to address these gaps

## Prerequisites

Complete the [Threat Brainstorm](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/exercises/explore/threat-brainstorm.html) exercise before beginning.

## Format

Individual reflection or group discussion

## Reflection Questions

Work through these questions thoughtfully:

1. **Discovery**: Were there any threats you discovered (or that others mentioned) that you wouldn't have been aware of previously?

2. **Shared Awareness**: If working in a group, was anyone surprised by the threats you mentioned? Why?

3. **Timeline**: How long do you think the threats you identified existed before you became aware of them?

4. **Early Detection**: How might you have become aware of them sooner?

5. **Communication**: How do you communicate about threats with your group or colleagues?

6. **Severity Perception**: What makes certain threats feel more or less serious?

7. **Calibration**: Can you identify any threats that feel more serious than they might actually be?

## Tips

- **Don't rush**: Listing all threats you face can be overwhelming. Allow adequate time and space for processing.
- **Acknowledge emotions**: Create space for people to express their feelings throughout the exercise.
- **Make it routine**: If you find this exercise valuable, consider making it a regular practice (weekly or monthly).
