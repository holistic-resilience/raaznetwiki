# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before hitting record and share.

## Physical Safety First

Before you start taking pictures or filming:

- Ensure you are in as physically safe a space as possible
- Ideally, have another person record you while you film (and vice versa) in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries
- Plan this buddy system in advance with your affinity group

## Secure Recording Tools

When using a smartphone, several apps help you safely capture pictures or video:

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| [ObscuraCam](https://guardianproject.info/apps/obscuracam/) | Blur faces to protect identities |
| [CameraV](https://guardianproject.info/apps/camerav) | Control metadata in images or video |
| [eyeWitness to Atrocities](http://www.eyewitnessproject.org/) | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| [Mobile Martus](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.martus.android) | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |

## Secure Transmission

When sending recorded content:

- **Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted
- **Use end-to-end encrypted messengers** like [Signal](https://whispersystems.org/)
- **Use encrypted email** with [GPG encryption](https://securityinabox.org/en/guide/thunderbird/windows)
- **Use Orbot** to upload content while protecting your identity

If storing content on an SD card, consider encrypting your phone or passing the card to someone who can take it to safety.

## Publishing and Sharing Safely

Before publishing recorded violations, take measures to protect identities:

### Remove Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data that can identify where, when, and by whom they were taken. Remove this using:

- [Metanull](https://securityinabox.org/en/lgbti-mena/metanull/windows)
- [Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit](https://mat.boum.org/)
- CameraV (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Cover Your Tracks

- Use [Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/) on desktop or Orbot on Android
- Use a VPN service to access blocked websites

### Seek Expert Guidance

If concerned about safety and legal implications:

- Consult lawyers and human rights organizations
- Maintain secure backups
- Carefully consider whether to keep non-anonymized materials and how to store them securely
