# Exercise: Security Indicators in Daily Life

![Security indicators visualization](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/media/exercises/images/default/explore_detail_05.jpg%3F1465900376)

## Purpose

This exercise helps you visualize your daily routines to identify moments where you can establish a security baseline and check for potential threats in your physical surroundings.

## Materials Needed

- Drawing materials (pen, pencil, markers)
- Notebook, electronic document, or whiteboard for your checklist

## Instructions

### Step 1: Map Your Daily Routine

Use drawing to visualize your typical day. Consider these aspects:

**Morning**
- Where do you have breakfast?
- How do you commute to work (vehicle, route, companions)?
- What devices and items do you carry (phone, keys, wallet)?

**Workday**
- Where is your workspace and who else is present?
- What devices do you use?
- Where and when do you take breaks for meals?

**Evening**
- What time do you finish work?
- What route do you take home?
- What do you do before sleeping?

**Other Locations**
- Where else do you regularly spend time outside work and home?

### Step 2: Identify Security Checkpoints

Create a checklist of moments to look for signs of danger:

| Location/Moment | What to Check |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Your vehicle | Signs of tampering (wheels, brakes, steering) |
| Commute route | Dangerous areas; whether you're being followed |
| Workspace | Everything in place on arrival and departure; doors/windows locked |
| Home surroundings | Unfamiliar people, vehicles, or police presence |

### Step 3: Share and Verify

Share your checklist with trusted friends, neighbors, or colleagues to:
- Double-check your observations
- Identify areas you may have overlooked

> **For high-risk individuals**: Consider mapping the daily routines of family members or close associates as well.

## Adapting for High-Risk Activities

During protests, resistance actions, or documentation missions, situations can change rapidly. Create separate checklists for these specific activities, noting different moments when you should check for potential threats.

## Tips

- Drawing externalizes your routines, offering a fresh perspective on activities you might not normally view through a security lens
- If your work involves diverse activities, repeat this exercise for each type of work
- Keep your checklist accessible for regular reference
