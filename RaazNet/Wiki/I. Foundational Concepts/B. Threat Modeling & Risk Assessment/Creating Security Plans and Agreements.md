# Creating Security Plans and Agreements

Once you have clarity about the threats you face during your activities, you can begin organizing your security protocols into security plans or agreements. These plans can be formal written documents or informal shared agreements, but either way they should be considered "live" and subject to regular updating and review.

You can organize these plans according to any logic that suits your needs:
- By activity
- By geographic region
- By person
- By time period
- By any other metric that corresponds to your way of working

Regardless of how you organize your plans, all effective security plans share common elements.

## Core Elements of Security Plans

### Prevention of Threats

Preventative tactics aim to reduce the likelihood of identified threats occurring. Many will reflect strategies of acceptance, deterrence, and protection. They may include:

- **Public engagement:** Advocacy campaigns or outreach to raise consciousness and acceptance of your work
- **Authority relations:** Engagement with civilian and military authorities
- **Alliance building:** Strengthening ties with allies and supporters
- **Capacity building:** Tactics that build your own capabilities and agility

Once implemented, these measures become a normal aspect of work and personal life, though they may take time to adjust to.

### Emergency Response

Also called contingency plans, these are actions taken when a threat becomes reality. Emergency responses aim to:

- Lessen the immediate impact of the event
- Reduce further harm in its aftermath
- Deter aggressors from carrying out further harm

**Examples of simple emergency preparations:**
- Having contact details of a trusted lawyer readily available
- Agreeing on coded alert messages to send in case of arrest at a protest
- Keeping a first aid kit in the office for injuries

Even the best preventative measures may fail. Rapidly changing circumstances can lead to incidents despite careful planning. Having emergency plans in place allows organized responses that limit harm.

## Additional Considerations

### Well-being

Include tactics for maintaining your own well-being:
- Schedule adequate time for eating, sleeping, and relaxation
- Maintain a mindful approach to your work and security
- Remember that self-care elements are equally important as other security measures

### Device and Information Security

Consider the devices you depend on for your work:
- Develop tactics to control who has access to your information
- Secure your communications
- Regularly review device security practices

## Balancing Planning with Agility

Solid, up-to-date security plans complement resilience and agility—but don't replace them. In addition to detailed analysis and planning, you must be prepared for the unexpected.

While security plans are important tools, the ability to adapt and adjust when situations require is equally, if not more, important. Developing a sense of centeredness and calm is vital for situations that arise unexpectedly or could not have been planned for.

For groups and organizations, the planning process may involve additional steps to ensure group dynamics are not negatively affected.
