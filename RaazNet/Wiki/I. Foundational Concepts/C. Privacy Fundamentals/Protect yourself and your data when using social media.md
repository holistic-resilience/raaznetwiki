# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

## Why Social Media Security Matters

Social media platforms help us connect with others and spread important messages. However, these platforms often profit from our data by sharing information with third parties—including our buying habits, social connections, location, birthday, and other identifying details—often without us realizing it.

### Key Privacy Concerns

- **Data monetization**: When you use social media services, you agree to terms that allow companies to share or sell your information
- **Government surveillance**: Social media platforms are actively monitored by governments and law enforcement agencies
- **Metadata collection**: Authorities collect not just public posts, but metadata like when photos were taken and who is connected to whom
- **Legal requests**: Companies may be compelled to turn over private information about individuals, including human rights defenders and activists

## Platform-Specific Security Guides

While this guide covers major platforms, most social networking services behave similarly and offer comparable security options. We recommend reviewing your account settings on each platform you use.

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Private Group Communication

| Use Case | Recommended Tools |
|----------|-------------------|
| Small, secure groups | [Signal](https://signal.org/), [Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/) |
| Larger private groups | [Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/), [Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/) |

### For Public Broadcasting

| Use Case | Recommended Tools |
|----------|-------------------|
| Microblogging (X alternative) | [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about), [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) |
| Blogging | [Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/), [Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/), [Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html) |
| Newsletters & mailing lists | [Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists), [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists) |

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp

While Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and broadcasting, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure—both companies have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you choose to use them, review the security guides for [Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp).
