# Digital Security 101: Border Crossing Module

This module focuses on preparation and expectations for those traversing the U.S. southern border, with particular emphasis on journalists serving border communities.

Unlike most modules in the J-school digital security curriculum, this begins with a distinct perspective on risk assessment, emphasizing individual attributes (e.g., nationality) and destination when traveling between countries.

## Why This Matters

Reporters living and working near the U.S. southern border routinely experience elevated levels of surveillance. Because this is a fact of life in the region, learning practical steps to protect prioritized information at key moments is essential.

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, students will be able to:

- Identify information assets and physical devices requiring protection when crossing a border
- Develop a strategy for minimizing risk to data stored on devices
- Identify the most pressing risks to data from border officials
- Develop a plan for speaking to CBP officials in case of detention

## Module Content Overview

The session covers:

1. **Risk Assessment** - Emphasis on individual attributes and travel destinations
2. **Device Preparation** - Strategies for preparing devices for potential search
3. **Collaborator Logistics** - Working securely with collaborators during travel
4. **Border Screening** - Minimizing risk during the screening process
5. **CBP Interview Simulation** - Practical exercise using the provided handout

## Class Activities

1. **Olivia Rodrigo Case Study** - After watching a short interview with Jimmy Fallon, students analyze why the performer may have been pulled into secondary screening
2. **Family Feud-Style Game** - Identify physical objects and data assets requiring protection at the border
3. **CBP Interview Simulation** - Students simulate a screening interview between a CBP officer and a reporter

## Discussion Questions

- What kind of data would you want to remove from your device before crossing?
- Are there certain types of data that would be impractical to remove from your devices before crossing? Why?
