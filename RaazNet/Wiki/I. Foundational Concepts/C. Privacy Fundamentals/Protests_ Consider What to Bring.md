# Protests: Consider What to Bring

Think critically about what you bring to a protest. Items on your person can be extremely useful for preventing or responding to attacks and accidents—or they can be used as a pretext to harass you. Make these decisions only after completing your context and risk analysis, and coordinate with your affinity group to distribute resources according to each person's responsibility.

## Items to Avoid

**Electronic devices** without first considering what's stored on them. Is there sensitive information? Remember that your mobile phone can track your location—this has been used to identify and harass peaceful protesters.

**Anything that could cause legal problems**, including items that could be considered weapons, illegal drugs, or alcohol.

## Essential Items to Bring

### Health and Safety
- Any special medication you need
- First aid kit
- Toilet paper and sanitary products
- Swimming goggles and bandana or scarf (protection against tear gas)
- Antacid solution for treating tear gas burns

### Sustenance
- High-energy food
- Water for both drinking and washing wounds in emergencies
- Supplies to last longer than the planned protest duration

### Practical Items
- Some cash for emergencies
- Comfortable clothing that allows easy movement
- Camera for documenting abuses (optional)

### Communication
- If bringing a phone, consider a new SIM card and "burner" phone
- Pre-loaded airtime credit
- Spare battery
- Emergency contacts written on your arm or leg

### Identification
Coordinate with your affinity group on whether to bring ID. In some countries, not carrying ID could be a pretext for arrest. In others, there's no obligation to carry it and it may be used against you.

## Preparing for Searches

Authorities commonly stop and search protesters to prevent weapons or illegal items from being brought to protests. However, this is also often used as a pretext for intimidation or harassment.

**Before the protest:**
- Consult a lawyer about legal restraints on police searches in your jurisdiction
- Understand both the legal codes and practiced realities
- Prepare and practice verbal resistance to searches (where legally possible)
- Ensure you carry nothing illegal

**During the protest:**
- Alert your affinity group immediately if you encounter a search situation so everyone can stay alert
