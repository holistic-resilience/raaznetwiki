# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Facebook

This guide explains how to secure your Facebook account, control your privacy settings, and handle abuse on the platform.

> **Related Guides:** See also guides on [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) for other Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration:** Commercial platforms like Facebook may not share your values and might remove or filter content based on their policies. They may also struggle to understand local contexts, particularly if they don't operate in your language.

## Understand Facebook's Policies

### How Facebook Uses Your Information

Review these resources to understand what happens to your data:

- **[Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/182)** - Simplified explanation of Facebook's terms
- **[Facebook Privacy Policy Explanation](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/explanation/)**
- **[Terms of Service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms/preview)**
- **[Community Standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)**

> **Why this matters:** It's often unclear what platforms do with your information. Is your data combined with other data to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these risks. Consider installing the [Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) for quick overviews.

### What Facebook Shares with Governments

- **[Meta's Law Enforcement Guidelines](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)**
- **[Government Data Requests Report](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)**
- **[Content Restrictions Report](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)**
- **[All Transparency Reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)**

> **Why this matters:** Social media sites may share your information—including private information—with governments or law enforcement when requested. Understanding these conditions helps you make informed decisions about what to share.

## Avoid the Mobile App

**Recommendation:** Access Facebook through a web browser instead of the mobile app.

> **Why this matters:** Mobile apps can constantly gather information about you, even when you deny permissions for location, microphone, etc. Using a browser prevents Facebook from maintaining a constant connection to a device you carry everywhere.

## Create a New Account

You can [create a Facebook account](https://www.facebook.com/help/188157731232424) for free.

### Choosing Your Name and Identity

**Consider these factors:**

- **IP Address Exposure:** Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network connection and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- **Separate Accounts:** Consider maintaining separate accounts for different activities (personal vs. work at minimum)
- **Trust Networks:** The key to safe social networking is trusting your connections. Establish ways to validate identities, whether using real or consistent pseudonyms
- **Name Policy Risk:** Facebook's [name policy](https://www.facebook.com/help/1090831264320592) allows them to remove accounts using names not used in everyday life. Their algorithms sometimes fail to recognize legitimate names, especially non-English ones. If using a pseudonym, choose one that appears plausible

> **Why this matters:** Many people maintain accounts with alternative names to organize and connect with others while reducing risks to their safety or liberty.

### Use a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email on a [privacy-friendly service](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- If protecting your identity, set up the email using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)

### Don't Link Your Phone Number

If possible, set up your account using only an email address. Phone numbers can easily be used to identify you.

> **Why this matters:** Consider whether local law enforcement could use your phone number to find activities associated with you, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Skip "Find Friends" Features

- **Dismiss** requests to access your contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- **Only connect** with people you know and trust
- **Avoid sharing** employer or educational information—platforms use this to share your profile unexpectedly

> **Why this matters:** Social media uses your contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you need to keep your contacts hidden. Law enforcement might use these lists to build cases, or the platform might reveal your associations publicly.

### Designate an Account Manager

**Important considerations:**

- Facebook won't give others direct access to your account, but you can arrange for them to ["memorialize" your account](https://www.facebook.com/help/275013292838654/)
- For emergency access, share login information through [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is detained, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- See the [Digital First Aid Kit](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/) for additional guidance
- [Learn about accounts for medically incapacitated persons](https://www.facebook.com/help/480409628639043)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test the data download process before you need it. Understand what data you can retrieve, its format, and how to use it. This helps if you ever need to migrate to a different service.

## Protect Your Account

### Verify Recovery Information

- [Check your recovery email](https://www.facebook.com/help/162801153783275/)
- [Check your recovery phone number](https://www.facebook.com/help/124895950923762/)
- **Update immediately** if you lose access to either

> **Why this matters:** Attackers might change this information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising either could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your Facebook account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Recommended methods (in order of preference):**
1. Security key
2. Authenticator app
3. Security codes

**Avoid if possible:** SMS or phone calls—your mobile provider has full access to these, and they're easier to compromise.

- [How 2FA works on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/148233965247823/)
- Complete the ["How to keep your account secure" checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- See the [comprehensive 2FA guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa)

### Save Recovery Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://www.facebook.com/help/148104135383285) for situations where your usual 2FA method is unavailable
- Print codes and store them securely (like in a locked safe)

### Consider Advanced Protection

[Facebook Advanced Protection](https://www.facebook.com/help/1052552578831700) provides stronger security for high-profile accounts, including monitoring for potential hacking threats.

> **Note:** This feature is only available to people selected by Facebook. If eligible, you'll see prompts to enroll.

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- [Learn about suspicious emails and messages](https://www.facebook.com/help/225602007465207)
- [Check recent emails from Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/recent_emails/security)

> **Important:** Never click links in suspicious security emails. Instead, log in directly to your account and check the security page.

### Creating an Anonymous Identity

If you need to use Facebook without connecting to your real identity:
- Create your account through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use an [email address created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check for Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Use a unique password not used elsewhere
3. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

> **Why this matters:** Adversaries may log into your account from their devices. You can often detect this in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Enable Login Alerts

Get notifications when someone tries logging in from an unusual device or location.

## If Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Perform a security checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/review/?review_id=573933453011661)
2. Follow steps on [Facebook's hacked account page](https://www.facebook.com/hacked)
3. Use the [step-by-step walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/help/1306725409382822)
4. If standard procedures fail, check the [Digital First Aid Kit troubleshooter](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

### Review Connected Apps and Services

- **Avoid** using Facebook to log into other sites—it creates additional attack vectors and leaves more evidence of your online activity
- Use unique passwords for each site, saved in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- Be cautious when connecting accounts—you may be anonymous on one site but exposed on another
- [Manage privacy settings for connected apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/218345114850283/)

## Control What Information You Share

### General Privacy Settings

- [Choose who sees your content](https://www.facebook.com/help/325807937506242#choose-who-you-share-content-with)
- Complete [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264)
- [Set up regular Privacy Checkup reminders](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264#set-up-privacy-checkup-reminders)
- [Manage what others see about you](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/dialog/how-to-clean-your-profile?helpref=privacycenter)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**High-risk information:**
- Personally identifying information
- Images revealing where you live (e.g., photos of your house)
- Details about family members
- Sexual orientation or activities
- Email addresses (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Best practices:**
- Remember that trusted connections can easily copy and spread your information
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared
- Consider what you reveal about friends that they might not want shared

> **Why this matters:** The more you reveal online, the easier it becomes to identify and monitor you. "Likes" on pages opposing government positions have led to targeting for surveillance or persecution. Family members of activists have been targeted because of publicly accessible information.

### Location Privacy

- [Understand how location services work](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509/)
- If using the mobile app, turn off location services
- [Don't add location to posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509#adding-or-sharing-your-location)

### Photos and Videos

**Never post images showing:**
- Sensitive locations
- Identifiable people without consent
- Information that could compromise safety

**Management options:**
- [Delete uploaded photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/208547132518386/)
- [Control shared album visibility](https://www.facebook.com/help/410653545719324)
- [Delete albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/152153388188974)
- [Remove album contributors](https://www.facebook.com/help/589394671112683)
- [Remove metadata before uploading](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

> **Why this matters:** Photos and videos can unintentionally reveal information, especially in backgrounds. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and camera model.

### Likes and Reactions

- [Change "Who can see your social interactions alongside ads"](https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences/ad_settings) to "Only Me"
- [Understand how video interactions are visible](https://www.facebook.com/help/410629549531206)

> **Why this matters:** In some countries, "likes" or other seemingly harmless interactions have been used in legal cases.

### Birthday Information

You can register with a different birthday and change it later. Keep track of what you enter in case you need it for account recovery.

## Manage Who Can See Your Content

### Audience Settings

- Complete the ["Who can see what you share" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- Set "Profile information" to "Only me"
- Consider "Limit past posts" to restrict visibility of older content
- [Review your activity log](https://www.facebook.com/settings/)
- [Change who sees your apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/201750739897196)

### Comment and Reply Controls

- [Choose who can comment on Page posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/369765040737128)
- [Hide comments](https://www.facebook.com/help/297845860255949)
- [Stop profile posts from others](https://www.facebook.com/help/115469971891543)
- [Control Page visitor posts](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/356113237741414)

> **Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims about human rights defenders' associations. Controlling replies also reduces harassment.

### Groups and Connections

- [Control who can add you as a friend](https://www.facebook.com/help/217125868312360)
- [Choose who can follow you](https://www.facebook.com/help/201148673283205)
- [Check if groups are public or private](https://www.facebook.com/help/2580077045343981)
- [Learn about app access to groups](https://www.facebook.com/help/261149227954100)
- [Leave a group](https://www.facebook.com/help/172944012764072) (note: past membership in public groups may remain visible)
- [Remove or ban group members](https://www.facebook.com/help/
