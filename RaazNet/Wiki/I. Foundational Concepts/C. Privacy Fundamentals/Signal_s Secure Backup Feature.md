# Signal's Secure Backup Feature

Signal has introduced **Secure Backups**, an optional feature that allows you to back up and recover your messages if you lose your phone or need to reinstall the app. As with all Signal communications, backups are end-to-end encrypted—meaning the service operator cannot read your messages.

## Storage Options

| Tier | Storage | Details |
|------|---------|---------|
| Free | 100MB | Text messages + last 45 days of messages and media |
| Paid | 100GB | $1.99/month for expanded storage |
| Local | Device-dependent | Password-protected backup stored on your device |

## How to Enable Secure Backups (Android)

1. Open Signal
2. Tap the circle icon at the top left to access **Settings**
3. Navigate to **Backups**
4. Choose between **Secure Backup** (cloud) or **on-device backup**

> **Note:** As of the original publication, this feature was rolling out first on Android (beta), with iOS and desktop support coming soon.

## Important: Protect Your Recovery Key

Signal does not retain a copy of your backup encryption keys. You will receive a **recovery key** that unlocks your backup archive.

**Best practices for your recovery key:**
- Store it in a [password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)
- Keep a secure offline copy
- Never share it with others

⚠️ **Warning:** If you lose your recovery key, you will permanently lose access to your backup archive.
