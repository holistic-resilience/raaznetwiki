# TikTok Security Considerations for Newsrooms

By the end of 2021, TikTok reached one billion monthly users, becoming one of the most popular social media platforms globally. In the United States, the platform significantly increased its share of users who regularly use it to get their news.

Whether you're a journalist new to the platform or part of a long-time TikToking newsroom, understanding how to mitigate the potential dangers of the platform is key. This guide focuses on the most important security considerations—like account hijacking, data breaches, and privacy—and provides recommendations for navigating the platform securely.

## Understanding Concerns About the Platform

The success of TikTok isn't random. Like many other social media platforms, the algorithm that personalizes your experience is powered by data collection, analysis of interests and interactions, and profiling of user behavior.

### Key Concerns

**Journalist Targeting**: In late 2022, TikTok admitted they targeted journalists using the app to investigate their sources.

**Data Collection Transparency**: The company has been vague and evasive in explaining what specific information is collected about users and for what purposes.

**Data Access from China**: TikTok is owned by the Chinese company ByteDance. While the platform has stated that none of its data centers are subject to Chinese law, leaked internal information revealed in 2022 that U.S. user data has been repeatedly accessed from China. The company has confirmed personal data on TikTok users who sign up to make money through the app is stored in China.

**Clipboard and Keystroke Monitoring**: 
- A 2020 Apple update revealed the TikTok app was constantly accessing the user's clipboard content on iOS
- In 2022, research discovered its in-app browser includes code that can monitor user keystrokes on websites

> **Recommendation**: Use a trusted browser installed on your device (Chrome, Firefox, or Brave) instead of TikTok's in-app browser.

### Content Awareness

When creating content for TikTok, be mindful of sharing information that you don't want public or that could put you or others at risk, including:
- Information about your daily routine
- Personal data about yourself or your loved ones
- Source information

## Securing Your TikTok Account

### Use a Unique and Strong Password

Many people use short, predictable passwords, which introduces unnecessary risk to account safety. Regardless of whether it's a personal or institutional account, protect it with a unique and strong password.

**Recommendations:**
- Use passphrases (a sequence of random words)—they are harder to guess and easier to remember
- Use a password manager to generate and securely store your passphrases

**To change your password:**
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Password*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Password > Enter code > Enter new password*

TikTok will send you a 6-digit code to validate your identity before allowing the password change.

### Adding Two-Step Verification

While TikTok doesn't support physical security keys or authentication apps, we still recommend activating two-step verification. Once configured, you will be prompted for a 6-digit one-time code at login, right after validating your password.

**To enable two-step verification:**
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Security > 2-step verification*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Security > 2-step verification > Choose phone, email, or additional password (must select 2) > Select "Turn on"*

You must select at least two of the available methods (SMS, email, password) and click *Turn on*. Enter your password and click next to confirm.

### Law Enforcement Data Requests

Even with strong security measures, your data is still available to the company.

**Important considerations:**
- Direct messages are **not end-to-end encrypted**
- TikTok's law enforcement guidelines specify they can disclose user data to government agencies, including direct message content

> **Recommendation**: If you need to discuss sensitive topics that could put you or others at risk, don't use TikTok. Move conversations to apps with end-to-end encryption, like Signal or WhatsApp.

## Privacy Settings to Consider

Account security is essential, but it's also important to weigh the available privacy options with how you want to use the app. A newsroom account may focus on maximizing reach, while a freelance journalist's account may want more control over interactions.

### App Permissions

When you launch the app for the first time, it will ask for permission to send notifications and access your contacts. If you don't want TikTok accessing your contact list to recommend accounts, deny this permission.

**To manage permissions:**
- **Android**: *Settings > Privacy > Permission manager*
- **iOS**: *Settings app > Apps > TikTok*

### Private vs. Public Accounts

**Business Accounts** provide access to advanced features like statistics, post scheduler, and auto-messaging, but must be public.

**To switch to a Business Account:**
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Switch to Business Account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Switch to business account*

**To make your account private:**
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy > Private account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy > Toggle private account on*

### Controlling Interactions

Access privacy settings at: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Privacy*

**Key settings to configure:**

| Setting | Description | Recommendation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Activity status** | Shows when you're active or were last active | Disable if you don't want others to see when you're using the app |
| **Suggest your account to others** | Allows people to find you through mutual connections or shared links | Disable options you're not comfortable with (note: TikTok will still suggest your account to people you follow) |
| **Comments** | Controls who can comment on your videos and stories | Set filters for spam, offensive comments, or specific keywords |
| **Mentions and tags** | Controls who can mention or tag you | Restrict as needed |
| **Direct messages** | Controls who can message you | Set message filtering and read receipt preferences |
| **Following list and liked videos** | Controls visibility of your activity | Restrict as needed |
| **Downloads, Duets, and Stitch** | Allows others to download or use your content | Disabling may reduce reach and engagement |

### Account Deactivation

You can temporarily or permanently deactivate your TikTok account so no one can see it or its content.

**To deactivate or delete:**
- **Android**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Deactivate or delete account*
- **iOS**: *TikTok > Settings and privacy > Account > Deactivate or delete account*

> **Note**: Permanent deletion includes a 30-day waiting period during which you can reverse the request and restore your account.
