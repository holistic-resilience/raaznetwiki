# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Physical Safety First

Ensure you are in as physically safe a space as possible before taking pictures or filming. Ideally, have another person recording you while you film, and vice versa, in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries. Plan this tactic in advance with your affinity group.

## Secure Recording Tools

When using a smartphone, several apps can help you record safely:

| App | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| [ObscuraCam](https://guardianproject.info/apps/obscuracam/) | Blur faces in photos and video |
| [CameraV](https://guardianproject.info/apps/camerav) | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| [eyeWitness to Atrocities](http://www.eyewitnessproject.org/) | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| [Mobile Martus](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.martus.android) | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |
| [Orbot](https://guardianproject.info/apps/orbot) | Upload content while protecting your identity |

## Secure Sharing Methods

**Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted.

**Use encrypted alternatives:**
- End-to-end encrypted messengers like [Signal](https://whispersystems.org/)
- [GPG-encrypted email](https://securityinabox.org/en/guide/thunderbird/windows)

**Protect storage:** If storing content on an SD card, encrypt your phone or pass the card to someone who can take it to safety.

## Publishing Safely

Before publishing or sharing recorded violations, protect your identity and the identities of others in the footage.

### Remove Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data that can reveal where, when, and by whom content was captured. Remove this using:

- [Metanull](https://securityinabox.org/en/lgbti-mena/metanull/windows)
- [Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)](https://mat.boum.org/)
- CameraV (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Cover Your Tracks

When uploading or sharing material:
- Use [Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/) on desktop
- Use Orbot on Android
- Consider a VPN service to access blocked websites

## Legal and Safety Considerations

If you are concerned about the safety and legal implications of sharing gathered information:

1. **Seek expert advice** from lawyers and human rights organizations
2. **Maintain secure backups** of all materials
3. **Consider carefully** whether to keep non-anonymized materials, and if so, how to store them securely
