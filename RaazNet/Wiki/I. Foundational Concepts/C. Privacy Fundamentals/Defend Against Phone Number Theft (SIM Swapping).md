# Defend Against Phone Number Theft (SIM Swapping)

## What is SIM Swapping?

SIM swapping is an attack where criminals impersonate you to your phone carrier and convince them to transfer your phone number to a device they control. Because phone numbers are commonly used for account recovery and two-factor authentication, this attack can give criminals access to your financial accounts, email, and other sensitive services.

Attackers typically call customer service, claim they have a new phone, and request that your number be ported to their device. Once successful, they can intercept text message verification codes and break into your online accounts.

## AT&T Wireless Account Lock

AT&T has implemented a feature called **Wireless Account Lock** that allows users to disable several account changes, significantly reducing the risk of phone number hijacking.

## What You Can Do

### Enable SIM Swap Protection

Search to learn if your phone carrier supports features to disrupt SIM swapping:

- **AT&T users**: Enable [Wireless Account Lock](https://www.att.com/support/article/wireless/000102016/)
- **T-Mobile users**: Enable [SIM swap protection](https://www.t-mobile.com/support/plans-features/help-with-t-mobile-account-fraud) through their website or app
- **Verizon users**: Enable [SIM swapping protection](https://www.verizon.com/about/account-security/sim-swapping) through their website or app

If your carrier doesn't offer this feature, contact customer service to manually add a PIN to your account. Note that this isn't bulletproof—representatives can still override your PIN in some circumstances.

### Use Stronger Two-Factor Authentication

If your phone number can be hijacked, so can the 2FA codes sent via text message. Consider switching to more secure authentication methods:

- **Authentication apps** (like Google Authenticator or Authy)
- **Hardware security keys** (like YubiKey)
- **Passkeys** where supported

For a detailed comparison of 2FA methods and setup instructions, see Freedom of the Press Foundation's [2FA beginner's guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/).
