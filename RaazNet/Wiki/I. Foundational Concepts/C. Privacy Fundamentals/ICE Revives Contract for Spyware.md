# ICE Revives Contract for Paragon Spyware

*From the digital security training team at Freedom of the Press Foundation (FPF)*

## Background

In 2023, President Biden issued an executive order limiting government use of commercial spyware that poses "significant counterintelligence or security risks" or risks of improper use by foreign entities. This led to a stop-work order on a $2 million contract between Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and spyware vendor Paragon.

## Recent Development

According to an August 30 update in a U.S. government database, ICE has sought to lift the stop-work order. This means ICE may soon receive access and training on targeted spyware capabilities.

**Why this matters for journalists:** In January, WhatsApp linked Paragon to a hacking campaign that allegedly targeted roughly 90 users, including reporters.

*Credit: Independent journalist Jack Poulson first reported this story in his All-Source Intelligence newsletter.*

## Protective Measures

### Stay Current with Updates
Highly targeted spyware tools are used sparingly to avoid exposure to security researchers. Most people are more likely to encounter simpler malware, which can be mitigated by installing software updates promptly.

### Apple Users: Enable Lockdown Mode
Early signs indicate Apple's Lockdown Mode has blocked targeted malware. Available on macOS, iPhones, and iPads, this setting hardens security at the cost of some functionality changes (certain text messages and website graphics may be blocked).

### Android Users: Enable Advanced Protection
Google's equivalent to Lockdown Mode requires Android 16. Ensure your device is updated to access this feature.

### Seek Expert Help if Compromised
If you suspect device infection, contact **Access Now's 24/7 Digital Security Helpline** for assistance from security researchers who investigate these attacks.
