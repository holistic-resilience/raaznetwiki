# Privacy Guides: Independent Privacy & Security Resources

Privacy Guides is a not-for-profit, volunteer-run project that hosts online communities and publishes news and recommendations surrounding privacy and security tools, services, and knowledge.

## Why Should I Care About Privacy?

> "I have nothing to hide. Why should I care about my privacy?"

Much like the right to interracial marriage, woman's suffrage, freedom of speech, and many others, our right to privacy hasn't always been upheld. In several dictatorships, it still isn't. Generations before ours fought for our right to privacy. **Privacy is a human right**, inherent to all of us, that we are entitled to without discrimination.

You shouldn't confuse privacy with secrecy. We know what happens in the bathroom, but you still close the door. That's because you want privacy, not secrecy. **Everyone** has something to protect. Privacy is something that makes us human.

## Getting Started: Make a Plan

Trying to protect all your data from everyone all the time is impractical, expensive, and exhausting. But don't worry! Security is a process, and by thinking ahead, you can put together a plan that's right for you.

Security isn't just about the privacy tools you use or the software you download. Rather, it begins by understanding the unique threats you face and how you can mitigate them.

This process of identifying threats and defining countermeasures is called **threat modeling**, and it forms the basis of every good security and privacy plan.

## About Privacy Guides

Established in 2021 due to the difficulty of finding unbiased reviewers in the VPN and privacy space, Privacy Guides is the most popular, trustworthy, non-profit website that provides information about protecting your personal data security and privacy.

### Key Principles

- **Ad-Free Recommendations** — No advertisements or affiliate programs influencing reviews
- **Complete Editorial Independence** — Strict journalistic standards and policies
- **Non-Profit & Open Source** — Operates as a public charity
- **Community-Driven** — Crowdsourced recommendations and reviews

The team behind this project has been researching privacy and security in the open-source space for over 5 years.

### Media Recognition

Featured in WIRED, Tweakers.net, The New York Times, and many other publications as a reliable source for privacy and security knowledge.

## What Are Privacy Tools?

Privacy Guides recommends a wide variety of **privacy tools** spanning software and hardware that you can use to improve your privacy. Many recommended tools are completely free and open-source, while some are commercial services.

Switching from mainstream data-hungry software like Google Chrome and Windows to privacy-focused tools like Brave and Linux can go a long way towards controlling the information you share with companies and others.

## Key Concepts: Privacy vs. Security vs. Anonymity

Many people confuse the concepts of privacy, security, and anonymity. You'll see people criticize various products as "not private" when they really mean it doesn't provide anonymity. Understanding the difference between these concepts is essential for making informed decisions about your digital life.
