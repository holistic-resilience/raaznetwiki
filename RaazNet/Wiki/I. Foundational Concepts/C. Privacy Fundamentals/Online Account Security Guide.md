# Online Account Security

It seems like every week we hear about a [new data breach involving passwords](https://haveibeenpwned.com/PwnedWebsites), and as [frequent targets](https://source.opennews.org/articles/worthy-of-data-breach/) of digital attacks, journalists are at particular risk. Most online attacks, however, aren't fancy. Attackers most commonly try to access your accounts by:

- **Guessing short, predictable passwords**
- **Sending phishing emails** designed to trick you into entering credentials on malicious login pages

Fortunately, the defenses are also simple. The guides and resources below will help you learn how to protect information in online accounts.

---

## Core Security Topics

### 1. Phishing Prevention and Email Hygiene

One of the most common security threats journalists face is **phishing** — convincing you to enter your real credentials in a fake login form.

**Learn how to:**
- Identify hints of phishing attempts
- Implement simple defenses against credential theft

### 2. Two-Factor Authentication for Beginners

One of the best and easiest ways to strengthen login security is by enabling **two-factor authentication (2FA)**. This means requiring a second piece of information beyond your password.

**This guide covers:**
- The strategy behind two-factor authentication
- Common types of 2FA methods
- Step-by-step setup instructions

📖 [Read the full guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/)

---

### 3. Three Types of Passphrases

Publicly available data breaches reveal that many people use short, predictable passwords, introducing unnecessary risk to account safety.

**Learn about:**
- Three ways to create more secure, hard-to-predict passphrases
- Passwords composed of memorable codes and random words

📖 [Read the full guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/three-types-of-passphrases/)

---

### 4. Choosing a Password Manager

Most people use one or a small number of passwords across multiple websites. If one website suffers a password breach, hackers can reuse your password on other sites.

**Password managers help by:**
- Making it easy to generate long, unique passwords for every website
- Isolating breaches to just one website if a password is compromised
- Automatically filling credentials on web browsers and smartphones

📖 [Read the full guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)

---

### 5. Passkeys: Passwordless Logins for Beginners

**You can't get phished if you don't type in your password!**

Passkeys allow you to log into online services without a password by proving you have an authorized device.

> **Note:** Passkeys aren't supported everywhere yet, so classic two-factor authentication is still usually necessary. However, it's worth setting up passkeys wherever available.

📖 [Read the full guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/passkeys-beginners/)
