# The Dim Sum Guide: Digital Security for Chinese-Speaking Readers

**By Haven | Published 2025.02.12**

> **Access the guide:** [https://yummydimsum.github.io/](https://yummydimsum.github.io/)

Some countries experience more complex censorship and cyber-attacks against human rights defenders than others. People from China are one of the groups in need of contextualised digital protection. The Dim Sum guide, developed with support from Front Line Defenders, builds on Security in a Box content to provide specific instructions for Chinese-speaking readers. Ideally, both guides should be read together.

## Key Topics Covered

### Restrictions on iOS Devices in China

The guide addresses restrictions on iPhones and iPads in China, including:
- Lack of eSIM support
- FaceTime limited to video calls only (no audio)
- Heavy censorship of books, music, movies, and other content

**AirDrop Warning:** During the 2022 "Sitongqiao Protests" and "White Paper Movement," many people used AirDrop to share protest-related content. In early 2024, Beijing authorities were apparently able to trace mobile phone numbers and email accounts of senders. **The Dim Sum guide recommends avoiding AirDrop entirely.**

### Safer Chinese Input Methods

Chinese input methods are essential software for Chinese language users, but inappropriate apps risk exposing sensitive information. Research has shown that some Chinese input methods from Chinese companies have security issues or backdoors. The guide provides recommendations for more secure input methods across different operating systems.

### Secure VPNs for China

China has one of the strictest internet filtering regimes in the world. However, not all VPNs work reliably or securely—some reports indicate Chinese police have developed VPNs specifically to phish user data. The guide recommends secure censorship circumvention solutions, with ongoing monitoring and updates.

### Responding to Police Phone Searches

Following the "White Paper Movement" in late 2022, police in many Chinese cities have randomly questioned people to check for VPNs, Telegram, and similar tools on their devices. The guide explains how to respond to police mobile phone checks from both digital security and legal perspectives.

## Staying Updated

The digital landscape constantly shifts, and no resource can guarantee 100% security. The team continuously monitors and updates the Dim Sum guide.
