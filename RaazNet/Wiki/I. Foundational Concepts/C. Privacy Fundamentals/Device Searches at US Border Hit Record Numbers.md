# Device Searches at US Border Hit Record Numbers

*From the digital security training team at Freedom of the Press Foundation (FPF)*

## Record-Breaking Device Searches

According to U.S. Customs and Border Protection, device searches conducted between April and June reached an all-time high of **14,899 devices**.

### Types of Searches

| Search Type | Percentage | Description |
|-------------|------------|-------------|
| **Basic** | ~93% | Border official manually examines device |
| **Advanced** | ~7% | Uses forensic tools to copy and process device data |

This quarterly figure tops the previous record of 12,766 devices (January-March 2022) by **16.7 percent**.

*Source: [CBP Border Search Statistics](https://www.cbp.gov/travel/cbp-search-authority/border-search-electronic-devices) | [Wired Coverage](https://www.wired.com/story/phone-searches-at-the-us-border-hit-a-record-high/)*

## What You Can Do

### Prepare Your Devices for Search

Americans returning from abroad cannot be denied entry, but you may still face:
- Requests to unlock devices
- Delays and extended detention
- Device seizure for search

**Non-U.S. citizens face elevated risks**, including potential denial of entry or deportation.

→ [FPF Guide: Preparing Devices for Border Crossing](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/border-security/)

### Consider Your Online Presence

Your online posts and published reporting may be scrutinized before you arrive. 

**Case Example:** Australian journalist Alistair Kitchen was pulled from a customs line. A federal officer explicitly told him: *"It's because of what you wrote online about the protests at Columbia University."*

Depending on your citizenship, visa status, and reporting focus, this may represent an elevated risk.

→ [U.S. Press Freedom Tracker: Kitchen Incident Report](https://pressfreedomtracker.us/all-incidents/australian-writer-deported-after-interrogation-over-reporting-phone-search/)
