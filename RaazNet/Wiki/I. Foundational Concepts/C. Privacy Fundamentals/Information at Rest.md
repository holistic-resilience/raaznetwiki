# Information at Rest

Information "at rest" refers to digital files and hard-copies that are being *stored* on devices, paper, or other formats—as opposed to information actively being transmitted or communicated.

## What is Information at Rest?

### Digital Information at Rest

Digital information at rest includes all files stored on devices whenever they are not actively being transferred between systems:

- **Documents**: Reports, evidence, proposals, contact databases, accounting records
- **Communications**: Emails, attachments, text messages
- **Metadata**: Hidden information about your files (creation dates, author names, location data)

**Storage locations include:**
- Computers and laptops
- Phones and tablets
- External hard drives and USB drives
- SD cards and CDs
- Cloud servers and email provider servers

> **Note**: Portable devices like smartphones contain significant amounts of data at rest. While the *device* may be in motion, the *data* stored on it remains "at rest" until actively transmitted.

### Physical Information at Rest

Hard copies of sensitive information may be stored in:
- Filing cabinets and vaults
- Office desks and drawers
- Wallets and purses
- Shelves, books, and ledgers

## Key Questions for Your Information Audit

When assessing your information at rest, consider:

1. **Location**: Where is the information stored?
2. **Access**: Who has access to it?
3. **Sensitivity**: How sensitive is the content to you, your organisation, or people mentioned (e.g., witness or victim statements)?
4. **Necessity**: How important is it to keep this information?
5. **Retention**: How long should it be kept?

## Mapping Your Information

Use a structured approach to catalogue your most important information and its storage locations. This allows you to identify concrete protection strategies.

| Information Type | Storage Location | Who Has Access | Sensitivity Level | Retention Period |
|------------------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| *Example: Witness statements* | *Encrypted drive + locked cabinet* | *Project lead only* | *High* | *Until case closed* |

Once you have mapped your critical information, you can develop appropriate security measures for each category based on its sensitivity and accessibility requirements.
