# Protect Your Data When Using TikTok

Read this guide to increase your privacy and security when sharing and viewing videos or streaming content on TikTok.

> **Important Consideration**: Before relying on commercial platforms, consider whether they can adequately protect your information. Corporate products may not share your values, may remove content that violates their policies, and may struggle to understand your local context—particularly if they don't operate in your language.

---

## Inform Yourself About TikTok's Policies

### Understand How TikTok Uses Your Information

Review these resources to understand what happens to your data:

- **[Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/1448)** - Plain-language clarification of TikTok's terms
- **[TikTok Privacy Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/eea/privacy-policy/en)** - Select your region for applicable policies
- **[Community Principles](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/community-principles)** and **[Community Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en)**

**Why this matters**: It's often unclear what service providers do with your information—whether it's combined with other data, sold to third parties, or shared without your consent. You can also install the [Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see policy summaries for services you use.

### Understand Government and Law Enforcement Access

- **[Law Enforcement Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/global/law-enforcement/en)**
- **[Government Removal Requests Reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/government-removal-requests-2024-1)**
- **[Information Requests Reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/es/information-requests-2024-1)**
- **[Transparency Center](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/)**

**Why this matters**: Social media platforms may provide your information to governments or law enforcement when requested, including information you intended to keep private.

## Create a New Account

You can create a free TikTok account. While TikTok suggests using their app, **using a web browser on a computer is more secure**.

### Web Browser Setup (Recommended)
1. Go to the [TikTok home page](https://www.tiktok.com/)
2. Click **Login**, then **Sign up**
3. Choose **Use phone or email**
4. Select **Sign up with email** to avoid using a phone number

### Consider Your Identity Carefully

**Real name vs. pseudonym considerations:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for different activities (personal vs. work vs. activism)
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency so trusted contacts can verify your identity

**Why this matters**: Many people maintain accounts with pseudonyms to organize and connect with less risk to their safety or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

1. Create a new email on a [privacy-friendly server](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
2. Use [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address) when creating the email

### Avoid Linking Your Phone Number

When possible, set up your account with only an email address. Phone numbers can easily be used to identify you and may be accessible to law enforcement or harassers.

### Don't Grant Contact Access

- **Skip** or dismiss requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect with Facebook friends
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share employer or educational background in your bio—platforms may use this to share your profile unexpectedly

**Why this matters**: Social media uses your contact lists to recommend connections, which can expose your network to surveillance or unwanted discovery.

### Plan for Account Emergencies

- TikTok doesn't accept requests from family members to delete or memorialize deceased users' accounts
- Share login information with a trusted person using [secure communication methods](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance securing their accounts

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test downloading your data from TikTok to understand:
- What data is available
- What format it's in
- How you can use it elsewhere

**Why this matters**: Before storing data on any commercial platform, understand what it takes to move your data out if you change your mind.

## Protect Your Account

Review [TikTok's security recommendations](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety).

### Verify Recovery Information

- [Check your recovery email and phone number](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/email-and-phone-number)
- Update immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters**: Attackers may change recovery information to take control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising them could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your TikTok account gains access to information about you and your connections.

- Follow guidance on [creating strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)
- If prompted about passkeys, review [recommendations on passkey usage](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords/#consider-how-and-when-to-use-passkeys)

### Enable Two-Step Verification

- Use an **authenticator app** and email for verification
- **Avoid SMS** if possible—mobile carriers have access to these communications and they're easier to compromise
- See [TikTok's 2-step verification instructions](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety)

Learn more in our [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa).

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- [Learn to identify phishing messages claiming to be from TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/avoid-fraudulent-message-attacks-on-tiktok)
- [Additional guidance for TikTok for Business accounts](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/how-to-avoid-phishing-attempts)

**Key rule**: Never click links in security emails or provide your password. Instead, log in directly to your account and check settings.

## Use TikTok More Securely

- For browsing profiles, publishing posts, and chatting, consider using a **web browser instead of the app**
- To use TikTok without connecting to your real identity:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use an [email created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)
  - Don't add your phone number

## Monitor for Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Devices

Check which devices have recently logged in to your account. If anything looks unfamiliar:
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

**Why this matters**: Adversaries who access your account from their devices may be visible in your Security & permissions settings.

### If Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Reset your password and remove suspicious devices](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/my-account-has-been-hacked)
2. If you can't log in, try [recovering through friend verification](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety#3)

## Control Your Information Sharing

### Make Your Account Private

- [Set your account to private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Adjust visibility for individual videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/video-visibility)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**Never post:**
- Information revealing where you live (videos in/near your home)
- Personally identifying information

**Consider carefully before sharing:**
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Email addresses (consider separate accounts for sensitive vs. general use)

**Best practices:**
- Get consent before posting about others
- Agree with your network on what's safe to share
- Remember that content can be easily copied and spread beyond your intended audience

**Why this matters**: The more information you reveal, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. In some countries, even "liking" posts opposing government positions has led to surveillance or persecution.

### Disable Location Sharing

1. [Understand how location works on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok)
2. [Turn off location services](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#3)
3. [Delete existing location data](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#4)

### Share Videos Safely

- Review what's visible in backgrounds
- [Remove metadata from videos shot outside TikTok](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files) before uploading
- Videos can reveal location, date/time, and camera information through embedded metadata

### Hide Your Liked Videos

[Ensure liked videos are visible only to you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/exploring-videos/liking#4).

**Why this matters**: In some countries, "likes" have been used as evidence in legal cases.

## Control Who Sees What

### Manage Your Followers

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking problematic users](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/blocking-the-users)

### Control Comments

- [Choose who can comment](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#4) (select "No one" to disable all comments)
- [Turn off comments for specific videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#5)
- [Filter comments to require approval](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#3)
- [Delete unwanted comments](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#6)

**Why this matters**: Comments have been used to build false claims about activists' associations and for harassment.

### Manage Connections Carefully

- [Unfollow people](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/following-and-unfollowing)
- [Remove followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers)

**Why this matters**: Your connections reveal information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in court cases.

### Control Mentions

- [Set who can mention you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/messaging-and-notifications/mentions-on-tiktok#3): Everyone, People you follow, Friends, or No one
- [Report inappropriate hashtags](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/report-a-problem/report-a-hashtag)

### Limit Direct Messages

- [Learn about DM controls](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/controlling-your-dms)
- [Choose who can message you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message)
- [Filter, hide, report messages, or block senders](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message#3)

### Disable Personalized Ads

- [Understand ads personalization](https://www.tiktok.com/privacy/ads-and-your-data/)
- [Turn off personalized ads](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/personalized-ads-and-data/personalization-and-data)

**Why this matters**: Governments or police may purchase advertising data to target you with disinformation or locate you.

## Leave No Trace

### On Public or Shared Devices

- Avoid logging in from shared devices (internet cafes, others' devices)
- Never save passwords on public machines
- Delete browsing history after use
- Change passwords for any accessed accounts as soon as possible using your own device

### Clear Your History

- [Turn off post view history](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/tiktok-post-view-history)
- [Turn off profile view history](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/tiktok-profile-visit-history)
- [Delete watch history](https://www.theverge.com/23289267/tiktok-history-find-delete-how-to)
- **Delete search history**: Profile icon → three-line menu → Settings and privacy → Activity center → Search history → Select and delete

**Why this matters**: If your account is compromised or device seized, search and viewing history could be used against you.
