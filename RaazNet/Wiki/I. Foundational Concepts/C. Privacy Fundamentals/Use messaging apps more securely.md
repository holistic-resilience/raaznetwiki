# Use Messaging Apps More Securely

Messaging apps are widely used for real-time communications, allowing immediate exchange of text, voice messages, files, and enabling voice/video calls and group chats. However, many popular messaging apps have privacy and security issues that can expose your communications to significant risks—messages could be tampered with or accessed by unauthorized third parties.

This guide helps you make informed decisions about which messaging tools best suit your needs and how to use them safely.

> **Related Reading:** To assess how sensitive your communications are and whether a messaging app is appropriate, see the guide on [protecting the privacy of your online communications](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/private-communication/).

---

## Assess Your Needs

Answer these questions to determine which messaging apps suit your communication requirements.

### Basic Communication Needs

**1. What do you need to exchange?**
- Text messages only, or also voice messages, files, images, and videos?
- Do you need audio and video calling capabilities?

**2. Individual or group communication?**
- For sensitive information with individuals, most messaging tools work well
- For groups, verify the app supports group messaging and check maximum group size

**3. Technical requirements**
- Is the app available on all devices and operating systems you and your contacts use?
- Can you install apps, or do you need browser-based access?
- If you can't install apps, check if a [secure browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing) web interface is available

### Identity and Registration

**4. Registration requirements**
- Does the app require a phone number or email to register?
- If you need to hide your identity:
  - Choose tools that don't require phone numbers
  - Prefer email registration over phone number registration
  - Create a separate email address for registration
  - Consider decentralized messaging tools
  - Learn more about [anonymous chat options](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

### Security Features

**5. End-to-end encryption**
- For sensitive information, ensure messages are protected by default with end-to-end encryption
- Verify that group chats can also be encrypted
- Confirm voice and video calls support encryption

**6. Disappearing messages**
- For sensitive exchanges, choose apps that automatically delete messages after a set time
- This protects information if devices are lost, stolen, or seized

### Evaluating App Trustworthiness

**7. Key questions about any messaging app:**

| Consideration | What to Check |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Mission** | Was it created for secure communications or commercial purposes? What's the business model? |
| **Data storage** | What data is stored and for how long? Check privacy policy and terms of service for logging practices |
| **Legal history** | How has the provider responded to government data requests? |
| **Jurisdiction** | Where are headquarters and servers located? Will they comply with your country's authorities? |
| **Encryption software** | What encryption protocols are used? Is the software mature? |
| **Open source** | Are both the app and server based on free, open-source software? |
| **Security audits** | Has independent third-party auditing been conducted on both the app and server infrastructure? |

### Group Chat Considerations

**8. For group communications, consider:**
- Maximum number of participants needed
- Whether participants need to register or can join anonymously
- Required administration and moderation features
- Whether you need announcements-only or full conversation capabilities
- End-to-end encryption and disappearing message support for groups
- Video calling requirements
- Ability to organize conversations by topic/channel

## Use Messaging Tools More Safely

Follow these practices regardless of which app you choose.

### Register with a Separate Identity

- **Avoid using your primary phone number** for account creation when possible
- Create separate identities for sensitive activities
- Keep messaging apps separate from your usual email and social media accounts

**Resources:**
- [How to choose a username](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/#choose-a-name)
- [Creating and protecting multiple online identities](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/)
- [How to chat anonymously](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

> **Why this matters:** Your phone number is typically connected to your official identity. Separating it from sensitive messaging activities prevents activities from being traced back to you.

### Enable End-to-End Encryption

- Use apps that protect conversations with end-to-end encryption by default
- If encryption isn't automatic, manually enable it for sensitive conversations

> **Why this matters:** Anyone with access to messaging servers can read unencrypted communications. End-to-end encryption prevents unauthorized access.

### Verify Contact Identities

- Always verify contacts through a second, safer channel
- Options include face-to-face meetings or encrypted email

### Manage Message History

- **Use disappearing messages** for sensitive conversations—messages auto-delete from all devices after a set time
- **Regularly delete chat history** manually if automatic deletion isn't available

> **Why this matters:** Chat apps keep records of everything shared. Disappearing messages limit the information stored on devices.

### Handle Links and Files Safely

- **Suspicious links:** Don't open unexpected links
- **Unexpected files:** Contact the sender through a different secure channel to confirm authenticity before opening
- **Consider alternatives:** Upload files to secure storage platforms and share download links instead

> **Why this matters:** Malware and spyware infections often occur through malicious links or file downloads. Verification prevents phishing attacks.

## Use Group Chats More Safely

### Control Group Access

- **Assign admin rights only to verified contacts**
- Know who you're inviting to join
- If you can't remove problematic members, create a new group
- Verify new members through a different trusted channel (secure call, in-person meeting)

### Configure Member Privileges

Review group settings to control:
- Who can write messages and send voice messages
- Who can add or remove members
- Who can see member names and information

> **Why this matters:** Proper privilege configuration lets you use groups as peer chat rooms or announcement channels, depending on your needs.
