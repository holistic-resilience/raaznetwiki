# Data Brokers and Privacy Threats

This guide explores one of the most persistent threats to digital privacy: data brokers. These companies collect and sell consumers' personal information, often obtained by purchasing data from other businesses and scraping public records.

## What Are Data Brokers?

Data brokers are companies that aggregate personal information from multiple sources and sell it to third parties. That unsettled feeling you get when served an all-too-accurate advertisement? That's data brokers at work.

### How They Operate

- Purchase data from other businesses
- Scrape public records
- Aggregate information from multiple sources
- Sell compiled profiles to advertisers, government agencies, and others

## The Regulatory Landscape

While many Americans feel creeped out by relentless tracking, America's relationship with data brokers has been inconsistent—one step forward, two steps back. Recent setbacks include the killing of much-needed regulation.

### Government Involvement

The federal government has long been purchasing location data from brokers. This practice raises serious Fourth Amendment concerns and directly affects journalists and whistleblowers by creating a loophole that links data brokers to law enforcement.

### Positive Developments

- Some states are making progress toward closing the law enforcement data broker loophole
- Congressional pressure on brokers who hide their opt-out pages from search results
- Increased investigative reporting on data broker practices

## Protecting Yourself from Data Brokers

### 1. Understand the Threat

Learn how your data is tracked, bought, and sold. Knowing what's happening behind the scenes helps you right-size your defenses.

**Resource:** [EFF's guide on online behavioral ads and surveillance](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/01/online-behavioral-ads-fuel-surveillance-industry-heres-how)

### 2. Use an Opt-Out Service

Professional opt-out services can systematically remove your information from data broker databases.

**Options:**
- **Abine's DeleteMe** - Comprehensive but requires yearly subscription
- **Consumer Reports Comparison Guide** - Helps you find a service that fits your budget and needs

### 3. DIY Opt-Out Approach

For those who prefer a hands-on approach:

- Use [Yael Grauer's Big Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List](https://github.com/yaelwrites/Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List)
- Cross-reference against reports from any opt-out services you use to fill coverage gaps

## Key Takeaways

- Data brokers collect and sell your personal information, often without your knowledge
- Government agencies purchase this data, potentially bypassing Fourth Amendment protections
- Both automated services and manual opt-out methods can help reduce your exposure
- Staying informed about data broker practices is the first step to protecting yourself
