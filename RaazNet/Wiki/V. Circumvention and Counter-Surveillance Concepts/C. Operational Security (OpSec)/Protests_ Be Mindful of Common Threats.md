# Protests: Be Mindful of Common Threats

Certain threats and tactics are regularly used against activists during protests. Being prepared for them improves your safety and the safety of those around you. Stay observant of anyone who may pose a threat and share indicators you notice—such as changes in police behavior—with your affinity group or the wider protest.

## Common Crowd Control Tactics

### Tear Gas

Tear gas is one of the most common "crowd control" methods used to disperse protests. The chemicals used vary by region and change over time, which means effective remedies also vary.

**Preparation:**
- Research which type of tear gas authorities use in your area
- Obtain a gas mask if possible (avoid old masks, as deteriorated filters can be harmful)
- Swimming goggles can help protect your eyes

**Treatment if exposed:**
- Rinse eyes with water (avoid warm or hot water, which can increase pain from CS gas)
- Antacids diluted with water may provide relief
- Diphoterine solution (a first aid product for chemical splashes) can also help

### Water Cannons

Water cannons are frequently used against protesters and can cause serious injury at close range.

**Safety measures:**
- If you see police preparing to deploy water cannons near you, move away immediately
- Maintain distance from the front lines if water cannons are present

### Mounted Police

Police horses are generally well-trained but can be unpredictable and capable of causing serious injury.

**Safety measures:**
- Never approach horses from behind
- Avoid standing or crouching directly in front of them
- Give mounted officers a wide berth when possible

## Monitor and Share Security Indicators

The security situation at a protest can change rapidly. Sharing and analyzing anything unusual that may affect safety helps everyone make informed decisions early.

**Best practices:**
- Maintain awareness of your surroundings at all times
- Designate a security person within your affinity group to monitor conditions
- Consider forming a security team for larger protests to coordinate responses
- Communicate observations quickly to help others avoid threatening situations

For more on monitoring and analyzing security indicators, see [Security Indicators](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/chapters/explore/2-7-security-indicators.html).

## Support Fellow Protesters

Everyone has an equal right to participate in protests—including children, elderly people, and people with disabilities. While protests carry risks for everyone, some individuals face particular challenges.

**How to help:**
- Offer assistance to those who may need it
- Be aware of people around you who might be more vulnerable
- Provide support during evacuation or when crowd control tactics are deployed
