# Exercise: Brainstorming New Capacities

![Strategise detail 03](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/media/exercises/images/default/strategise_detail_03.jpg%3F1465900908)

## Purpose

This exercise helps you identify new capacities needed to maintain your well-being while addressing the threats you've previously identified and prioritized. By examining your existing capacities alongside your vulnerabilities, you can develop targeted strategies to strengthen your security posture.

## Prerequisites

Before beginning this exercise, you should have completed:

- **Exercise 2.5b**: Threat identification and prioritization
- **Exercise 3.5a**: Capacity assessment
- **Exercise 3.5b**: Vulnerability assessment

## Materials Needed

- Outputs from the prerequisite exercises listed above
- Writing materials or digital document
- Template from Appendix A (optional)

## Exercise Steps

For each prioritized threat, work through the following questions systematically. Consider documenting your responses using the format provided in Appendix A.

### 1. Protecting Those Under Threat

**Question:** What new capacities should the person or people under threat build to reduce the likelihood or impact of the identified threat?

Consider skills, knowledge, resources, and support systems that could strengthen protection.

### 2. Addressing the Threat Actor

**Question:** How might you influence the cost-benefit analysis of the people or institution behind the threat?

Explore whether you can:
- Improve their tolerance or acceptance of your work
- Deter them from acting against you
- Increase the perceived cost of their actions

### 3. Protecting Sensitive Information

**Question:** What information would be necessary for the threat actor to carry out an attack?

Identify ways to:
- Further protect sensitive information about your work
- Prevent critical information from falling into the wrong hands

### 4. Securing Physical Spaces

**Question:** How can you increase the security capacities of physical spaces around you?

Consider security improvements for:
- Buildings and offices
- Vehicles
- Private property
- Meeting locations

### 5. Well-being and Awareness

**Question:** What practices can you build to reduce stress and tiredness in order to be more aware and react more creatively to threats?

Focus on:
- Stress management techniques
- Rest and recovery practices
- Support networks
- Sustainable work habits

## Next Steps

After completing this exercise, prioritize the new capacities you've identified and develop an action plan for building them. Consider which capacities are most urgent and which will have the greatest impact on your overall security.
