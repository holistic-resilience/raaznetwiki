# Protests: Leave Safely

Leaving a protest safely requires the same vigilance as participating in one. The period immediately after a demonstration can present unique risks, including surveillance and identification by authorities or hostile actors.

## Key Practices for Safe Departure

### Maintain Situational Awareness
- **Stay alert** and continue monitoring your surroundings until you reach a secure location
- **Don't leave alone**—depart in pairs or with your affinity group
- **Communicate your departure** to a trusted contact outside the protest, including your expected arrival time (use a secure channel when possible)

### Counter-Surveillance Measures
- If you suspect you're being followed, avoid taking a direct route—vary your path to identify or lose potential followers
- **Don't linger** in the area where the protest occurred

### Handle Sensitive Materials
- **Protest materials** (banners, flyers, signage) may link you to the event—dispose of them safely or conceal them from view
- **Protect recordings**—photos, audio, and video should be hidden on your person or given to an inconspicuous "runner" who can transport them to safety immediately

## Post-Protest Recovery

Once you've reached a safe location:

1. **Debrief** with your affinity group to share observations and address concerns
2. **Transition psychologically** by taking time alone to decompress—consider activities like showering, changing clothes, exercising, or prayer to help shift out of the heightened state of the event
