# Digital Security 101: Border Crossing Module

This module focuses on preparation and expectations for those traversing the U.S. southern border, centering on journalists serving border communities.

## Overview

Unlike most digital security modules, this session emphasizes individual attributes (e.g., nationality) and destination when assessing risk for international travel. The curriculum includes:

- A [simulation handout](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1svEFIL5nyvD_phLcOQFm0WF2BAY_aV16vAfFk7Q_2og/edit) for practicing CBP interview scenarios
- Activities analyzing real-world secondary screening cases
- Exercises on protecting data and physical items during travel
- Strategies for device preparation and collaborator coordination

**Developed in collaboration with:** [Electronic Frontier Foundation](https://www.eff.org/), University of Texas at El Paso [Multimedia Journalism Program](https://www.utep.edu/liberalarts/communication/academic-programs/undergraduate/ba-multimedia-journalism.html), and [Borderzine](https://borderzine.com/).

---

## Session Details

**Estimated Time:** 80-90 minutes

**Prerequisites:** None

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, students will be able to:

1. Identify information assets and physical devices requiring protection when crossing a border
2. Develop strategies for minimizing risk to data stored on devices
3. Recognize the most pressing risks to data from border officials
4. Create a plan for speaking with CBP officials if detained

---

## Why This Matters

Reporters living and working near the U.S. southern border routinely experience elevated surveillance levels. Understanding practical steps to protect prioritized information at key moments is essential for journalists in this region.

## Activities

1. **Case Study Analysis:** After watching a short interview, students analyze why performer Olivia Rodrigo may have been pulled into secondary screening
2. **Asset Identification Game:** Family Feud-style activity identifying physical objects and data assets requiring protection at the border
3. **Screening Simulation:** Students practice navigating CBP conversations to lower legal risk through role-play exercises

---

## Discussion Questions

- What data would you want to remove from your device before crossing?
- Are certain types of data impractical to remove? Why?
