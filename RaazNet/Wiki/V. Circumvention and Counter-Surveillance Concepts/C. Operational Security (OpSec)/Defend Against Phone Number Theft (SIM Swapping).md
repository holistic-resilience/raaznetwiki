# Defend Against Phone Number Theft (SIM Swapping)

## What is SIM Swapping?

SIM swapping is an attack where criminals impersonate you to your phone carrier, convincing a representative that they have a new phone and need your phone number transferred to their device. Once successful, attackers can:

- Intercept two-factor authentication codes sent via text message
- Break into your online accounts (banking, email, social media)
- Commit financial fraud or steal cryptocurrency

Your phone number is extremely valuable because it's commonly used to verify your identity with financial services and other critical accounts.

## AT&T Wireless Account Lock

AT&T has implemented a feature called **Wireless Account Lock** that allows users to disable several account changes, significantly lowering the risk of phone number hijacking.

## What You Can Do

### Enable Carrier Protection Against SIM Swapping

Search to learn if your phone carrier supports SIM swap protection features:

| Carrier | Protection Feature | How to Enable |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **AT&T** | Wireless Account Lock | [AT&T Support Instructions](https://www.att.com/support/article/wireless/000102016/) |
| **T-Mobile** | SIM Swap Protection | [T-Mobile Account Fraud Help](https://www.t-mobile.com/support/plans-features/help-with-t-mobile-account-fraud) |
| **Verizon** | SIM Swap Protection | [Verizon Account Security](https://www.verizon.com/about/account-security/sim-swapping) |

**If your carrier doesn't offer this feature:** Contact customer service to manually add a PIN to your account. Note that this isn't bulletproof—representatives can still override your PIN in some circumstances.

### Use Stronger Two-Factor Authentication Methods

If your phone number can be hijacked, so can the 2FA codes sent via SMS. Consider switching to more secure authentication methods:

- **Authenticator apps** (Google Authenticator, Authy)
- **Hardware security keys** (YubiKey, Titan Key)
- **Passkeys** (when available)

For a detailed comparison of 2FA methods and setup instructions, see the [Freedom of the Press Foundation's 2FA guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/).
