# Recover from Possible Account Compromise

While it is rarely possible to know for certain if your account has been compromised, there are warning signs that can indicate tampering. This guide helps you identify these signs and take appropriate action.

## Signs Your Account May Have Been Compromised

Watch for these indicators that someone may have accessed your account without authorization:

- **Unexplained changes** to content or account settings you did not make
- **Messages sent** from your account that you did not write
- **Login failures** despite entering correct credentials
- **Missing messages** that contacts confirm they sent
- **Information leaks** where a third party knows details shared exclusively through this account
- **Password reset notifications** you did not request (successful or not)
- **Lost or stolen device** that was signed into the account
- **Suspicious activity logs** showing connections from unfamiliar times or locations
- **Security alerts** from your service provider about login attempts

## Immediate Response Steps

### 1. Stop Using the Account for Sensitive Information

Pause all sensitive communications through this account until you understand what happened.

### 2. Change Your Password Immediately

Create a new, strong password. See [how to create and maintain strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) for guidance.

### 3. Update Passwords on Related Accounts

If other accounts share the same or similar passwords, change them immediately with unique passwords for each.

### 4. Review Connected Device Security

Audit all devices that have accessed this account, stored the password, or hold your two-factor authentication method. See guides on [protecting phones and computers from attacks](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers).

### 5. Check Linked Services

Many services allow login via Facebook, Google, Apple, or Microsoft accounts:
- Search your inbox for "verify your email" or "account confirmation" to identify linked accounts
- Unlink services where possible
- Prioritize financial and sensitive communication accounts

### 6. Log Out of All Sessions

Most email and communication providers let you view active sessions:
- Review all logged-in locations
- Log out of unrecognized connections
- Re-authenticate on trusted devices as needed

See [basic security guides for phones and computers](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers) for platform-specific instructions.

### 7. Follow Provider-Specific Recovery Steps

Check [social media security guides](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/social-media/) for instructions under "If you think your account has been hacked" or "Protect your account."

## If You Cannot Log In

1. **Use standard password recovery** procedures offered by the service
2. **Follow the Digital First Aid Kit** troubleshooter: [I lost access to my account](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)
3. **Seek expert help** if recovery attempts fail
