# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Instagram

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Instagram account, protect your privacy, and handle abuse on the platform.

Also see related guides on [Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration:** Before relying on commercial, corporate products to protect your information, consider whether they align with your values. Commercial corporations may remove or filter content that violates their policies, and they may not understand your local context, particularly if they don't operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Instagram's Policies

### Learn How Instagram Uses Your Information

Understanding what happens to your data is essential before sharing it on any platform.

**Key Resources:**
- [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/219) - Simplified explanation of Instagram's terms
- [Instagram's Privacy Policy](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy)
- [Instagram's Terms of Use](https://help.instagram.com/581066165581870/)
- [Community Standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

> **Why this matters:** It's often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these practices. Consider installing [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see quick overviews of terms for services you use.

### Learn What Instagram Will Turn Over to Governments

Social media sites may provide your information to governments or law enforcement when requested, including information you intended to keep private.

**Key Resources:**
- [Meta's Law Enforcement Guidelines](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Government Data Requests Transparency Report](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)

## Create a New Account

You can [create an Instagram account](https://help.instagram.com/155940534568753) for free.

### Decide on Your Identity Strategy

**Consider using a pseudonym if:**
- You need to separate personal and professional activities
- You're involved in activism or sensitive campaigns
- You want to reduce the connection between your online and offline identity

**Important considerations:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider maintaining separate accounts for different activities (personal vs. work)
- If using pseudonyms, establish ways for trusted contacts to verify your identity
- Consistency matters—use the same pseudonym across related activities

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

For maximum privacy:
1. Create a new email address on a [privacy-friendly email service](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
2. Set up this email while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number

When setting up your account, avoid entering your phone number if possible. Instagram allows account creation with only an email address.

> **Why this matters:** Your phone number can easily be used to identify you. Law enforcement can request information from service providers using your phone number, and harassers might use it to locate you.

### Skip "Find Friends"

When setting up your account:
- **Dismiss** requests to access your device contacts
- **Skip** importing contacts from email
- **Decline** connecting other social media accounts
- **Avoid** sharing employer or educational background

> **Why this matters:** Social media platforms use your contact lists to recommend connections, which can reveal your network to others. Consider what might happen if law enforcement accessed these contact lists, or if your associations were made public.

### Designate an Emergency Contact

Plan for situations where you might be unable to access your account:

- Instagram won't give others access to your account, but they can [memorialize it](https://help.instagram.com/231764660354188) with permission
- Verified family members can [request account removal](https://help.instagram.com/264154560391256#removing-the-account) for deceased users
- Share login information with trusted contacts using [secure communication methods](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is detained, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance

### Know How to Export Your Data

Before storing significant data on Instagram, test the data download process to understand:
- What data Instagram provides
- What format it's in
- How you can use it elsewhere

This ensures you can migrate to a different service if needed.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Your Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Verify your recovery information](https://help.instagram.com/583107688369069) is current
- Change it immediately if you lose access to your recovery email or phone
- [Read more on keeping your account secure](https://help.instagram.com/369001149843369/)

> **Why this matters:** Attackers may try to change your recovery information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising these could allow password resets.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who gains access to your Instagram account can see extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Recommended methods (in order of security):**
1. Security key (most secure)
2. Authenticator app
3. Backup codes

**Avoid if possible:**
- SMS verification
- Phone calls

> **Why this matters:** Your mobile carrier has access to SMS communications, and these methods are easier to compromise. See the [2FA guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for more details.

[Learn how to set up 2FA on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/566810106808145)

### Save Backup Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://help.instagram.com/1006568999411025) for signing in without your usual 2FA method
- Store codes securely (password manager or printed in a locked safe)
- Generate these **before** you need them

### Consider Advanced Protection

If Instagram identifies your account as vulnerable, you may be invited to enroll in their Advanced Protection program.

[Learn about Advanced Protection](https://help.instagram.com/945221763907783)

> **Note:** This program is invitation-only. If you're eligible, respond promptly to enrollment prompts, as delayed enrollment may result in account lockout.

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- [Learn to identify phishing emails and messages](https://help.instagram.com/670309656726033)
- [Verify emails actually came from Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/emails/emails_sent/) through your account settings
- Never click links in security emails—log in directly to your account instead

## Use Instagram More Securely

**Browser vs. App:**
- The app is required for some features (like Stories)
- For browsing, posting, and messaging, consider using a browser instead of the app

**For Anonymous Use:**
- Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP address](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use an [email address created through Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

Regularly check which devices have logged into your account:
- Does every login look familiar?
- Are there unexpected locations or devices?

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Use a unique password not used elsewhere
3. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

### Enable Login Notifications

Set up alerts for login attempts from unusual devices or locations.

## Decide What Information to Share

### Make Your Account Private

- [Learn how to make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/517073653436611)
- [Explore privacy protection tools on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/811572406418223/)

### Avoid Sharing Sensitive Information

**Never post:**
- Information revealing where you live (photos of your home, neighborhood landmarks)
- Personally identifying documents or information

**Consider carefully before posting:**
- Details about family members
- Information about sexual orientation or activities
- Email addresses (consider having separate accounts for different sensitivity levels)

**Best practices:**
- Get consent before posting about others
- Establish guidelines with your network about what can and cannot be shared
- Remember that content can easily be copied and spread beyond your intended audience

> **Why this matters:** The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. In some countries, even "liking" posts opposing government positions has led to targeting and persecution.

### Disable Automatic Cross-Posting

- [Turn off automatic sharing to Facebook](https://help.instagram.com/797182937605779/#sharing-your-posts-automatically-to-facebook)
- [Unlink from other social networks](https://help.instagram.com/536741816349927)

> **Why this matters:** You may be anonymous on one platform but exposed on another. Connected accounts also create additional attack vectors for hackers.

### Turn Off Location Sharing

- [Learn how location works on Instagram](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/guide/location/)
- [Turn off location on iPhone/iPad](https://help.instagram.com/171821142968851)
- For Android, see ["Stop an app from using your device's location"](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507)

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Before posting images, check for:**
- Visible addresses, street signs, or recognizable landmarks
- Documents, ID cards, or sensitive information
- People who haven't consented to being photographed
- Background details that reveal location

**Additional steps:**
- [Remove metadata](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files) before posting
- Consider [making your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555) for personal photos

### Be Aware of "Likes" Visibility

[Your likes are always visible, even on private accounts](https://help.instagram.com/281388201973414). In some countries, likes and reactions have been used as evidence in legal cases.

### Don't Share Your Real Birthday

- You can register with a different birthday and change it later
- Keep track of what you enter for account recovery purposes
- Instagram doesn't share your birthday publicly—it's used for age verification

## Decide Who Can See What

### Control Your Audience

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://help.instagram.com/413012278753813) (they won't be notified)
- [Block users](https://help.instagram.com/426700567389543)
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555)
- [Remove public content from search engines](https://help.instagram.com/147542625391305)

> **Note:** If you share private posts to other platforms, [anyone with that link can see the content](https://help.instagram.com/442837725762581).

### Manage Comments

- [Turn off comments](https://help.instagram.com/1766818986917552)
- [Remove individual comments](https://help.instagram.com/289098941190483)
- [Delete multiple comments at once](https://help.instagram.com/289376615404536) (app only)

> **Why this matters:** Replies can be used to build false claims about your associations or to harass you. Disabling comments can reduce stress.

### Be Selective About Connections

- [Unfollow people](https://help.instagram.com/286340048138725)
- [Deny follow requests](https://help.instagram.com/207917546007234)

> **Why this matters:** Your connections reveal information about you. People may assume you support what your connections say or do. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in court.

### Control Mentions and Tags

[Manage who can mention and tag you](https://help.instagram.com/345505572238391)

### Limit Who Can Contact You

- [Control who can send DMs and add you to groups](https://help.instagram.com/296884305019942/)
- [Block accounts](https://help.instagram.com/454180787965921)

### Manage Advertising

- [De-select all ad topics](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/245100253430454)
- [Opt out of partner data for ads](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/2885653514995517)
- See also [Facebook advertising management](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook/#manage-advertising)

> **Why this matters:** Governments or police may purchase advertising data to target you with disinformation or locate you.

## Leave No Trace

### On Public or Shared Devices

- Avoid logging into Instagram on shared devices
- Never save passwords on public computers
- Delete browsing history after using public machines
- Change passwords for any accounts accessed on shared devices as soon as possible

### Clear Your Search History

[Clear your Instagram search history](https://help.instagram.com/354860134605952) regularly.

> **Why this matters:** If your account is compromised or device seized, search history could be used against you.
