# Situational Monitoring: PESTLE Analysis Exercise

## Purpose

This exercise helps you examine how external developments in society may impact your security. By systematically reviewing political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors, you can identify emerging threats and opportunities for your work.

## Output

A documented analysis of current situational factors and their potential security implications.

## Materials Needed

- Paper and pens
- Access to your regular news and information sources

## Exercise Steps

### Step 1: Assess Your Current Monitoring Practices

Reflect on how you currently carry out situational monitoring:

- What spaces do you have for discussing ongoing developments in society?
- How frequently do you review external factors affecting your work?

### Step 2: Identify Your Information Sources

List your sources of information for each PESTLE category. For each source, note:

- **Strengths**: What makes this source valuable?
- **Weaknesses**: What are its limitations?
- **Bias assessment**: Is it objective or does it have a particular perspective?

### Step 3: Analyze Recent Developments

Review significant developments from the past 12 months in each area:

| Category | Key Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Political** | Changes in government, policy shifts, elections, political tensions |
| **Economic** | Funding landscape, economic stability, resource availability |
| **Social** | Public opinion shifts, social movements, community dynamics |
| **Technological** | New tools, platform changes, surveillance capabilities |
| **Legal** | New laws, regulatory changes, legal precedents |
| **Environmental** | Climate events, infrastructure changes, physical security factors |

### Step 4: Connect Developments to Security

For each significant development identified:

1. Could this impact your security directly or indirectly?
2. If so, how might it manifest as a threat or opportunity?
3. Did you experience any security incidents in the past year? How do they relate to these broader developments?

## Follow-Up

Consider scheduling regular PESTLE reviews (quarterly or biannually) to maintain situational awareness and update your security planning accordingly.
