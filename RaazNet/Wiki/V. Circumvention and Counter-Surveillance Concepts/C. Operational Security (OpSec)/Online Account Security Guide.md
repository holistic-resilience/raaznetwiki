# Online Account Security

It seems like every week we hear about a [new data breach involving passwords](https://haveibeenpwned.com/PwnedWebsites), and as [frequent targets](https://source.opennews.org/articles/worthy-of-data-breach/) of digital attacks, journalists are at particular risk. Most online attacks, however, aren't fancy. Attackers most commonly try to access your accounts by guessing short, predictable passwords, or by sending *phishing* emails designed to trick you into sending them your credentials in a malicious login webpage.

Fortunately, the defenses are also simple. The following guides and resources will help you learn how to protect information in online accounts.

## Core Security Guides

### Phishing Prevention and Email Hygiene

One of the most common security threats journalists face is phishing—convincing you to enter your real credentials in a fake login form. Learn how to identify hints of phishing and implement simple defenses.

**Resource:** [Phishing Prevention and Email Hygiene](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/email-security-tips/)

### Two-Factor Authentication for Beginners

One of the best and easiest ways to strengthen login security is by enabling two-factor authentication (2FA). This means requiring a second piece of information beyond your password. This guide describes the strategy behind two-factor authentication, common 2FA methods, and step-by-step setup instructions.

**Resource:** [Two-Factor Authentication for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/2fa-beginners/)

### Three Types of Passphrases

We know from publicly available data breaches that many people use short, predictable passwords, introducing unnecessary risk to account safety. This guide introduces three ways to create more secure, hard-to-predict *passphrases*—passwords composed of memorable codes and random words.

**Resource:** [Three Types of Passphrases](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/three-types-of-passphrases/)

### Choosing a Password Manager

Most people use one or a small number of passwords across multiple websites. If one website suffers a password breach, hackers can reuse your password on other websites as well. Password managers solve this problem by making it easy to generate long, unique passwords for every website, isolating any breach to just one account. Many password managers also automatically fill credentials in web browsers and smartphones, making sign-in faster, easier, and more secure.

**Resource:** [Choosing a Password Manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)

### Passkeys: Passwordless Logins for Beginners

You can't get phished if you don't type in your password! This guide explains the "why" and "how" of using passkeys, which allow you to log into online services by proving you have an authorized device. Passkeys aren't supported everywhere yet, so classic two-factor authentication is still usually necessary, but it's worth setting up wherever available.

**Resource:** [Passkeys: Passwordless Logins for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/passkeys-beginners/)
