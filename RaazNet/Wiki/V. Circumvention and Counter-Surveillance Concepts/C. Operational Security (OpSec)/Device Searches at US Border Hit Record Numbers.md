# Device Searches at US Border Hit Record Numbers

According to U.S. Customs and Border Protection, the number of device searches the agency conducted between April and June 2024 reached an all-time high. CBP searched **14,899 devices** during this period.

## Types of Searches

- **Basic searches (~93%)**: A border official manually examines a device
- **Advanced searches (~7%)**: Officials use more invasive forensic tools designed to copy and process data on mobile devices

This quarterly figure tops the previous record of 12,766 devices searched (January-March 2022) by 16.7 percent.

**Source**: [Wired - Phone Searches at the US Border Hit a Record High](https://www.wired.com/story/phone-searches-at-the-us-border-hit-a-record-high/)

## What You Can Do

### Prepare Your Devices for Search

Americans returning to the U.S. from abroad cannot be denied entry, but you may still be asked to unlock your devices. Regardless of whether you comply, you risk:

- Delays at the border
- Extended detention
- Having your device seized for search

**Non-U.S. citizens face additional risks**, including potential denial of entry or deportation.

**Resource**: [FPF Guide on Preparing Devices for International Travel](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/border-security/)

### Consider Your Online Presence

Your online posts and reporting may be scrutinized ahead of a visit. Australian journalist Alistair Kitchen was pulled from a customs line, with a federal officer explicitly stating: *"It's because of what you wrote online about the protests at Columbia University."*

Depending on your citizenship, visa status, and the nature of your reporting, this may represent an elevated risk.

**Resource**: [US Press Freedom Tracker - Australian Writer Deported After Interrogation](https://pressfreedomtracker.us/all-incidents/australian-writer-deported-after-interrogation-over-reporting-phone-search/)
