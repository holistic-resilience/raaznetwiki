# Protests: Communicate Often and Safely

For a successful protest, establish a clear communication plan for monitoring developments, coordinating actions, and responding to emergencies. Unexpected situations require clarity about who to contact, when, and how.

## Establish an External Contact

You or a member of your affinity group should maintain regular communication with a trusted contact outside the protest who can raise the alarm if something happens.

**With your external contact:**
- Organize scheduled check-in times
- Share security indicators (code words or phrases)
- Agree on protocols if something goes wrong
- Consider routing communication through civil society organizations or human rights groups when necessary

## Emergency Contact Preparation

Create a list of people and organizations you can contact in emergencies, including:
- A lawyer familiar with protest-related legal issues
- Trusted friends or family members
- Legal support hotlines
- Human rights organizations

**Storage tips:**
- If storing contacts on your mobile, consider using codenames
- Carry a written backup copy in case you lose your phone, leave it behind, or run out of battery
- Write the most critical numbers on your arms or legs in case possessions are lost or confiscated

## Communication Security Awareness

Mobile phone calls, SMS messages, and many common messenger applications are easy to intercept. Authorities and other adversaries increasingly surveil communication channels during protests.

**Key considerations:**
- Simply having your mobile device at the protest can identify you and be used against you
- If you use mobile phones, prioritize end-to-end encrypted messaging and calling apps
