# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

We use social media platforms to connect with others and spread messages we find important. However, the companies managing these platforms often have business models based on monetizing our communications and contacts. They share information about us with third parties—including our buying habits, social connections, location, birthday, and other identifying information—often without our full awareness.

When you use their services, you agree to terms that permit them to share or sell this information. Sometimes it's unclear just how public that information becomes.

## Why This Matters

Social media platforms are actively monitored by governments and law enforcement agencies. Authorities collect publicly available data and, when possible, gather **metadata** that we may not consider—such as when a photo was taken or who is connected to whom. They may also request that social media companies turn over private information about individuals of interest, often including human rights defenders or activists.

> **Note:** While this guide provides instructions for specific platforms, many social networking services behave similarly and offer comparable privacy options. We recommend reviewing your account settings and taking protective steps on each of your social media accounts.

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Small Group Organizing
- **[Signal](https://signal.org/)** or **[Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/)** — Secure messaging apps ideal for carefully chosen groups

### For Larger Private Groups
- **[Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/)** — Self-hosted team communication
- **[Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/)** — Open-source team collaboration platform

### For Public Broadcasting (X/Twitter Alternatives)
- **[Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about)** — Decentralized social network
- **[Bluesky](https://bsky.app/)** — Alternative microblogging platform
- See also: [Our blog post on leaving X](https://securityinabox.org/en/blog/x-odus)

### For Publishing Content
- **[Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/)** — Blogging platform by [Autistici/Inventati](https://autistici.org/)
- **[Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/)** — Alternative blogging platform
- **[Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html)** — Privacy-focused hosting

### For Mailing Lists and Newsletters
- **[Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists)** — Activist-focused mailing lists
- **[Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)** — Mailing lists and newsletters

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp
While Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and broadcasting, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure—both companies have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you use them, consult our guides on **[Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram)** and **[WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp)** security.
