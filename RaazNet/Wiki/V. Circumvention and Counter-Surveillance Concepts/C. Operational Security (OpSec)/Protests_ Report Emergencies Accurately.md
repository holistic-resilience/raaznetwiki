# Protests: Report Emergencies Accurately

During protests or demonstrations, witnesses may observe human rights abuses by authorities or opposing groups. Accurate reporting is essential for coordinating an effective response. This can be particularly challenging when experiencing fear or distress.

## Essential Information to Report

When reporting an emergency, gather and communicate the following details:

- **Your identity and location** — Who are you, and where are you?
- **What happened** — Describe the incident clearly
- **Location of incident** — Where exactly did it occur?
- **Time of incident** — When did it happen?
- **Affected individuals** — Who was impacted?
- **Perpetrator identification** — Who was responsible, and how do you know?
- **Immediate needs** — What assistance is required?
- **Contact information** — How and when can you be reached?

> **Tip:** Practice these reporting scenarios before the protest if you consider emergencies sufficiently likely.

## Responding to Arrests

If someone is arrested during the protest:

1. **Document arrest details immediately:**
   - Time and location of arrest
   - Arresting officer information (name, badge/ID number—usually printed on uniforms)
   - Vehicle descriptions and license plates

2. **Disseminate information** to affinity groups, human rights organizations, and media contacts

3. **Conduct a roll call** if the arrested person was part of your affinity group to confirm everyone else is safe

4. **Follow up on custody details:**
   - Confirm detention location
   - Inquire about medical condition
   - Determine charges filed

5. **Regroup and adapt** your plan, reassigning duties if you intend to continue the action

> **Important:** If arrest is likely, define a comprehensive release strategy before the protest, ideally involving national and/or international human rights organizations.
