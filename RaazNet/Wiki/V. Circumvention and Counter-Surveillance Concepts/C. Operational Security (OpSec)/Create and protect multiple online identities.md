# Create and Protect Multiple Online Identities

This guide helps you reflect on why you may want to create alternative online identities and provides methods to secure these identities, making it difficult to connect them to your private life or other online personas.

## Develop Your Strategy

Strategies for maintaining separate identities range from full transparency to full anonymity. Your choice depends on your risk level, situation, needs, and goals.

### Real Identity

Publishing content under your official name makes sense when your identity provides credibility and trust. Being transparent doesn't mean exposing your entire private life—you can use your legal name for professional work while using pseudonyms for personal social media and private communications.

**Key principle:** Always apply basic practices to keep your public and private identities separate.

### Persistent Pseudonymity

Using an alternative name consistently over time allows you to:
- Mitigate threats like political or economic retribution
- Develop an online reputation connected to your alternative name
- Build trust in online communities

You can maintain multiple pseudonyms for different purposes (activism, hobbies, etc.). Alternative identities can also be managed collectively, though this requires strong coordination and security measures.

### Anonymity

Total online anonymity is difficult to achieve but essential in high-risk situations. Unlike persistent pseudonymity, complete anonymity involves using short-lived identities to achieve specific goals without leaving traces—for example, a whistleblower communicating with journalists.

**Warning:** In some countries, authorities may interpret anonymization efforts as suspicious behavior. Consider whether alternative approaches might achieve your goals.

Learn more: [Anonymizing your connections, messages, and file sharing](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity)

### Comparing Strategies

| Strategy | Risk to You | Reputation Building | Effort Required |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Real identity | Higher | Strong | Low |
| Persistent pseudonymity | Lower | Moderate | Moderate |
| Anonymity | Lowest | Minimal | High |

## Check What Information Exists About You Online

Before creating alternative identities, research what the internet reveals about you—an activity called "self-doxing."

**Resources:**
- [Access Now Helpline's guide to exploring your online traces](https://guides.accessnow.org/self-doxing.html)
- [New York Times guide to finding and removing personal information](https://open.nytimes.com/how-to-dox-yourself-on-the-internet-d2892b4c5954)

**Why this matters:** Malicious actors gather information from all available sources to identify targets. Self-doxing reveals what information exists so you can delete it before it's used against you.

## Remove Unwanted Information and Old Identities

If you find images, videos, or information that could connect your identities:

1. **Document everything** following [Digital First Aid Kit guidelines](https://digitalfirstaid.org/documentation/)
2. **File takedown requests:**
   - Request Google to [remove personal contact information from search results](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/12719076?hl=en)
   - EU residents can request removal of results containing their name

**Why this matters:** Long-term identity use creates information that can be used for profiling or attacks. Consider all identities as potentially disposable—remove old information when necessary.

## Map Your Social Domains

Before creating multiple identities, map your social domains to identify overlaps that could lead to cross-domain attacks.

**Process:**
1. List your different activities and networks (online and offline)
2. Assess the sensitivity of each domain
3. Identify which sensitive domains need separation from other networks
4. Develop online identities to maintain that separation

**Why this matters:** Everyone belongs to multiple social domains—workplace, activist groups, family, friends, hobbies. We often secure some networks more than others. Intersections between domains create vulnerabilities. For example, if an activist discusses hobbies publicly, attackers might use that interest to deliver malware through seemingly innocent hobby-related content.

## Create and Secure an Alternative Identity

### Choose a Name

- Select a name that platforms will find credible (especially Facebook, which requires "everyday" names)
- Research thoroughly to ensure no one else uses that name
- Consider the risk of account suspension when using pseudonyms

### Get a Different Phone Number

Phone numbers often link to official identity documents. Options include:
- Pre-paid SIM cards (in countries allowing anonymous purchase)
- Online calling services: [Hushed](https://hushed.com/), [Google Voice](https://voice.google.com/about), or [Twilio](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/phone-numbers)

### Hide Your IP Address

To prevent ISPs and platforms from linking your official identity to alternative identities:
- Use a [trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/circumvention) (remember: VPN providers can see your activity)
- For maximum protection, [anonymize connections with Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity)

**Important:** Hide your IP both when creating accounts and when using them.

### Create a New Email Account

Register a new email account for your alternative identity. Options include:
- New accounts with privacy-focused providers
- Email aliases
- Disposable email services (for temporary needs)

Learn more: [Guide on safe email](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-email)

## Keep Your Identities Separate

### Basic Practices

- Use private profiles for personal details, accessible only to selected contacts
- Consider whether shared information could identify or endanger someone
- Ask permission before writing about individuals
- Post event information only after events conclude
- Use [strong, unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) and [two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for every account
- [Reduce tracking when browsing](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)

### Remove Identifying Information from Files

Delete metadata from images, videos, and files before uploading. Learn more: [Removing identifying data](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/)

### Use Different Accounts and Platforms

Each identity should have separate accounts registered through different:
- IP addresses
- Phone numbers
- Email addresses

### Advanced Separation Practices

- Create accounts using the device, browser, or app designated for that persona
- Review privacy settings for each account
- Be cautious with profile information—profile pictures and cover photos are typically public
- Avoid providing excessive biographical details; if creating a backstory, make it distinct from your real life
- **Don't let your identities follow each other** or share the same contacts
- Avoid reposting or liking content between accounts
- Disable location services and check settings on photo/video sharing sites
- Consider scheduling posts at different times for different identities

## Compartmentalize Using Different Devices or Environments

### Computer Options

**Different devices:** Use separate computers for each identity (must receive security updates)

**Bootable USB systems:**
- [Tails](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tails): Runs from USB, connects via Tor, leaves no trace
- [Ubuntu live USB with persistence](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LiveUsbPendrivePersistent): Keep USB secure as it's not encrypted by default

**Virtual machines and sandboxing:**
- [Qubes OS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/tools/#qubes-os): Security-focused OS with isolated compartments
- [Whonix](https://www.whonix.org/): Anonymous VM routing all traffic through Tor
- [VirtualBox](https://www.virtualbox.org/), [VMware](https://www.vmware.com/products/desktop-hypervisor/workstation-and-fusion), or [KVM](https://www.redhat.com/en/topics/virtualization/what-is-KVM): Standard VMs (resource-intensive, doesn't change IP)
- [Sandboxie](https://sandboxie-plus.com/) (Windows): Lighter sandboxing option

**User accounts:** Create different user accounts for each identity (less protection against sophisticated attacks)

**Browser separation:**
- Use different browsers for different identities
- Use browser profiles within the same browser
- Install extensions like [Firefox Multi-Account Containers](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/multi-account-containers/) or [SessionBox](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/sessionbox-multi-login-to/megbklhjamjbcafknkgmokldgolkdfig?hl=en)

### Phone Options

**Different devices:** Dedicate separate phones to each identity (must receive security updates)

**Android user accounts:** Create different user accounts on tablets or phones

**Android sandboxing:** [Shelter](https://f-droid.org/packages/net.typeblog.shelter/) (F-Droid only) compartmentalizes identities in Android work profile

**Browser separation:** Use [Tor Browser](https://www.torproject.org/) on Android or [Onion Browser](https://onionbrowser.com/) on iOS for alternative identities

**Why compartmentalization matters:** Separating identities across devices, profiles, or sandboxes prevents human errors (like responding from the wrong account) and makes it harder to connect identities through device or browser fingerprinting.

## Use Anonymous Payment Methods

For online purchases under your pseudonymous identity:
- Cash-purchased prepaid cards from supermarkets
- Virtual credit cards from banks offering identity protection
- Cryptocurrencies (more complex to manage)

## Manage Identities in Physical Spaces

If your alternative identity involves offline activities:

- Consider disguises, different looks, or masks for public appearances
- Only show your face in trusted spaces where photography is prohibited
- Remember that routes to trusted spaces may have surveillance cameras
- Create disposable contact identities for new acquaintances until you establish trust

## Advanced: Create an Anonymous Website

To create a website that cannot be connected to your identity:

1. Check whether your domain registrar offers privacy protection
2. Look for registrars offering free privacy protection
3. For maximum anonymity, register through [Njalla](https://njal.la/)—an anonymous registrar accepting encrypted requests and cryptocurrency payments

**Note:** Some top-level domains [don't support privacy protection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_privacy#Privacy_forbidden). Anyone looking up your domain can find its owner unless privacy protection is enabled.
