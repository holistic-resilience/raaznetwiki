# How to Choose a Secure and Reliable VPN Service

Choosing a secure and reliable VPN service and points related to user security and privacy when using a VPN is a question for many internet users. Paying attention to influential factors for selecting a secure VPN provider, along with related security recommendations, are among the items prepared for you in this educational article.

## What is a VPN and What Does It Do?

Generally, VPNs (Virtual Private Network) allow users to:

- Access blocked websites
- Hide their real geographical location while browsing
- Protect their internet activities to some extent while using public Wi-Fi or untrusted networks

### VPN Security Limitations

It should be known that VPNs do not offer complete security for their users. Real examples show that even popular providers can be hacked:

- **NordVPN**: Admitted that hackers infiltrated one of the company's servers 18 months ago
- **TorGuard and VikingVPN**: Were also infiltrated by hackers

> ⚠️ **Note**: Although the infiltrated companies claimed that no data was stolen, these claims have not been independently verified.

---

## When Should You Use a VPN?

VPN services work by routing users' network traffic path to their servers and establishing an encrypted connection. Instead of the internet traffic origin being known from the current location, it originates from the VPN provider's servers.

### Recommended Use Cases

1. **Public Places**: Using untrusted Wi-Fi
2. **Protection from Eavesdropping**: Protecting internet activity on insecure networks
3. **Changing Geographical Location**: Showing activity location different from the real location

### Important Limitations

- You cannot count on the complete protection of VPN providers
- Basic protective measures are still necessary
- VPN does not help against suspicious links and phishing websites

---

## Does Using a VPN Make Me Anonymous on the Internet?

**Short Answer: No**

### What a VPN Hides

In case of using a VPN, details of your internet activities will not be visible to the following:

- Internet Service Providers (ISP)
- Network administrators
- Any other person monitoring your internet traffic

### What a VPN Does Not Hide

- Your identity is visible to the VPN service provider
- In most cases, records of users' online activities are stored by providers

> 💡 **Recommendation**: If you are looking for true anonymity on the internet, using **Tor Browser** is the best option.

---

## Criteria for Choosing a Secure and Reliable VPN

### 1. Researching the Provider Company

- Checking records of security incidents (data leaks, being hacked)
- Reading user reviews
- Independent security audits

### 2. Free or Paid VPN?

| Type     | Pros                            | Cons                               |
| -------- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Paid** | Better support, higher security | Monthly cost                       |
| **Free** | No cost                         | Risk of selling data, targeted ads |

> ⚠️ **Warning**: Free providers may earn revenue by tracking activities and selling data to third-party companies.

### 3. Checking App Permissions

**Normal Permissions:**

- Full network access
- View network connections

**Suspicious Permissions (Warning!):**

- Text messages
- Contact list
- Device camera

### 4. Logging Policy

**Most Important Point**: Ensure the VPN provider does not keep user traffic records (Log).

**Risks of Keeping Logs:**

- If servers are hacked, your information is at risk
- In case of request by governments or judicial authorities, information will be providable

### 5. Encryption and Security Protocols

Secure VPN services use effective encryption algorithms and security protocols.

**Recommended Protocols:**

- **OpenVPN** - Open source and audited
- **SSTP** - Secure and reliable
- **L2TP/IPsec** - Strong encryption

> 📝 **Reminder**: These encryptions only secure your communication channel with the VPN provider, not the entire internet activity.
