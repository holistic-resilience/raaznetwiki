# Exercise: Existing Practices and Capacities

## Purpose

This exercise helps you evaluate your current security practices and capacities against each threat you've previously identified. By assessing what protections already exist, you establish a baseline from which to build and improve your security posture.

## Prerequisites

- Completed threat identification and prioritization (Exercise 2.5b)
- List of prioritized threats ready for review

## Materials Needed

- Your prioritized threat list
- Writing materials or document for recording capacities
- Quiet space for reflection

---

## Assessment Framework

Work through each prioritized threat using the following questions:

### 1. Asset Protection

**Question:** What capacities already protect this person or asset from this threat?

**Examples:**
- For judicial harassment → strong legal knowledge or legal support network
- For device confiscation → encrypted hard drives and secure backups

### 2. Adversary Engagement

**Question:** What tactics exist for engaging with the threat actor?

Consider:
- Existing strategies for preventing adversary action
- Resources previously leveraged against this actor
- Past responses to similar threats and their effectiveness
- Current gaps (noting these is valuable for later planning)

### 3. Information Security

**Question:** What practices prevent critical information from reaching adversaries?

Evaluate your:
- Information protection protocols
- Counter-surveillance practices
- Data handling procedures

### 4. Physical Security

**Question:** How do you secure physical access to yourself and your property?

Review:
- Building and office security measures
- Vehicle and transportation safety
- Personal safety practices in high-risk environments
- Access control to private spaces

### 5. Well-being Practices

**Question:** What practices support psychological resilience against this threat?

Consider:
- Stress reduction techniques
- Rest and recovery practices
- Mindfulness or awareness practices
- Support networks and community care

---

## Important Considerations

> **Caution:** For each practice you identify, critically evaluate whether it actually provides protection. Ask yourself:
> - How do I know this practice is effective?
> - Could this create a false sense of security?
> - Have I tested or verified this assumption?

If uncertain about any practice's effectiveness, consult with colleagues or trusted advisors for an outside perspective before relying on it.

---

## Next Steps

After completing this assessment, you will have:
- A clear picture of existing security capacities
- Identified gaps requiring attention
- A foundation for developing improved security strategies
