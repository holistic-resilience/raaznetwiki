# Lock Down Firefox Add-ons

## Firefox Add-on Developers Targeted in Phishing Attacks

If you use Firefox, you should know that Mozilla [issued a warning](https://blog.mozilla.org/addons/2025/08/01/warning-phishing-campaign-detected/) to add-on developers about a phishing campaign. Add-ons such as browser extensions used to customize browsers often have wide-reaching access to users' browsing activity, so it's especially important that developers maintain control over these accounts to protect the users of their browser extensions.

Attackers are targeting developer accounts through AMO ([addons.mozilla.org](http://addons.mozilla.org/)). According to Mozilla, these emails are variants on the message:

> "Your Mozilla Add-ons account requires an update to continue accessing developer features."

Mozilla has not yet shared details about the scale of the attacks, nor how many developers may have been compromised. [Read more at The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2025/08/04/mozilla_add_on_phishing/).

## What You Can Do

### Remove Unneeded Extensions

This is a good reminder to review your browser extensions and minimize risk by uninstalling any that you don't need.

**Firefox users:**
- Click the hamburger (three-line) menu at the top right corner
- Navigate to "Extensions and themes"
- Select "Extensions"

**Chrome users:**
- Click the three-dot menu at the top right
- Navigate to "Extensions"

### Make an Exception for Privacy-Preserving Extensions

We recommend a few browser extensions for boosting your safety when browsing:

- **[Privacy Badger](https://privacybadger.org/)** (Electronic Frontier Foundation) - Helps block many trackers embedded in websites you browse
- **[uBlock Origin](https://ublockorigin.com/)** - Helps block ads that may contain tracking code. You can disable uBlock Origin on websites you'd like to support—or even better, become a paid subscriber!
