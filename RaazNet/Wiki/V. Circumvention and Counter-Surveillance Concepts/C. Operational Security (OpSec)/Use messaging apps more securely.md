# Use Messaging Apps More Securely

Messaging apps are widely used for informal and real-time communications, enabling immediate exchange of text and voice messages, file sharing, voice and video calls, and group chats.

Despite their popularity, many messaging apps have privacy and security issues that can expose your communications to risks—your messages could be tampered with or accessed by unauthorized third parties. It's important to reflect on your needs before deciding whether to use a messaging app for sensitive information and which apps to use.

This guide provides questions to help you make informed decisions about real-time communication tools, along with recommendations for choosing the best apps and using them safely.

> **Related Reading:** To assess how sensitive your communications are, see the [guide on protecting the privacy of your online communications](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/private-communication/).

## Assess Your Needs

Answer these questions to determine which messaging apps suit your communication needs.

### Basic Communication Requirements

1. **What do you need to exchange?**
   - Text only, or also voice messages, files, images, videos?
   - Do you need audio and video calls (individual or group)?

2. **Individual or group communication?**
   - For sensitive information with individuals, most messaging tools work
   - For group communications, verify the app supports groups and check size limits

3. **Technical requirements?**
   - Can you access the app on all devices and operating systems you use?
   - Can you install apps, or do you need browser-based access?
   - If you can't install apps, check if a [secure browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing) web interface is available

### Privacy and Identity Considerations

4. **Registration requirements—are you willing to provide contact information?**
   - If you need to hide your identity, choose tools that don't require a phone number or email
   - Prefer tools allowing email registration over phone number registration
   - Consider tools based on decentralized connections
   - [Learn more about chatting anonymously](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

### Security Features

5. **Do you need end-to-end encryption?**
   - For sensitive real-time information sharing, ensure messages are protected by default through end-to-end encryption
   - Verify group chats can also be encrypted
   - Confirm voice/video calls support end-to-end encryption

6. **Can conversations auto-delete?**
   - For sensitive exchanges, use tools that automatically delete messages after a set period
   - This protects information if a device is lost, stolen, or seized

### Evaluating App Trustworthiness

7. **Can you trust the messaging app?** Consider these factors:

   | Factor | What to Check |
   |--------|---------------|
   | **Mission** | Was it created for secure communications or commercial purposes? What's the business model? |
   | **Data Storage** | What data is stored, and for how long? Check privacy policy and terms of service for logging practices |
   | **Legal History** | How has the provider responded to government data requests? |
   | **Jurisdiction** | Where are headquarters and servers located? Will they comply with requests from your country's authorities? |
   | **Encryption Software** | What encryption software does the app use? Is it mature? |
   | **Open Source** | Are both app and server based on free and open-source software? |
   | **Security Audits** | Has the app undergone independent third-party audits? Were servers audited too? When was the last audit? |

### Group Chat Requirements

8. **For group communications, consider:**
   - Maximum number of participants needed
   - Whether participants must register or can join anonymously
   - Required administration and moderation features
   - Whether you need announcement-only channels or interactive discussions
   - End-to-end encryption and auto-deletion for sensitive group information
   - Video call requirements
   - Ability to organize conversations by topic/thread

## Use Messaging Tools Safely

Follow these recommendations regardless of which messaging app you use.

### Register with a Separate Identity

- **Avoid using your phone number** to create accounts when possible
- Create a separate email address for registration
- [Learn how to choose a username](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/#choose-a-name)
- [Learn how to create and protect multiple online identities](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/)
- [Learn how to chat anonymously](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

> **Why this matters:** Your phone number is typically connected to your official identity. Keeping messaging apps separate from your phone number, email, and social media accounts gives you control over who can reach you.

### Enable End-to-End Encryption

- Use apps that protect conversations with end-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
- If encryption isn't enabled by default, manually enable it for sensitive conversations

> **Why this matters:** Anyone with access to messaging servers can read unencrypted communications. End-to-end encryption prevents unauthorized access to your information.

### Verify Contact Identities

- Always verify contacts' identities through a second, safer channel
- Options include: face-to-face meetings, encrypted email, or a different secure platform

### Manage Message History

- **Enable disappearing messages** for sensitive conversations
- **Regularly delete chat history** if automatic deletion isn't available
- Find the option in your app to delete all or specific conversations

> **Why this matters:** Chat apps keep records of everything shared by default. Disappearing messages limit the information stored on your phone.

### Handle Links and Files Carefully

- **Suspicious links:** Don't open unexpected links
- **Unexpected files:** Contact the sender through a different secure channel to confirm before opening
- **Sharing files:** Consider uploading to secure file storage and sending the download link instead

> **Why this matters:** Many malware and spyware infections occur through clicking malicious links or downloading infected files. These precautions help prevent phishing attacks.

## Use Group Chats Safely

### Control Group Access

- **Assign admin rights carefully** — only to [verified contacts](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat/#verify-your-contacts-identities)
- **Know who you're inviting** before adding members
- **If you can't remove someone:** Start an entirely new group
  - Double-check all phone numbers, emails, or usernames before sending invitations
  - Verify each person's identity through a different trusted channel

### Configure Member Privileges

Review group settings to control who can:
- Write messages and send voice messages
- Add or remove members
- See members' names and contact information

> **Why this matters:** Proper privilege settings let you use groups either as peer chat rooms or as announcement channels for wider audiences, depending on your needs.
