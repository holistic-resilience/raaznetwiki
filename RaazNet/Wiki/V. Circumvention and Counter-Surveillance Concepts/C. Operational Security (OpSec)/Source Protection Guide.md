# Source Protection Guide for Journalists

Are you a journalist working on sensitive subjects? The effort to protect your sources needs to start before they contact you. These guides will help you create the processes that you need to make sure that both you and your sources stay safe from unwanted threats.

## Essential Guides

### First Contact Security

**[The First Time They Reach Out: Protect Sources from Themselves](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/first-time-they-reach-out-protect-sources-themselves/)**

One of the most significant challenges when working with sensitive sources is the "first contact problem" — when a source reaches out over a channel that could immediately compromise their confidentiality. This guide examines how to get ahead of the first contact problem by proactively advertising secure tip channels and how to reach out more safely in advance.

### Secure Messaging

**[Signal, the Secure Messaging App: A Guide for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/signal-beginners/)**

A walkthrough on how to set up and get started messaging with Signal, the secure messaging app.

### Tip Page Best Practices

**[Security Considerations for Confidential Tip Pages](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/security-confidential-tip-pages/)**

Not all tip pages are created equal. Some support email and phone, while others support encrypted channels such as Signal and SecureDrop. This guide covers strategy for explaining the varying security properties of tip channels, unpacking source safety for those who desire anonymity, and pitfalls to avoid when crafting your tip page.

### Working with Whistleblowers

**[Working with Whistleblowers: A Guide for Journalists](https://whistleblower.org/resources/guides/working-with-whistleblowers-a-guide-for-journalists/)**

This guide from the Government Accountability Project provides an in-depth look at common trends among whistleblowers, the legal threats they face, and tips for journalists on how to engage with them effectively and securely.

### Address Book Protection

**[To Protect Your Sources, Protect Your Address Book](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/to-protect-your-sources-protect-your-address-book/)**

An examination of the many ways address books can be leaked, particularly through cloud backups that may be vulnerable to law enforcement requests, and how to minimize related risks.

### Specialized Reporting Considerations

**[Taking Care with Source Security When Reporting on Abortion](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/abortion-reporting/)**

Since the U.S. Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade in 2022, journalists reporting on abortion may find themselves speaking to sources in reproductive health spaces targeted by law enforcement. This guide examines several types of data at issue and how to minimize risk when speaking to sensitive sources.

### For Sources: Sharing Information Securely

**[Here's How to Share Sensitive Leaks with the Press](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/sharing-sensitive-leaks-press/)**

Language to help communicate best practices for reaching the press securely. Covers security strategy and legal considerations that potential sources may want to think through before reaching out, plus how to use widely supported tip channels.
