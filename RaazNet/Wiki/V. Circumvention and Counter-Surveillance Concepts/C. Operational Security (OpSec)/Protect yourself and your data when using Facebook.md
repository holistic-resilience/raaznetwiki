# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Facebook

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Facebook account, protect your privacy, and handle potential abuse situations.

> **Related Guides:** See also the guides on [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration:** Commercial corporations may not share your values and might remove or filter content that they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Facebook's Policies

### Learn How Facebook Uses Your Information

- Review Facebook's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/182)
- [Learn about Facebook's privacy policy](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/explanation/)
- Review their [terms of service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms/preview) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

> **Why this matters:** It is often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to guess things about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these risks. Consider installing [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see an overview of terms for each service you use.

### Learn What Facebook Will Turn Over to Governments

- [Meta's policy on law enforcement requests](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Transparency report on government requests for user data](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [Transparency report on content restrictions based on local law](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [All Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

> **Why this matters:** Social media sites may give your information, including information you were trying to keep private, to governments or law enforcement if requested. Understanding these conditions helps you make informed decisions about what to share.

## Don't Use the Mobile App

**Recommendation:** Do not use the Facebook app on your mobile device. Connect using a browser instead.

> **Why this matters:** An app that constantly gathers information can violate your privacy in many ways, even when you deny permissions for geolocation, microphone, etc. Accessing Facebook through a browser ensures the platform is not constantly connected to a device you carry everywhere.

## Create a New Account

You can [create a Facebook account](https://www.facebook.com/help/188157731232424) for free.

### Decide Whether to Use a Real or Fake Name

**Key considerations:**

- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider using separate accounts for different campaigns and activities
- Keep personal and work accounts separate
- The key to safe social networking is trusting the people you connect with—use consistent identities that your network can validate
- Be aware of Facebook's [name policy](https://www.facebook.com/help/1090731264320592), which allows them to remove accounts using names not used in everyday life
- If choosing a non-everyday name, select one that Facebook's algorithms will recognize as plausible

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a [privacy-friendly server](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- If protecting your identity, use [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address) when setting up the email

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number with Your Account

- When possible, set up your account with only an email address
- Phone numbers can easily be used to identify you

> **Why this matters:** Consider whether your local law enforcement could make requests to service providers using your phone number to find activities associated with you, or whether someone seeking to harass or locate you might use your number.

### Skip "Find Friends"

- Dismiss requests to "allow access" to contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- Select contacts carefully afterwards
- Only connect to people you know and trust
- If connecting with unknown like-minded individuals, [carefully consider what information to share](#decide-what-information-to-share)
- Avoid sharing employer or educational background

> **Why this matters:** Social media uses contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous when you want to keep contacts hidden. Consider whether law enforcement might use these lists against you, or what might happen if social media revealed your associations publicly.

### Designate Someone to Manage Your Account

- Facebook will not give others direct access; instead, they can [close and "memorialize" your account](https://www.facebook.com/help/275013292838654/)
- For emergency access, arrange to share login information via [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- See [Digital First Aid Kit: Someone I Know Has Been Arrested](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/)
- [Remove accounts for medically incapacitated persons](https://www.facebook.com/help/480409628639043)

### Know How to Move Your Data Out

- Test downloading your data from Facebook
- Review what data is provided, in what format, and how you can use it

> **Why this matters:** Before storing data on a large platform, understand what it takes to move your data out. Complete deletion from servers may take considerable time.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Your Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Check your recovery email](https://www.facebook.com/help/162801153783275/)
- [Check your recovery phone number](https://www.facebook.com/help/124895950923762/)
- Change this information immediately if you lose access to these recovery methods

> **Why this matters:** Ensure an attacker hasn't changed recovery information to gain control later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as adversaries may hack them to change your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who accesses your Facebook account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Recommended methods (in order of preference):**
1. Security key
2. Authenticator app
3. Security codes

**Avoid:** SMS or phone calls—your mobile carrier has full access to these, and they're easier to hack.

- [Learn how 2FA works on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/148233965247823/)
- Complete the ["How to keep your account secure" checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa)

### Download and Save Recovery Codes

- [Get recovery codes for when you can't use your usual 2FA method](https://www.facebook.com/help/148104135383285)
- Print codes and store them securely (e.g., locked safe)

### Consider Turning on Advanced Protection

- [Learn about Advanced Protection](https://www.facebook.com/help/1052552578831700)

> **Note:** Advanced Protection is only available for people selected by Facebook. If eligible, you'll see prompts to enroll. We recommend considering these prompts promptly.

### Be Careful with Emails Claiming to Be from Facebook

- [Learn about suspicious emails and messages](https://www.facebook.com/help/225602007465207)
- [Check if Facebook emailed you recently](https://www.facebook.com/recent_emails/security)

> **Why this matters:** Phishing messages try to trick you into giving account access. Don't click links in security emails—instead, log in directly to your account's security page to verify legitimacy.

### Create an Alternative Identity

If using Facebook without connecting to your real identity:
- Create your account through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use [an email address created using Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

- Check which devices have recently logged into your account
- If you see suspicious activity, immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) not used elsewhere
- Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

> **Why this matters:** Adversaries may log into your account from their devices. You can often detect this in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Get Notified About Logins

Enable alerts for login attempts from unusual devices or locations.

## If You Think Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Perform a security checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/review/?review_id=573933453011661)
2. Follow [Facebook's steps for securing a hacked account](https://www.facebook.com/hacked) or the [step-by-step walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/help/1306725409382822)
3. If standard procedures don't work, check [Digital First Aid Kit: I lost access to my account](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

### Review Connected Sites and Apps

- Avoid using Facebook to log into other sites—use separate passwords stored in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- Be careful connecting accounts—you may be anonymous on one site but exposed on another
- [Manage privacy settings for connected apps and websites](https://www.facebook.com/help/218345114850283/)

> **Why this matters:** When other sites and apps have access to your account, hackers can exploit these connections to access your accounts.

## Decide What Information to Share

- [Choose who you share content with](https://www.facebook.com/help/325807937506242#choose-who-you-share-content-with)
- Complete [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264)
- [Set up regular Privacy Checkup reminders](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264#set-up-privacy-checkup-reminders)
- [Manage what others can see about you](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/dialog/how-to-clean-your-profile?helpref=privacycenter)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**High-risk information:**
- Personally identifying information
- Information revealing where you live (e.g., pictures of your house)
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider separate sensitive/non-sensitive accounts)

**Best practices:**
- Remember that information can easily be copied and spread beyond your intended audience
- Agree with your network on what to share for safety reasons
- Be sensitive about what you reveal about friends; ask them to do the same for you

> **Why this matters:** The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. "Likes" on opposition pages, for example, might attract government attention. Family members of activists abroad may be targeted because of publicly accessible information.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location services work on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509/)
- If using the mobile app, turn off location services
- [Don't add location to your posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509#adding-or-sharing-your-location)

> **Why this matters:** If worried about location tracking, stop your accounts from storing location information. This also improves battery life.

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Never post images that include or reveal:**
- Sensitive locations or identifiable backgrounds
- Identifiable information about others without consent

**Actions:**
- [Delete photos you uploaded](https://www.facebook.com/help/208547132518386/)
- [Control who can see shared albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/410653545719324)
- [Delete albums with all contained photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/152153388188974)
- [Remove contributors from shared albums](https://www.facebook.com/help/589394671112683)
- [Remove metadata before posting](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

> **Why this matters:** Photos and videos can reveal information unintentionally, especially backgrounds. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and camera used. Seek consent before posting about others.

### Change Who Can See Your "Likes"

- [Change "Who can see your social interactions alongside ads"](https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences/ad_settings) to "Only Me"
- [Learn how video interactions can be seen by others](https://www.facebook.com/help/410629549531206)

> **Why this matters:** In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when leaving reactions, depending on your legal situation.

### Don't Share Your Birthday

- You can register with a different birthday and change it later
- Keep track of what you enter for account recovery purposes

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share to Select People

- Complete the ["Who can see what you share" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- Set everything under "Profile information" to "Only me"
- Consider "Limit past posts" to restrict visibility of older posts
- [Check your activity log](https://www.facebook.com/settings/)
- [Change who can see your apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/201750739897196)

### Manage Who Can Reply

- [Choose who can comment on your Page's posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/369765040737128)
- [Hide comments from posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/297845860255949)
- [Stop people from posting on your profile](https://www.facebook.com/help/115469971891543)
- [Control what visitors can post on your Page](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/356113237741414)

> **Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims about associations. They can also be used for harassment. Controlling replies can reduce stress.
