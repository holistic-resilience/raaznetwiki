# Secure Communication

We'd probably object if someone put a microphone and camera in our room and could listen to our conversations at any time. Yet, whether through ordinary phone calls or messages on social media, we often use chat tools that enable the service provider to eavesdrop on our conversations.

For added privacy and security, we recommend using chat tools that are **end-to-end encrypted**, meaning only the participants in the conversation can read the calls and messages. No uninvited guests—not even the service provider—should be able to listen in.

## Encrypted Messaging Apps

### Signal

**[Signal for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/signal-beginners/)**

Signal is one of the best end-to-end encrypted messengers available, enabling voice, video, and messaging with other Signal users. Signal differentiates itself by minimizing the amount of information it stores about conversations, such as who spoke to whom and when. This guide walks you through setting up and using Signal for Android and iOS.

**[Locking Down Signal](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/locking-down-signal/)**

This guide covers advanced settings that Signal users can leverage to harden the app even further, along with considerations for minimizing risk.

**[Taking Private Photos with Signal](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/taking-private-photos-signal/)**

Your phone may back up or store photos to your camera roll, which may not preserve your privacy if you have automatic backups enabled on Google Drive or iCloud. This guide explains how to use Signal to take ephemeral and private photos within the app that are never saved to the camera roll unless you choose.

### WhatsApp

**[Upgrading WhatsApp Security](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/upgrading-whatsapp-security/)**

With over two billion users, WhatsApp is one of the most popular chat services in the world. The good news: WhatsApp offers end-to-end encryption. The bad news: Its parent company, Meta, collects significant metadata about users and their contacts, and many useful security features are not enabled by default. This guide describes how to enable stronger security settings within WhatsApp and minimize risk when using it.

### Keybase

**[Keybase for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/keybase-beginners/)**

Keybase is a powerful yet fairly simple end-to-end encrypted file-sharing and chat service. This guide describes how to set up and use Keybase, along with tips for using it safely.

## Encrypted Email

### Proton Mail

**[Proton Mail like a Pro](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/protonmail-pro/)**

Since the 1990s, [PGP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pretty_Good_Privacy) has been the standard for encrypting messages and files, including email. However, it is notoriously tricky to use, and even experienced users make mistakes. Proton Mail is an email client designed to make PGP easier to work with, particularly when sending encrypted messages with other Proton Mail users.

### Mailvelope

**[Encrypting Email with Mailvelope](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/encrypting-email-mailvelope-guide/)**

Mailvelope offers PGP email encryption and decryption through a browser add-on, enabling users to send encrypted messages using popular email clients including Gmail and Microsoft Outlook. This in-depth guide explains how to set up and use Mailvelope.

## Key Takeaways

- **End-to-end encryption** ensures only conversation participants can read messages
- **Signal** offers strong encryption with minimal metadata collection
- **WhatsApp** provides encryption but requires additional security configuration
- **Proton Mail and Mailvelope** make email encryption more accessible
