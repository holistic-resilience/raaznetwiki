# Exercise: Security Indicators in Daily Life

![Security indicators visualization](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/media/exercises/images/default/explore_detail_05.jpg%3F1465900376)

## Purpose

This exercise helps you visualize your daily routines to identify moments where you can establish security baselines and check for potential threats. By externalizing activities you normally take for granted, you gain a fresh perspective on your physical security.

## Materials Needed

- Drawing materials (pen, pencil, markers)
- Notebook, electronic document, or whiteboard for your checklist

## Instructions

### Step 1: Map Your Daily Routine

Use drawing to visualize your typical day. Consider these questions:

- **Morning**: Where do you have breakfast? What's your morning environment like?
- **Commute**: How do you travel to work? What vehicle, route, and companions?
- **Belongings**: What devices, keys, and items do you carry daily?
- **Workplace**: Where do you work? Who else is present? What devices do you use?
- **Meals**: Where and when do you eat? How long are your breaks?
- **Evening**: What time do you finish work? What route do you take home?
- **Night**: What's your pre-sleep routine? When do you normally sleep?
- **Other locations**: Where else do you regularly spend time?

### Step 2: Identify Security Checkpoints

From your routine map, note moments to check for signs of danger:

| Location | What to Check |
|----------|---------------|
| **Vehicle** | Signs of tampering (wheels, brakes, steering) |
| **Route** | Dangerous areas; whether you're being followed |
| **Workspace** | Everything in place on arrival and departure; doors and windows locked |
| **Home surroundings** | Unfamiliar people, vehicles, or police presence |

### Step 3: Create Your Checklist

Document the specific moments when you'll check your physical surroundings. Consider sharing this checklist with trusted friends, neighbors, or colleagues.

If you're at high risk, include the daily routines of family members or close associates.

## Tips

- **Multiple activities**: If your work involves diverse activities, repeat this exercise for each type.
- **Verification**: Share your observations with a trusted person to double-check your analysis and identify blind spots.

> **For High-Risk Situations**: During protests, resistance actions, or documentation missions, security conditions change rapidly. Create a separate checklist specifically for these activities, noting different moments when you should check for danger signs.
