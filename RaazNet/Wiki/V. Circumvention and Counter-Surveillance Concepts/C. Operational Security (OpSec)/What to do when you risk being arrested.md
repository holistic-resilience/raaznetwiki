# What to Do When You Risk Being Arrested

In some regions, prominent human rights defenders, activists, journalists, and lawyers must choose between going into exile or risking arrest and legal prosecution, often in unfair conditions. This is a situation that [the Front Line Defenders Digital Protection team](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/en/programme/digital-protection) has frequently observed, especially in Central American countries like Guatemala, El Salvador, and Mexico.

This guide provides guidelines and recommendations to help people at risk protect their activities, information, and contact networks by making it harder to extract data from personal and work devices.

> **Related Resource:** If you are preparing to go into exile or travel in riskier situations, read [our guide on how to protect your information and devices while traveling](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel).

---

## Assess Your Situation

### Talk to a Lawyer

- **Assess your legal situation:** How likely is it that you will be detained?
- **Know your rights:** Understand what you can and cannot be compelled to do regarding device access and passwords.

> **Important:** If violent interrogation or torture are possible scenarios, it is better to have empty devices so you can provide your passwords without exposing yourself or others to higher risks.

### Map Your Data

Take an inventory of the most important information on your devices (phones, computers, external hard drives, USB sticks, SD cards, etc.). Include all information stored online (cloud storage platforms, mailboxes, etc.).

- Follow recommendations in [the Holistic Security Manual](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/exercises/explore/information-ecosystem.html)
- Create a digital inventory of sensitive information: pictures, text documents, PDFs, chats, attachments, login credentials, etc.

---

## Prepare for Potential Detention

Follow this sequence to minimize your digital exposure:

1. **Delete** what you don't need
2. **Back up** what you want to keep
3. **Delete** backed-up content from your devices
4. **Disconnect** devices from online storage and email services containing sensitive information
5. **Uninstall apps** (if possible) that access online services; use browsers instead

### Back Up Important Information

- [Learn how to back up your data more securely](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/)
- Back up to an encrypted external storage device and/or a secure online storage platform
- Repeat the backup process regularly if you continue producing content, then delete information again

### Minimize Data in Your Devices

- After completing your backup, delete all data—or at least the most sensitive items—from your devices
- Review our [guide on protecting information while traveling](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel/) for device cleanup recommendations
- Consider keeping all data and work in a [secure cloud storage service](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/#encrypted-cloud-services)

**Store sensitive files in a secure app:** If you must keep files on your computer, use a secure storage application.

### Protect Your Devices Against Malware

If you suspect an ongoing investigation, protect your devices against surveillance through spyware.

- [Read our guide on preventing malware infections](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/malware/)

---

## Prepare Your Phone

### For All Phones

- Avoid linking your phone to personal Google or iCloud accounts; create a new account if necessary
- **Disable biometrics** for phone unlock; use at least a 6-digit PIN or an 8+ character password
- Enable automatic updates
- Remove apps containing private or sensitive information or connecting to sensitive online services
- Use your phone only for less sensitive purposes
- Read guides for [Android](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/android/) or [iOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/ios/) security
- **iPhone:** [Enable Lockdown Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/guide/iphone/iph049680987/ios)
- **Android:** Enroll in [Google's Advanced Protection program](https://support.google.com/android/answer/16339980)

### Recommended: Install GrapheneOS

For enhanced security:

1. Obtain a Google Pixel (ideally [8a](https://store.google.com/us/product/pixel_8a) or newer)
2. Have a trusted technologist install [GrapheneOS](https://grapheneos.org/)
3. Create separate profiles (admin, work, bank, social media, etc.)
4. Use the default browser and PDF reader built into GrapheneOS

## Prepare Your Computer

- Follow security guides for [macOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/mac/), [Windows](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/windows/), or [Linux](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/linux/)
- If keeping information on your computer, [encrypt your hard drive](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/secure-file-storage/#consider-encrypting-your-whole-device)

### Recommended: Use Tails

Tails is an operating system that runs from a USB stick or SD card. When you shut down, no traces remain on the hard drive or RAM. Tails connects to the internet exclusively through [the Tor network](http://torproject.org/), anonymizing your traffic.

- [Learn about Tails](https://tails.net/about/index.en.html)
- [Install Tails](https://tails.net/install/index.en.html)
- [Get started with Tails](https://tails.net/doc/first_steps/index.en.html)
- [Create persistent storage](https://tails.net/doc/persistent_storage/index.en.html) for encrypted file storage

### Optional: Install Qubes OS

For protection against online attacks, consider [Qubes OS](https://www.qubes-os.org/), which increases security by isolating different compartments (called *qubes*). Configure it using [the SecureDrop guide for journalists and human rights workers](https://github.com/freedomofpress/securedrop-workstation).

> **Note:** Qubes OS protects against remote hacking but may not fully protect data from forensic tools during physical device seizure.

## Review Your Password Strategy

Weak passwords are the most vulnerable point of any encryption or secure service.

- [Use strong and unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
- [Save passwords in a secure password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- [Enable 2-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for all online accounts
- Back up your 2FA codes safely; consider [Proton Authenticator](https://proton.me/support/back-up-2fa-codes)

---

## Secure Your Communications

### Chat More Securely

- [Read recommendations on secure messaging](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat)
- Use apps with end-to-end encryption by default, ideally [Signal or another secure messaging app](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications)

### Protect Your Connections

- [Browse the web more securely](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)
- Connect only through [a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/circumvention/#choose-a-vpn-service)

### Minimize App Usage for Online Services

Access sensitive emails or cloud services through a browser, then log out when finished. Avoid using apps whenever possible to reduce traces of activity on your devices.

---

## Prepare Your Support Network

### Choose Trusted Contacts

- Securely share passwords to your most sensitive accounts (including Google or iCloud) with trusted contacts
- Ask them to suspend your social media accounts and wipe your phone remotely if you are arrested
- Compile a list of digital security helplines to help contacts act quickly

### Set Up Emergency Apps

- **Android:** Configure the [Personal Safety app](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9319337) for [emergency actions](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9319337#zippy=%2Cuse-emergency-sos-to-call-for-help-alert-your-contacts-record-videos) (calling for help, sharing location, recording video)
- **iPhone:** Use [Emergency SOS](https://support.apple.com/guide/personal-safety/emergency-call-text-iphone-apple-watch-ips4f0cd709b) to alert emergency contacts and share live video or photos

---

## During Detention

- Keep all devices **off** if possible
- Only turn on your phone to call trusted contacts (if permitted), then switch it off again
- **Do not unlock your phone** after calling

---

## After Release from Detention

- **Do not use returned devices** until an expert performs forensic analysis to check for malware
- Be prepared that confirming device infection may be impossible—you may need to replace devices entirely
