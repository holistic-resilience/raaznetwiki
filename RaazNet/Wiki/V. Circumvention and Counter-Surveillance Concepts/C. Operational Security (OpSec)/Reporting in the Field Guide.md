# Reporting in the Field

Journalism often takes you beyond the desk. Whether you're covering a protest, meeting with a source, or crossing international borders to pursue a story, each scenario presents unique security challenges. This guide compiles essential resources to help you stay safe while working in the field.

## Essential Field Security Resources

### Getting Started

**[First Steps for Online Safety](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/first-steps-for-online-safety/)**

A foundational guide covering digital security basics: risk assessment, account security, malware defense, and encrypted communications. Start here if you're new to digital security.

### Mobile Photography & Media

**[Redacting Photos on the Go: A Field Guide](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/redacting-photos-on-the-go/)**

Learn how to safely redact sensitive information from photos on your mobile device before sharing or publishing—essential for protecting sources and locations captured in the field.

**[Digital Security for Filmmakers: Protecting Your Footage](https://www.digitalsecurity.film/protecting-your-footage)**

Created in collaboration with Field of Vision, this guide covers methods to encrypt and password-protect footage on storage devices, computers, and cloud services.

### Tools & Services

**[How Secure Are Journalists' Favorite Transcription Tools?](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/how-secure-are-journalists-favorite-transcription-tools/)**

A security analysis of five popular transcription services (including Otter.ai), examining how they handle the privacy and security of your transcripts and audio files.

**[Encryption Toolkit for Media Makers](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/introduction/)**

Step-by-step guides for encrypting external storage using:
- BitLocker To Go (Windows)
- Disk Utility (macOS)
- VeraCrypt (cross-platform)

### Protest Coverage

**[How Journalists Can Protect Themselves While Covering Protests](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/how-journalists-can-protect-themselves-while-covering-protests/)**

Focused guidance for U.S. journalists on managing device security and reporting materials during protest coverage. Addresses digital security alongside physical safety and legal rights.

## Key Considerations for Field Work

When working outside secure environments, prioritize:

- **Device security**: Encrypt devices and use strong authentication
- **Data minimization**: Carry only essential information
- **Secure communications**: Use end-to-end encrypted messaging
- **Source protection**: Redact identifying information before sharing
- **Situational awareness**: Understand local laws and risks
