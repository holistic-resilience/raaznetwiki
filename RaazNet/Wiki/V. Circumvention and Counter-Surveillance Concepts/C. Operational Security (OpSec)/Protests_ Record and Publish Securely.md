# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Before Recording

Ensure you are in as physically safe a space as possible before taking pictures or filming. Ideally, have another person record you while you film, and vice versa, in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries. Plan this tactic in advance with your affinity group.

## Secure Recording Tools

When using a smartphone, several apps help you record safely:

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| [ObscuraCam](https://guardianproject.info/apps/obscuracam/) | Blur faces in photos and videos |
| [CameraV](https://guardianproject.info/apps/camerav) | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| [eyeWitness to Atrocities](http://www.eyewitnessproject.org/) | Record legally verifiable footage |
| [Mobile Martus](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.martus.android) | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |
| [Orbot](https://guardianproject.info/apps/orbot) | Upload content while protecting your identity |

## Secure Sharing

**Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted.

**Use encrypted communication instead:**
- [Signal](https://whispersystems.org/) for end-to-end encrypted messaging
- [GPG-encrypted email](https://securityinabox.org/en/guide/thunderbird/windows) for secure file transfer

**If storing on an SD card:** Consider encrypting your phone, or pass the card to a trusted person who can take it to safety.

## Publishing Safely

Before publishing recorded material, protect your identity and the identities of others captured in the footage.

### Remove Metadata

Photos and videos contain EXIF data that can reveal where, when, and by whom the content was created. Remove this using:
- [Metanull](https://securityinabox.org/en/lgbti-mena/metanull/windows)
- [Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)](https://mat.boum.org/)
- CameraV (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Cover Your Tracks

When uploading or sharing material:
- Use [Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/) on desktop
- Use Orbot on Android
- Consider a VPN service to access blocked websites

## Legal and Safety Considerations

If you're concerned about the safety and legal implications of sharing gathered information:
- Seek advice from lawyers and human rights organizations
- Maintain secure backups
- Decide whether to keep non-anonymized materials, and if so, how to store them securely
