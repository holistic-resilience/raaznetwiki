# Security Indicators

A **security indicator** is anything out of the ordinary that may signal a change in your security situation. These indicators range from concrete incidents—such as declared threats, attacks against partner organizations, or suspicious behavior—to subtle developments like changes in device behavior or shifts in your health and well-being.

What unites all security indicators is their potential to reveal emerging threats. They should be shared and analyzed with trusted allies to establish their meaning and determine appropriate responses.

## Why Monitor Security Indicators?

We naturally monitor situations and notice when we no longer feel safe—our intuition guides us. Developing a practice of analyzing security indicators means taking a more **deliberate and organized approach** to this natural instinct.

Regular habits of noting, recording, sharing, and analyzing indicators with colleagues and allies helps maintain objectivity about your security situation and prevents threats from going unnoticed.

---

## Categories of Security Indicators

### Socio-Political Environment

Monitoring the political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental context of your work is vital for security and operational success.

**Key practices:**
- Talk regularly with trusted friends, colleagues, and partner organizations
- Follow and document relevant news developments
- Meet with subject-matter experts when beginning work in new areas
- Track changes in laws, policies, or enforcement patterns that affect your work

### Digital Security

Identifying digital security indicators often requires deliberate attention. With increasing electronic surveillance targeting activists and human rights defenders, learning to spot digital anomalies is essential.

**Establish a baseline** of what constitutes "normal" behavior for your devices, then monitor for changes:

| Area | What to Monitor |
|------|-----------------|
| **Malware detection** | Run regular scans with anti-malware software |
| **Network security** | Check firewall settings and logs |
| **System processes** | Look for unauthorized programs or processes |
| **Account security** | Enable two-factor authentication; monitor login activity |
| **Physical tampering** | Use UV markers or tamper-evident tape on devices; photograph them for comparison |

If you suspect something is wrong, consult a trusted digital security expert.

### Daily Activities

Your daily routines offer numerous opportunities to notice security indicators. Consider:

- **Travel patterns**: How you move between home and work
- **Regular activities**: What you do throughout a typical day
- **Devices**: What you carry and how they behave
- **Interactions**: Who you encounter and any changes in those relationships

By understanding your normal patterns, you can more easily identify factors that signal a change in your security situation.

### Health and Well-Being

Changes in health often indicate reactions to external problems such as increased stress, which directly impacts your security situation. As activists, the tendency to push through can cause lasting damage or impair your ability to assess threats accurately.

**Monitor your physical, emotional, and psychological state** methodically. Changes may occur gradually, making a structured approach valuable. Tools like a stress table can help track patterns over time.

## Sharing and Analyzing Security Indicators

Sharing indicators with trusted friends, colleagues, or partner organizations helps identify patterns and verify your perceptions. Since security incidents are sensitive—both as information and emotionally—create a **safe space** for discussion.

**Recommendations:**
- Include security indicators as a regular meeting agenda item
- Ensure affected individuals feel supported when sharing
- Take action when patterns emerge

---

## Framework for Analyzing Security Incidents

For significant security indicators—especially concrete incidents—use these questions to guide analysis:

1. **What happened?**
2. **When did it happen?**
3. **Where did it happen?**
4. **Who was affected?**
5. **Was gender-based violence involved?** Consider both physical and psychological factors.
6. **Who was responsible?** (for incidents involving aggressors)
7. **Why do we believe this happened?** Focus on facts rather than accusations.
8. **What was the origin?** Was this related to common crime, environmental factors, or your work and activism?

---

## Recording Security Indicators

Create a secure system for recording indicators in detail. This might be a document or spreadsheet that you review periodically (weekly or monthly) to identify trends.

**Important considerations:**
- Use the analysis questions above to structure your records
- Include as much detail as possible
- Review regularly to spot patterns
- **Treat this record as highly sensitive information**
