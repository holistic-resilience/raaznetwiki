# Protests: Affinity Groups - Care for Yourself and Others

Protesting effectively and securely often means coordinating with a trusted group of friends or colleagues who can act together and care for one another. This guide covers tactics for coordinating action to keep yourself and others safe.

## What is an Affinity Group?

An affinity group is a small group of fellow protesters or friends who prepare, carry out, and leave the protest together, interacting with the larger protest as a unit. Members create their security plan together and divide up roles for the action and for responding in emergencies.

**Key characteristics:**
- Typically 2-15 people
- Small enough for personal sharing, direct trust, and mutual care
- Can be formed for a specific action or as a continuous part of a movement
- Can operate autonomously or coordinate with other affinity groups

## Setting Up Your Affinity Group

### Assign Roles

Consider distributing responsibilities such as:
- **Facilitator** - coordinates group decisions and communication
- **Representative** - liaises with other groups or organizers
- **Media contact** - handles any press interactions
- **Medical support** - provides first-aid assistance
- **"Good vibe watch"** - monitors group morale and stress levels

Ideally, one or two members should have first-aid and psycho-social care skills for emergencies.

### Establish Decision-Making

Create a clear decision-making structure before the action. This ensures the group can respond quickly and cohesively during the protest.

### Plan Together

- Discuss your action plan as a group
- Check one another's perceptions on security issues
- Agree on communication methods during the action
- Plan how you'll respond to emergencies together
- Commit to leaving together

## Managing Well-Being

### Before the Protest

- **Know your stress responses:** How does your body and mind react when you reach an unhealthy level of stress? Identify practices or resources that help you return to a healthy state.
- **Foster open communication:** Build a culture where you can share fears and talk about stress and traumatic experiences openly.
- **Practice active listening:** Build solidarity and care for one another through genuine attention and support.

### During the Protest

- Ensure access to food and water
- Prepare for different weather conditions
- Stay in communication with your group
- Watch for signs of distress in yourself and others

### After the Protest

- **Debrief together:** Once you've arrived at a safe location, take time to discuss what happened with your affinity group.
- **Provide mutual support:** Make time for whatever sharing and support the group needs.
- **Process individually:** Take some time alone to process the experience and transition back into daily life.

## Building an Inclusive Movement

Security isn't just about physical protection—for many people, security comes from a sense of belonging, solidarity, and mutual respect. This is especially important for those who may face discrimination or violence based on gender identity, race, sexual orientation, appearance, or other factors.

### Examine Privilege Within Your Group

Those with more privilege may not notice when they reproduce inequality within movement spaces. Consider:
- Are positions of influence held disproportionately by people from traditionally privileged groups?
- Are responsibilities distributed equally across different identities?
- Are some voices consistently centered while others are marginalized?

### Take Action

- **Challenge power imbalances:** If some parts of the group hold all the power, address this directly.
- **Create opportunities:** Give people chances they may have traditionally been denied.
- **Zero tolerance policy:** Make clear that discrimination, sexual harassment, or violence will result in consequences, including possible removal from the movement.
- **No "jokes":** These issues are never appropriate material for humor.
- **Plan for inclusion:** People who face discrimination may need special protection measures or active inclusion during protests. Address this in your security planning.
