# Talking About Security in Groups and Teams

## Purpose & Output

This exercise helps teams reflect on how and when they discuss security with peers and colleagues. While best facilitated with at least two people, it also works as individual reflection on group interactions.

**Goal:** Start a constructive process for discussing security within your team or group.

## Materials Needed

- Writing materials (index cards, sticky notes, markers)
- Large wall space, flip-chart, or pin-board for documentation

## Format

Individual reflection followed by group discussion

---

## Exercise Steps

### Step 1: Pair Discussion

Divide into pairs and discuss the following questions, writing down your answers:

1. What topics take up the majority of time in group conversations?
2. What topics do we never seem to find time for?
3. What aspects of our group interaction do we find energizing?
4. What aspects of our group interaction do we find exhausting?
5. What happens in the group when people disagree?
6. Do you have enough space to talk about security issues with peers or close colleagues? How might this space be created or expanded?

### Step 2: Compile Responses

Collate all responses on a board or in a shared notebook.

### Step 3: Team Discussion

As a full team, consider these questions:

1. Where and how do we want to set our priorities concerning security?
2. What common problems arise when talking about security as a group?
3. What prevents us from talking about security? How can we address this?
4. How can we create and maintain adequate space for security discussions? What time and resources will this require?
5. How might we increase the effectiveness of our group interaction on security?
6. What problems arise around committing to changing our security practices? Do we resist change, and why?

### Step 4: Individual Reflection

Each person reflects privately on:

1. Should you have similar security awareness for family and loved ones?
2. How are family and loved ones affected differently by security concerns?
3. How do you communicate the threats you face to people outside your work circle?

### Step 5: Establish Guidelines

As a whole group:

1. Share points that individuals feel comfortable discussing
2. Agree on what information can be communicated to those outside the group
3. Establish clear guidelines around confidentiality, intimacy, and security for the entire group

---

## Discussion Points

Consider these scenarios as a group:

- What happens if you don't make progress on discussing security?
- What happens if someone doesn't follow the agreed guidelines about external communication?
