# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Before Recording

Ensure you are in a physically safe location before taking pictures or filming. Ideally, have another person record you while you film, and vice versa, in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries. Plan this tactic in advance with your affinity group.

## Secure Recording Tools

If you're using a smartphone, several tools can help you record safely:

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **ObscuraCam** | Blur faces to protect identities |
| **CameraV** | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| **eyeWitness to Atrocities** | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| **Mobile Martus** | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |

## Secure Transmission

When sending recorded files:

- **Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted
- **Use end-to-end encrypted messengers** like Signal
- **Use GPG-encrypted email** for sensitive content
- **Use Orbot** to upload content while protecting your identity

If storing content on an SD card, consider encrypting your phone or passing the card to someone who can take it to safety.

## Publishing Safely

When publishing or sharing recordings, protect your identity and the identities of others in the footage.

### Remove Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data — metadata that can reveal where, when, and by whom content was captured. Remove this using:

- **Metanull**
- **Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)**
- **CameraV** (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Cover Your Tracks

- Use **Tor Browser** (or Orbot on Android)
- Use a **VPN service** to mask your location and access blocked sites

### Seek Expert Guidance

If you have concerns about safety or legal implications of sharing gathered information, consult lawyers and human rights organizations. Maintain secure backups and carefully consider whether to retain non-anonymized materials.
