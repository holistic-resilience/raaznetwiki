# Language Tools

Text inputted to grammar, spelling, and style checkers, as well as translation services, can contain sensitive information which may be stored on their servers for an indefinite amount of time and sold to third parties. The language tools listed on this page do not store your submitted text on a server and can be self-hosted and used offline for maximum control of your data.

## Privacy Concerns Addressed

- **Privacy from Service Providers** - These tools don't require sharing your text with third parties
- **Surveillance Capitalism** - Avoid having your writing analyzed and monetized

## Grammar & Spelling

### LanguageTool

**LanguageTool** is a multilingual grammar, style, and spell checker that supports more than 20 languages. According to their privacy policy, they do not store any content sent to their service for review. For higher assurance, the software is [self-hostable](https://dev.languagetool.org/http-server).

**Key Features:**
- Supports 20+ languages
- Available as browser extensions (Firefox, Chrome, Edge, Safari)
- Desktop apps for Windows and macOS
- Integration with office suites and email clients
- Self-hosting option available

**Resources:**
- [Homepage](https://languagetool.org/)
- [Privacy Policy](https://languagetool.org/legal/privacy)
- [Documentation](https://languagetooler.freshdesk.com/en/support/solutions)
- [Source Code](https://github.com/languagetool-org)

## Translation Tools

### LibreTranslate

**LibreTranslate** is a free and open-source machine translation web interface and API server. It uses [Argos Translate](https://github.com/argosopentech/argos-translate) models on the backend for translations.

**Key Features:**
- Free and open-source
- Multiple public instances available
- Accessible via Tor onion service or I2P eepsite
- Self-hostable for maximum privacy

**Resources:**
- [Homepage](https://libretranslate.com/)
- [Public Instances](https://github.com/LibreTranslate/LibreTranslate#mirrors)
- [Source Code](https://github.com/LibreTranslate/LibreTranslate)

## Selection Criteria

These tools were selected based on the following requirements:

1. **Must be open source** - Allows verification of privacy claims
2. **Must be possible to self-host** - Enables complete control over submitted data
