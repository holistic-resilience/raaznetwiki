# The Dim Sum Guide: Digital Security for Chinese-Speaking Readers

**By Haven | Published: February 12, 2025**

> **Access the guide:** [https://yummydimsum.github.io/](https://yummydimsum.github.io/)

Some countries experience more complex censorship and cyber-attacks against human rights defenders than others. People from China are one of the groups requiring contextualised digital protection. The Dim Sum guide, developed with support from Front Line Defenders, builds on Security in a Box content while providing specific instructions for Chinese-speaking readers. **Ideally, both guides should be read together.**

## Key Topics Covered

### iOS Device Restrictions in China

The guide addresses restrictions on iPhones and iPads in China, including:

- Lack of eSIM support
- FaceTime limited to video calls only (no audio)
- Heavy censorship of books, music, movies, and other content

**AirDrop Warning:** During the 2022 "Sitongqiao Protests" and "White Paper Movement," many used AirDrop to share protest-related content. In early 2024, Beijing authorities were apparently able to trace mobile phone numbers and email accounts of senders. **The Dim Sum guide recommends avoiding AirDrop entirely.**

### Safer Chinese Input Methods

Chinese input methods are essential software for Chinese language users, but inappropriate apps risk exposing sensitive information. Research has proven that some Chinese company input methods have security issues or backdoors. The guide provides recommendations for more secure alternatives across different operating systems.

### Secure VPN Solutions

China maintains one of the world's strictest internet filtering regimes. However:

- Not all VPNs work reliably or securely
- Reports indicate Chinese police have developed phishing VPNs to harvest user data

The guide recommends secure censorship circumvention solutions and is continuously updated with current information.

### Responding to Police Phone Searches

Following the 2022 "White Paper Movement," police in many Chinese cities began randomly checking devices for VPNs, Telegram, and similar tools. The guide explains how to respond to police phone checks from both:

- Digital security perspectives
- Legal perspectives

## Staying Current

The digital landscape constantly shifts, and no resource guarantees 100% security. The team continuously monitors and updates both the Dim Sum guide and Security in a Box.
