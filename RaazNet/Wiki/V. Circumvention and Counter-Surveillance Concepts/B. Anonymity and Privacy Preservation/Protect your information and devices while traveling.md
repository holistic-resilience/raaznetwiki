# Protect Your Information and Devices While Traveling

*Last Updated: 3 June 2025*

## Overview

Traveling exposes you, your devices, and your data to significant risks. Laptops and mobile devices can be stolen, lost, damaged, or confiscated. Data transmitted over public networks can be intercepted. Using untrusted systems may expose you to keyloggers and other attacks.

**Critical consideration:** When crossing any national border, you have few if any rights to refuse inspection of your luggage—including data stored on your devices. In many countries, including the US, customs officers need no "probable cause" or warrant to search your devices or confiscate them for further inspection.

---

## Basic Security Practices

Implement these foundational measures before any journey:

### Account Security
- Set **strong, unique passwords** for all accounts
- Enable **multi-factor authentication** wherever possible
- Update your operating system and all software to patch vulnerabilities

### Device Protection
- Ensure **updated antivirus software** is running
- Configure devices to **lock automatically** when unattended
- Require password authentication at login and when resuming from standby

### Behavioral Security
- **Avoid opening** unrequested email attachments or suspicious links
- **Watch for shoulder surfers**—be aware of who can see your screen or keyboard
- Look for cameras that might capture your typing

### Pre-Travel Research
Investigate your destination country:
- Is the internet censored?
- Are VPNs and circumvention tools legal?
- Is encryption banned?
- Are there laws requiring you to surrender passwords or encryption keys to border authorities?

## Preparing for Border Crossings

### Minimize Data You Carry

**Ask yourself:** What data do I actually need while traveling?

**Recommended approach:**
1. Copy non-essential data to an encrypted computer or drive that stays home
2. Delete that data from travel devices
3. Consider uploading encrypted data to secure cloud storage and traveling with a minimal device
4. Download needed data after arrival; re-upload before departure

**Why this matters:** Data you don't carry cannot be lost, stolen, or discovered in a border search. Some content that's legal at home may be illegal elsewhere (certain media, images, etc.).

### Use a Temporary Email Account

1. Log out of all regular email accounts
2. Create a new web-based email address specifically for the trip
3. Use this for non-sensitive communications only
4. Dispose of the account after returning

**Why this matters:** If the temporary account is compromised, attackers gain nothing valuable beyond your trip duration.

### Remove Accounts from Devices

- **Log out** of all accounts before crossing borders
- **Remove or hide** social media and sensitive apps
- **Consider creating** alternative social media profiles with innocuous content (populated weeks in advance)

**Why this matters:** Border agents may inspect social media accounts found on your devices.

### Clean Up Browsers and Apps

**Web browsers:**
- Log out of all accounts
- Clear temporary files and browsing history

**Chat applications:**
- Search for and remove sensitive conversations
- Rename groups to non-descriptive names (if you're admin)
- Enable disappearing messages for sensitive chats
- Consider whether having no messaging apps might appear suspicious

### Hide Sensitive Files (Advanced)

| Platform | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Android** | Use Safe folder in Files app, or use Tella (can be camouflaged as a calculator) |
| **iOS** | Use Hidden album for photos, or use Tella |
| **Computer** | Create hidden VeraCrypt volume |

## Using Encryption

### Check Legal Status First

Encryption import, export, or use is **illegal in some countries**. Research your destination's laws before traveling.

**If encryption is illegal:** Upload encrypted data to secure cloud storage, delete from devices, and download after arrival.

### Encrypt Your Devices

- Enable **full-disk encryption** on all devices
- Encrypt **external storage devices** (USB drives, portable hard drives)
- Understand that encryption makes data recovery harder if hardware fails—maintain good backups

### Protect Cryptographic Keys (Advanced)

If you use PGP encryption:
- Your private keys are extremely valuable targets
- Never leave devices containing keys unattended
- Consider storing keys on an encrypted USB drive kept on your person at all times

## Using Public Access Points

### Shared Computers

**Treat all shared computers as compromised.**

**Mitigation strategies:**
- Bring a small USB keyboard to defeat hardware keyloggers
- Travel with a **live USB** containing your own trusted operating system
- Boot from the USB to bypass the untrusted computer entirely

### Untrusted Networks

Hotel, airport, and public WiFi networks are vulnerable to machine-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks that can intercept your traffic.

**Protection measures:**
- Configure your browser to **use HTTPS only**
- Install a **trusted VPN** before traveling
- Use **protective browser extensions** (uBlock Origin, etc.)
- **Pay attention to certificate warnings**—they may indicate an attack (though absence of warnings doesn't guarantee safety)

## Internet Censorship

If traveling to countries with internet restrictions:

1. Learn how internet censorship works
2. Research circumvention tools appropriate for your destination
3. **For high-risk situations:** Contact [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) or [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help) for country-specific guidance

## Mobile Phone Security

### Consider a Travel-Only Device

**Best practice:** Leave your primary phone at home and travel with a different device that doesn't contain your data.

**If you must bring your work phone:**
1. Back up all data to a separate device
2. Remove sensitive data from the phone
3. Follow platform-specific hardening guides

### Use Secure Messaging

Communicate through end-to-end encrypted messaging apps for:
- Text messages
- Voice calls
- File transfers

### Hide Sensitive Apps (Advanced)

| Platform | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Android** | Use Private Space feature |
| **iOS** | Use app hiding/locking features |

- [ ] Avoid shared computers when possible
- [ ] Keep devices physically secure
