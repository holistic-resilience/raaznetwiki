# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Social Media

We use social media platforms to connect with others and spread messages we find important. At the same time, the companies managing these platforms often have a business model based on using our communications and contacts to make money. They share information about us with third parties—like our buying habits, social connections, location, birthday, and other identifying information—often without us realizing it. When you use their services, you agree to their terms, which permit them to share or sell this information. Sometimes it is not clear how public that information will become.

Furthermore, social media platforms are actively monitored by governments and law enforcement agencies. The authorities collect publicly available data and, when possible, gather "metadata" about that data, which we may not think about them gathering (like when a photo was taken, or who is connected to whom). They may also ask social media companies to turn over private information about individuals of interest, often including human rights defenders or activists.

While this guide only provides instructions for certain social media platforms, many companies running social networking platforms behave in similar ways and offer comparable options. We recommend reviewing your account settings and taking the same steps to protect yourself on each of your social media accounts.

## Privacy-Focused Alternatives

### For Private Group Communication

- **Small groups**: Move to [secure chat apps](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications) like [Signal](https://signal.org/) or [Delta Chat](https://delta.chat/)
- **Larger private groups**: Consider setting up a [Mattermost](https://mattermost.com/) or [Rocket.chat](https://www.rocket.chat/) instance

### For Public Broadcasting

- **Microblogging** (X alternatives): [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about) or [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) — see [our blog post on leaving X](https://securityinabox.org/en/blog/x-odus)
- **Blogging platforms**: [Noblogs](https://noblogs.org/), [Blackblogs](https://blackblogs.org/), or [Shelter](https://www.shelter.is/services.html)
- **Mailing lists**: [Riseup](https://riseup.net/en/lists) or [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)
- **Newsletters**: [Autistici/Inventati](https://www.autistici.org/services/lists)

### A Note on Telegram and WhatsApp

Telegram and WhatsApp are commonly used for organizing and spreading messages. However, communications on these platforms cannot be considered fully secure, as the companies managing them have commercial agendas similar to social media platforms. If you use these apps, read our guides on using [Telegram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/telegram) or [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) more securely.
