# Stopping Spam Received via Google Calendar

## Introduction

Sending spam via Google Calendar is one of the new methods used by internet scammers and cybercriminals to deceive users. This method has increased significantly in recent years.

## Why Does This Problem Exist?

One of the features of Google Calendar is the ability to share Events with others. Normally, you can register your friends' email addresses to inform them of a special occasion so that event details along with a reminder are sent to them.

**The Main Problem:** Cybercriminals abuse this feature to bypass spam filters in email services and redirect users to fake websites. Since event reminders are displayed as valid notifications in the mobile app, the probability of deceiving users is high.

---

## Deactivation Steps

### Step 1: Accessing Settings

1. Open Google Calendar from [calendar.google.com](https://calendar.google.com/)
2. Click on the Settings icon (gear) at the top of the page
3. Select the **Settings** option

### Step 2: Configuring Invitations

1. In the **General** section, click on **Event settings**
2. In the **Automatically add invitations** section, select the following option:

   > **No, only show invitations to which I have responded**

### Step 3: Deactivating Automatic Events from Gmail

1. In the **General** section, click on **Events from Gmail**
2. **Disable** the following option:

   > **Automatically add events from Gmail to my calendar**

---

## Conclusion

By performing these steps, unwanted events and spam will no longer be automatically added to your calendar.
