# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Facebook

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Facebook account, protect your privacy, and handle potential abuse.

> **Note:** Also see guides on [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

> **Important Consideration:** Commercial, corporate products may not share your values and could remove or filter content they believe violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they don't operate in your language.

## Inform Yourself About Facebook's Policies

### Learn How Facebook Uses Your Information

- Review a clarification of Facebook's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/182)
- [Learn about Facebook's privacy policy](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/explanation/)
- Review Facebook's [terms of service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms/preview) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

**Why this matters:** It's often unclear what online service providers will do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these practices. You can also install [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see policy overviews for services you use.

### Learn What Facebook Will Turn Over to Governments

- [Meta's policy on law enforcement requests](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Meta's transparency report on government data requests](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [Meta's transparency report on content restrictions](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [All Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

**Why this matters:** Social media sites may provide your information—including information you intended to keep private—to governments or law enforcement when requested. Understanding when and how this happens helps you make informed decisions about what to share.

## Don't Use the Mobile App

**Recommendation:** Access Facebook through a browser instead of the mobile app.

**Why this matters:** An app that constantly gathers information can violate your privacy in many ways, even when you deny permissions for location, microphone, etc. Using a browser ensures Facebook isn't constantly connected to a device you carry everywhere.

## Create a New Account

You can [create a Facebook account](https://www.facebook.com/help/188157731232424) for free.

### Decide on Your Identity Strategy

**Using real vs. fake names:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your network and IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for different campaigns and activities (personal vs. work, at minimum)
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency so your network can verify who you are
- Be aware of Facebook's [name policy](https://www.facebook.com/help/1090831264320592)—they may remove accounts using names not used in everyday life. Choose names their algorithms will recognize as plausible if not using your real name

**Why this matters:** Some people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with less risk to their safety or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email on a [privacy-friendly server](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)
- If protecting your identity, use [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address) when setting up the email

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number

If possible, set up your account with only an email address—it's harder to associate with you than a phone number.

**Why this matters:** Your phone number can easily identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could request information from providers using your number, or whether someone seeking to harass you might exploit it.

### Skip "Find Friends"

- Dismiss requests to access contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share employer or educational background—social media may use this to share your profile unexpectedly

**Why this matters:** Social media uses your contact lists to find connections, which can be dangerous when you want to keep contacts hidden. Consider whether law enforcement could use these lists against you, or what happens if social media reveals your associations publicly.

### Designate an Account Manager

- Facebook won't give others access to your account, but you can designate someone to ["memorialize" your account](https://www.facebook.com/help/275013292838654/)
- For emergency access, arrange to share login information via [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested and you need to suspend their account, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact)
- [Remove accounts for medically incapacitated persons](https://www.facebook.com/help/480409628639043)

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test downloading your data from Facebook. Review what's provided, in what format, and how you can use it.

**Why this matters:** Before storing data on a large platform, understand what it takes to move your data out. Complete deletion from servers may take considerable time.

## Protect Your Account

### Check Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Check your recovery email](https://www.facebook.com/help/162801153783275/)
- [Check your recovery phone number](https://www.facebook.com/help/124895950923762/)
- Change immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters:** Attackers may change recovery information to gain control later. Secure your recovery methods, as adversaries may hack them to change your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone accessing your Facebook account gains access to extensive information about you and your connections.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Set Up Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

- Use a security key, authenticator app, or security codes
- Avoid SMS or phone calls—mobile companies have full access, and these methods are easier to compromise
- [Learn about 2FA on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/148233965247823/)
- Complete the ["How to keep your account secure" checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)

See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for more details.

### Save Recovery Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://www.facebook.com/help/148104135383285) for signing in without your usual 2FA method
- Print and store codes somewhere secure, like a locked safe

### Consider Advanced Protection

[Learn about Advanced Protection](https://www.facebook.com/help/1052552578831700)—it provides stronger security for particularly visible people, including monitoring for potential hacking threats. It's only available to people selected by Facebook; if eligible, you'll see prompts to enroll.

### Beware of Phishing

- [Learn about suspicious emails and messages](https://www.facebook.com/help/225602007465207)
- [Check recent Facebook security emails](https://www.facebook.com/recent_emails/security)

**Why this matters:** Phishing messages try to trick you into giving account access. Never click links in security emails—log in directly to your account to verify legitimacy.

### Create an Alternative Identity

To use Facebook without connecting to your real identity:
- Create your account through [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use [an email address created using Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Check Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Security Events

Check which devices have recently logged in—does every login look familiar? If you see suspicious activity:
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

**Why this matters:** Adversaries may log in from their devices. You can review security issues in the Password and security section of your Accounts center.

### Enable Login Notifications

Get alerts when someone tries logging in from an unusual device or location—useful if you suspect your account is at risk.

## If You Think Your Account Has Been Hacked

1. [Perform a security checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/review/?review_id=573933453011661)
2. [Follow steps to secure a hacked account](https://www.facebook.com/hacked) or use the [step-by-step walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/help/1306725409382822)
3. If standard procedures don't work, see [Digital First Aid Kit: Lost Account Access](https://digitalfirstaid.org/topics/account-access-issues)

### Review Connected Apps and Sites

- Avoid using Facebook to log into other sites—use separate passwords saved in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- Be careful connecting accounts—you may be anonymous on one site but exposed on another
- [Manage privacy settings for connected apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/218345114850283/)

**Why this matters:** Connected services can be exploited by hackers to access your accounts.

## Decide What Information to Share

- [Choose who you share content with](https://www.facebook.com/help/325807937506242#choose-who-you-share-content-with)
- Complete [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264)
- [Set up regular Privacy Checkup reminders](https://www.facebook.com/help/443357099140264#set-up-privacy-checkup-reminders)
- [Manage what others can see about you](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/dialog/how-to-clean-your-profile?helpref=privacycenter)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

Consider avoiding:
- Personally identifying information
- Information revealing where you live (e.g., pictures of your house)
- Details about family members
- Information on sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional considerations:**
- Even trusted contacts can inadvertently spread your information
- Agree with your network on what to share for safety
- Be sensitive about revealing information about friends; ask them to reciprocate

**Why this matters:** The more you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. "Likes" or other interactions with certain pages could attract government attention in some regions.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location services work on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509/)
- If using the app, turn off location services
- [Don't add location to posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/337244676357509#adding-or-sharing-your-location)

**Why this matters:** Stopping location storage prevents tracking of your current or past locations and extends battery life.

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Never post images showing:**
- Sensitive locations or identifiable backgrounds
- Information that could identify others without consent

**Actions:**
- [Delete uploaded photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/208547132518386/)
- [Control shared album visibility](https://www.facebook.com/help/410653545719324)
- [Delete albums with all photos](https://www.facebook.com/help/152153388188974)
- [Remove shared album contributors](https://www.facebook.com/help/589394671112683)
- [Remove metadata before posting](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)

**Why this matters:** Photos and videos can unintentionally reveal information. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and camera used.

### Manage "Likes" Visibility

- [Change "Who can see your social interactions alongside ads"](https://www.facebook.com/adpreferences/ad_settings) to "Only Me"
- [Understand how video interactions are visible](https://www.facebook.com/help/410629549531206)

**Why this matters:** In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when reacting to content.

### Don't Share Your Real Birthday

You can register with a different birthday and change it later—just track what you enter for account recovery purposes.

**Why this matters:** Your actual birthday can be used to identify you.

## Decide Who Can See What

### Share with Select People

- Complete the ["Who can see what you share" walkthrough](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/)
- Set "Profile information" to "Only me"
- Consider "Limit past posts" to restrict visibility of older posts
- [Check your activity log](https://www.facebook.com/settings/)
- [Change who can see your apps](https://www.facebook.com/help/201750739897196)

### Manage Replies

- [Choose who can comment on Page posts](https://www.facebook.com/help/369765040737128)
- [Hide comments](https://www.facebook.com/help/297845860255949)
- [Stop people from posting on your profile](https://www.facebook.com/help/115469971891543)
- [Control visitor posts on your Page](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/356113237741414)

**Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims about human rights defenders' associations. Controlling replies can also reduce harassment-related stress.

### Manage Group Membership and Connections

- [Control who can add you as a friend](https://www.facebook.com/help/217125868312360)
- [Choose who can follow you](https://www.facebook.com/help/201148673283205)
- Check [whether groups are public or private](https://www.facebook.com/help/2580077045343981)
- Be aware that [third-party apps can access groups](https://www.facebook.com/help/261149227954100)
- [Leave a group](https://www.facebook.com/help/172944012764072) (note: past public membership may remain visible)
- [Remove or ban group members](https://www.facebook.com/help/211909018842184)
- [Delete group posts or comments](https
