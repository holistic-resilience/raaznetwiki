This guide helps you reflect on reasons for creating alternative online identities and provides methods to secure these identities, making it difficult to connect them to your private life or other online personas.

## Develop Your Strategy

Strategies for maintaining separate identities range from full transparency to full anonymity. Depending on your risk level, situation, needs, and goals, you can choose from three main approaches.

### Real Identity

Publishing content under your official name makes sense when your identity provides credibility and trust. Being transparent about who you are doesn't mean exposing your entire private life online.

**Example approach:** Use your documented name for professional contributions to media outlets while maintaining private social media profiles with a pseudonym for friends and family.

The key is consistently applying practices to keep your identities separate.

### Persistent Pseudonymity

Using an alternative name consistently over time for certain activities allows you to:
- Mitigate threats like political or economic retribution
- Develop an online reputation connected to your alternative name
- Allow others to assess your trustworthiness in trust-based communities

You can maintain multiple pseudonyms for different purposes—for example, separating your activist identity from a hobby-related nickname.

> **Collective identities:** An alternative identity can be managed by a group. While this offers collaboration benefits, you'll need strong coordination practices and security measures to avoid exposing individuals to risks created by others' choices.

### Anonymity

Total online anonymity is difficult to achieve but essential in high-risk situations that could compromise your freedom, wellbeing, or endanger close contacts.

Unlike persistent pseudonymity, complete anonymity involves using short-lived identities to achieve specific goals without leaving traces. For example, a whistleblower might use anonymization tools to communicate with journalists, then delete all accounts created for that purpose.

> **Warning:** In some countries, authorities may interpret anonymization as evidence of wrongdoing. Consider whether you could achieve your goals without using the internet at all.

Learn more: [[Anonymizing Your Connections]]

### Strategy Comparison

| Strategy | Risk to You | Reputation Building | Effort Required |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Real Identity** | Higher | Strong | Lower |
| **Persistent Pseudonymity** | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate |
| **Anonymity** | Lower | Minimal | Higher |

## Check What Information Exists About You Online

Before creating alternative identities, research what the internet reveals about you—an activity called "self-doxing."

**Recommended resources:**
- [Access Now Helpline's guide to exploring your online traces](https://guides.accessnow.org/self-doxing.html)
- [New York Times Open Team's guide to finding and removing personal information](https://open.nytimes.com/how-to-dox-yourself-on-the-internet-d2892b4c5954)

> **Why this matters:** Doxing involves gathering information about someone using all available sources to publish it. Malicious actors may do this to identify valuable information about their targets. Self-doxing helps you discover what's available and delete it before it can be used against you.

## Remove Unwanted Information and Old Identities

If you find images, videos, or information that could connect your identities, contact the relevant websites and platforms to request removal.

### Steps to Take

1. **Document everything:** Create a list of all content you want removed, following [Digital First Aid Kit documentation guidelines](https://digitalfirstaid.org/documentation/)

2. **File takedown requests:**
   - Request Google to [remove personal contact information from search results](https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/12719076?hl=en)
   - EU residents can request removal of results containing their name from Google searches

> **Why this matters:** Using an identity for extended periods creates information that could be used to profile or attack you. Consider each identity—even those using your official name—as potentially disposable. Remove information, pictures, videos, and old accounts when necessary.

## Map Your Social Domains

Map your social domains to identify overlaps and potential cross-domain attack vectors.

### Process

1. **Identify your networks:** Think about different activities and networks, both online and offline
2. **Assess sensitivity:** Consider how sensitive each domain is
3. **Plan separation:** Determine how to keep sensitive domains separate from other networks
4. **Develop identities:** Create online identities needed to separate sensitive domains from other activities

> **Why this matters:** Each person belongs to multiple social domains—workplace, activist organization, family, friends, sports teams, neighborhood, gaming communities. We tend to secure some networks more than others.
>
> **Example threat:** If an activist discusses their hobby on social media, someone wanting to spy on them might send an infected PDF about that hobby. This attack only works if the person uses the same identity for everything. Keeping social domains separate helps secure sensitive activities.

## Create and Secure an Alternative Identity

### Choose a Name

Select a name that social media platforms will find credible as an official name. Some platforms (notably Facebook) require users to register under "the name they use in everyday life."

**Important considerations:**
- Research thoroughly to ensure no one else uses your chosen name
- Keep in mind the risk of account suspension if using a pseudonym
- Plan alternative communication methods if you lose social media access

### Get a Different Phone Number

Phone numbers often link to official identity through registration requirements, making them a weak point for tracing alternative identities.

**Options:**
- **Pre-paid SIM cards:** In some countries, you can buy already-registered cards without showing documents
- **Online calling services:** [Hushed](https://hushed.com/), [Google Voice](https://voice.google.com/about), or [Twilio](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/phone-numbers)

### Hide Your IP Address

To prevent your ISP, email provider, and online platforms from linking your official identity to alternatives, hide your IP address when creating and managing accounts.

**Options:**
- **VPN:** Use a trusted VPN, but remember the provider can see your online activity
- **Tor:** For stronger protection, anonymize connections with Tor

Learn more: [[Choosing a Trusted VPN]] | [[Anonymizing Connections with Tor]]

### Create a New Email Account

Register a new email account for your alternative identity. Options include:
- Creating a completely new account
- Creating an alias from your current email
- Using disposable email services for temporary needs

**Critical:** Use a tool to hide your IP address when creating the account, and always use the same tool when checking mail.

Learn more: [[Secure Email Guide]]

## Keep Your Identities Separate

### Basic Practices

Apply these practices regardless of which approach you choose:

- **Private profiles:** Share personal details only on profiles accessible to selected contacts
- **Event information:** Consider whether shared information could identify or endanger someone; ask permission before writing about individuals; post only after events conclude
- **Account security:** Use [strong, unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords) and [two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for every account
- **Browser security:** [Reduce tracking when browsing](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)

### Remove Identifying Information from Files

Delete metadata and identifying information from images, videos, and other files before uploading.

Learn more: [[Destroying Identifying Information in Files]]

### Maintain Account Separation

Each identity should have separate accounts registered through different:
- IP addresses
- Phone numbers
- Email addresses

### Account Management Best Practices

- **Use dedicated tools:** Manage each identity through its designated device, browser, or app
- **Review privacy settings:** Know what's public, who can see posts, who can contact you, and who can look you up
- **Limit profile information:** Profile pictures and cover photos are generally publicly available regardless of privacy settings
- **Create distinct personas:** If developing a character, create a full story and psychological attitude different from your own
- **Avoid cross-connections:** Don't let different identities follow each other; avoid having the same contacts follow multiple identities; don't repost or like content between accounts
- **Manage location data:** Double-check settings, especially on photo and video sharing sites; GPS and network data can reveal location
- **Remove metadata:** Delete location and other metadata from photos and videos before publishing
- **Vary posting times:** Publish from different identities at different times; use scheduled posting features

## Compartmentalize Using Different Devices or Environments

### Computer Options

#### Separate Devices
Use a different computer for each identity. Ensure all devices receive security updates.

#### Bootable USB Systems
- **[Tails](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tails):** Free, open-source OS that runs from USB, connects via Tor, and leaves no trace. Use different Tails sticks for sensitive identities.
- **[Ubuntu Live USB](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LiveUsbPendrivePersistent):** Create a persistent live USB for separate identities. Note: Not encrypted by default; keep in a safe place.

#### Virtual Machines and Sandboxing
- **[Qubes OS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/tools/#qubes-os):** Security-oriented OS creating isolated compartments (qubes) for each identity. Check [system requirements](https://www.qubes-os.org/hcl/).
- **[Whonix](https://www.whonix.org/):** Anonymous virtual machine routing all traffic through Tor
- **Standard VMs:** [VirtualBox](https://www.virtualbox.org/), [VMware](https://www.vmware.com/products/desktop-hypervisor/workstation-and-fusion), or [KVM](https://www.redhat.com/en/topics/virtualization/what-is-KVM). Resource-intensive; doesn't change IP address; run VPN or Tor before starting.
- **[Sandboxie](https://sandboxie-plus.com/):** Windows sandboxing tool; less resource-intensive but doesn't change IP address

#### User Accounts
For protection against casual inspection (not sophisticated attacks), create different user accounts for each identity.

#### Browser Separation
- Use different browsers for different identities
- Use different browser profiles within the same browser
- Install compartmentalization extensions:
  - [Firefox Multi-Account Containers](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/multi-account-containers/)
  - [SessionBox for Chrome/Chromium](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/sessionbox-multi-login-to/megbklhjamjbcafknkgmokldgolkdfig?hl=en)

### Phone Options

#### Separate Devices
Dedicate a separate phone to each identity. Ensure devices receive security updates.

#### Android User Accounts
For protection against casual inspection, create different user accounts on tablets or phones.

#### Android Sandboxing
[Shelter](https://f-droid.org/packages/net.typeblog.shelter/) (F-Droid only) compartmentalizes an alternative identity in the Android work profile. Note: Doesn't change IP address or protect against sophisticated attacks.

#### Mobile Browsers
- Use different browsers for different identities
- [Tor Browser](https://www.torproject.org/) (Android) or [Onion Browser](https://onionbrowser.com/) (iOS) for managing identities with different IP addresses

> **Why compartmentalize:** Separating identities across devices, profiles, or sandboxes prevents human errors (like replying from the wrong account) and makes it harder to connect identities through [device or browser fingerprinting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Device_fingerprint).

## Use Anonymous Payment Methods

For online purchases under a pseudonymous identity, use payment methods not connected to your official name and bank account:

- **Prepaid cards:** Purchase with cash at supermarkets
- **Virtual credit cards:** Some banks offer cards that hide your identity from sellers
- **Cryptocurrencies:** More complex to manage but offer strong anonymity

## Manage Multiple Identities in Physical Spaces

If your alternative identity's activities extend beyond the online sphere, plan how to present yourself physically.

### Considerations

- **Appearance:** Disguise yourself with different looks or masks
- **Trusted spaces:** Only show your face in spaces where photography and video are prohibited
- **Surveillance awareness:** Routes to trusted spaces may be monitored by security cameras
- **Disposable identities:** Create temporary identities for new acquaintances; assess trustworthiness before including them in more trusted networks

## Advanced: Create an Anonymous Website

### Domain Registration Privacy

- Check whether your domain registrar offers privacy protection
- Find providers offering this option for free
- Note: [Some top-level domains don't support privacy protection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_privacy#Privacy_forbidden)

### Maximum Anonymity

Register your domain through [Njalla](https://njal.la/), an anonymous registrar accepting:
- Encrypted requests
- Cryptocurrency payments

> **Why this matters:** Anyone looking up your domain can find its owner unless your registrar offers privacy protection. This may be included in basic packages, cost extra, or be unavailable depending on the top-level domain.
