# Digital Security 101: Border Crossing Module

This module focuses on preparation and expectations for those traversing the U.S. southern border, with particular emphasis on journalists serving border communities.

## Overview

Unlike standard digital security modules, this curriculum begins with a distinct perspective on risk assessment, emphasizing:
- Individual attributes (e.g., nationality)
- Destination considerations when traveling between countries
- Practical interview simulation exercises

The module was developed in collaboration with the [Electronic Frontier Foundation](https://www.eff.org/), the University of Texas at El Paso [Multimedia Journalism Program](https://www.utep.edu/liberalarts/communication/academic-programs/undergraduate/ba-multimedia-journalism.html), and [Borderzine](https://borderzine.com/).

## Session Details

**Estimated Time:** 80-90 minutes

**Prerequisites:** None

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. Identify information assets and physical devices requiring protection when crossing a border
2. Develop strategies for minimizing risk to data stored on devices
3. Identify pressing risks to data from border officials
4. Create a plan for speaking with CBP officials if detained

## Why This Matters

Reporters living and working near the U.S. southern border routinely experience elevated surveillance levels. Learning practical protective steps for prioritized information at key moments is essential for journalists in this region.

## Homework Assignments

### Before Class
- **Required:** Read the CBP handout on device search procedures: "[Border Search of Electronic Devices](https://www.cbp.gov/sites/default/files/assets/documents/2023-Oct/Border%20Search%20of%20Electronic%20Devices%20Tearsheet.pdf)"
- **Optional:** "[Researching the Mexico-US border: a tale of dataveillance](https://shura.shu.ac.uk/33041/3/Meneses-Gutierrez-ResearchingTheMexico-US%28VoR%29.pdf)" by Mitxy Mabel Meneses Gutierrez

### After Class
- **Optional:** Learn device encryption using BitLocker To Go, Disk Utility, or VeraCrypt: "[Encryption toolkit for media makers](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/introduction/)"

## Class Activities

1. **Case Study Analysis:** Analyze why performer Olivia Rodrigo may have been pulled into secondary screening
2. **Asset Identification Game:** Family Feud-style activity identifying physical objects and data assets requiring protection
3. **Interview Simulation:** Practice screening conversations between CBP officers and reporters

## Discussion Questions

- What data would you want to remove from your device before crossing?
- What types of data would be impractical to remove? Why?
