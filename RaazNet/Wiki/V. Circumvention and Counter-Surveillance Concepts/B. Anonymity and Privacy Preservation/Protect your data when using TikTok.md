# Protect Your Data When Using TikTok

Read this guide to increase your privacy and security when sharing and viewing videos or streaming content on TikTok.

> **Important Consideration**: Before relying on commercial, corporate products, consider whether they are secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that violates their policies. They may also struggle to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

---

## Inform Yourself About TikTok's Policies

### Learn How TikTok Uses Your Information

- Review TikTok's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/1448)
- Read [TikTok's privacy policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/eea/privacy-policy/en) (select your region using the button above the title)
- Review TikTok's [Community Principles](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/community-principles) and [Community Guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en)

**Why this matters**: It's often unclear what service providers do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you understand these risks. Consider installing [the Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) for quick overviews.

### Learn What TikTok Shares with Governments

- Review [TikTok's law enforcement guidelines](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/global/law-enforcement/en)
- Check [government removal requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/government-removal-requests-2024-1)
- Review [information requests reports](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/es/information-requests-2024-1)
- Explore [TikTok's transparency center](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/)

**Why this matters**: Social media sites may provide your information to governments or law enforcement when requested—including information you intended to keep private.

## Create a New Account

You can create a TikTok account for free. While TikTok suggests using their app, **using a web browser on a computer is more secure**.

**To create an account via web browser:**
1. Go to the [TikTok home page](https://www.tiktok.com/)
2. Click Login, then Sign up
3. Choose "Use phone or email"
4. Select "Sign up with email" to avoid using a phone number

If you must use the app, see [TikTok's account creation guide](https://support.tiktok.com/en/getting-started/creating-an-account/creating-an-account).

### Decide on Your Identity Strategy

**Consider using a pseudonym:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider separate accounts for different activities (personal vs. work)
- If using pseudonyms, maintain consistency so trusted contacts can verify your identity

**Why this matters**: Some people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with others while reducing risks to their safety or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email on a privacy-friendly server while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- See [suggested privacy-friendly mail services](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Don't Associate Your Phone Number with Your Account

When setting up a new account, skip entering your phone number. TikTok allows account creation with only an email address.

**Why this matters**: Your phone number can easily be used to identify you. Consider whether local law enforcement could use your number to request information about your activities from service providers.

### Don't Allow TikTok to Access Your Contacts

- Skip or dismiss requests to "allow access" to contacts, import contacts, or connect with Facebook friends
- Only connect with people you know and trust
- Don't share employer or educational background in your bio

**Why this matters**: Social media platforms use your contact lists to recommend connections, which can have dangerous effects when you want to keep your associations private.

### Designate an Emergency Account Manager

TikTok does not accept requests from family members to delete or memorialize deceased users' accounts.

- If someone may need emergency access to your account, share login information via [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance
- Review the [Digital First Aid Kit troubleshooter](https://digitalfirstaid.org/arrested/) for additional guidance

### Know How to Export Your Data

Test how to download your data from TikTok. Review what data is provided, its format, and how you can use it.

**Why this matters**: Before storing data on any commercial platform, understand what it takes to move your data elsewhere if needed.

## Protect Your Account

Review [TikTok's recommendations on account security](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety).

### Check Your Recovery Email and Phone Number

- [Verify your recovery email and phone number](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/email-and-phone-number)
- Update this information immediately if you lose access to either

**Why this matters**: Ensure an attacker hasn't changed your recovery methods to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone, as compromising either could allow password changes.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who accesses your TikTok account gains access to information about you and your connections.

- See [how to create and maintain strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)
- If prompted to create a passkey, read [recommendations on using passkeys](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords/#consider-how-and-when-to-use-passkeys)

### Set Up Two-Step Verification (2SV)

- Use an authenticator app and email for verification
- **Avoid SMS verification** if possible—mobile carriers have access to these communications, and SMS is easier to compromise
- Learn about [2-step verification on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety)

See the [guide on two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for more details.

### Beware of Phishing Attempts

- Learn to [identify phishing messages claiming to be from TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/avoid-fraudulent-message-attacks-on-tiktok)
- For business accounts, see [how to avoid phishing attempts](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/how-to-avoid-phishing-attempts)

**Why this matters**: Phishing messages try to trick you into giving access to your account. Never click links in security emails—instead, log in directly to your account to verify any alerts.

## Use TikTok More Securely

- For browsing and basic activities, **use a web browser instead of the app** when possible
- To use TikTok without connecting it to your real identity:
  - Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
  - [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
  - Use an [email address created via Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)
  - Don't add your phone number to your profile

## Check for Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions and Account Activity

Check which devices have recently logged into your account. Does every login look familiar?

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to a [new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

**Why this matters**: If adversaries log into your account from their devices, you may be able to detect this in the Security & permissions section of your account settings.

### If Your Account Has Been Hacked

- [Reset your password and remove suspicious devices](https://support.tiktok.com/en/log-in-troubleshoot/log-in/my-account-has-been-hacked)
- If you can't log in, try [recovering your account through friend verification](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/account-and-user-safety/account-safety#3)

## Decide What Information to Share

- Consider [making your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- Adjust visibility of individual posts by [changing video settings](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/video-visibility)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

**Never share:**
- Personally identifying information
- Content revealing where you live (videos showing your home or surroundings)

**Consider carefully before sharing:**
- Details about family members
- Information about your sexual orientation or activities
- Your email address (consider having separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Additional precautions:**
- Remember that contacts can copy and spread your information beyond your intended audience
- Agree with your network on what should and shouldn't be shared for safety
- Be sensitive about what you reveal about friends, and ask them to reciprocate

**Why this matters**: The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. "Likes" or posts opposing government positions could lead to surveillance or persecution.

### Don't Share Your Location

- [Learn how location works on TikTok](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok)
- [Turn location services off](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#3)
- [Delete existing location data](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/location-services-on-tiktok#4)

### Share Videos Safely

**Before posting, consider what's visible in your videos:**
- Background details that could reveal your location
- People who may not want to be identified
- Sensitive information visible on screens or documents

**Best practices:**
- If posting only private videos, [make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)
- [Remove metadata from videos shot outside TikTok](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files)
- Seek consent before posting content featuring others
- Work with colleagues to establish sharing guidelines

**Why this matters**: Videos can reveal information unintentionally. Many cameras embed hidden metadata about location, date, time, and camera used.

### Hide Your Liked Videos

Your liked videos are hidden by default. [Ensure they remain visible only to you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/exploring-videos/liking#4).

**Why this matters**: In some countries, "likes" have been used in legal cases. Use caution when leaving reactions depending on your legal context.

## Decide Who Can See What

### Control Your Followers

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers) (they won't be notified)
- Consider [blocking problematic users](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/blocking-the-users)
- [Make your account private](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/making-your-account-public-or-private)

### Manage Comments

- [Choose who can comment on your videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#4)—select "No one" to disable all comments
- [Turn off comments for specific videos](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#5)
- [Filter comments requiring your approval](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#3)
- [Delete comments](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/comments#6)

**Why this matters**: Replies have been used to build false claims about human rights defenders' associations. Comments can also be used for harassment.

### Think About Who You Connect With

- [Unfollow people](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/following-and-unfollowing)
- [Remove followers](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/followers-and-following/removing-followers)

**Why this matters**: Your connections reveal information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used in legal cases, even for loose associations.

### Control Mentions and Hashtags

- [Control who can mention you](https://support.tiktok.com/en/using-tiktok/messaging-and-notifications/mentions-on-tiktok#3): Everyone, People you follow, Friends, or No one
- [Report inappropriate hashtags](https://support.tiktok.com/en/safety-hc/report-a-problem/report-a-hashtag)

### Limit Who Can Contact You

- [Learn to control direct messages](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/controlling-your-dms)
- [Choose who can send you DMs](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message)
- [Filter, hide, report messages, or block senders](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/account-privacy-settings/direct-message#3)

### Manage Advertising

- [Learn how ads personalization works](https://www.tiktok.com/privacy/ads-and-your-data/)
- [Turn off personalized ads](https://support.tiktok.com/en/account-and-privacy/personalized-ads-and-data/personalization-and-data)

**Why this matters**: Governments or police might buy advertising data to target you with disinformation or attempt to locate you.

- Avoid logging into TikTok from shared devices (internet cafes, others' devices)
- Never save passwords and delete browsing history on public machines
- Change passwords for any accounts accessed from shared devices as soon as possible
