# Privacy Guides: Independent Privacy & Security Resources

Privacy Guides is a not-for-profit, volunteer-run project that hosts online communities and publishes news and recommendations surrounding privacy and security tools, services, and knowledge.

## Why Should I Care About Privacy?

> "I have nothing to hide. Why should I care about my privacy?"

Much like the right to interracial marriage, woman's suffrage, freedom of speech, and many others, our right to privacy hasn't always been upheld. In several dictatorships, it still isn't. Generations before ours fought for our right to privacy. **Privacy is a human right**, inherent to all of us, that we are entitled to without discrimination.

You shouldn't confuse privacy with secrecy. We know what happens in the bathroom, but you still close the door. That's because you want privacy, not secrecy. **Everyone** has something to protect. Privacy is something that makes us human.

In the modern age of digital data exploitation, your privacy has never been more critical, yet many believe it is already a lost cause. It is not. Your privacy is up for grabs, and you need to care about it. Privacy is about power, and it is so important that this power ends up in the right hands.

## Getting Started: Threat Modeling

Trying to protect all your data from everyone all the time is impractical, expensive, and exhausting. Security is a process, and by thinking ahead, you can put together a plan that's right for you. Security isn't just about the privacy tools you use or the software you download—it begins by understanding the unique threats you face and how you can mitigate them.

This process of identifying threats and defining countermeasures is called **threat modeling**, and it forms the basis of every good security and privacy plan.

## What Are Privacy Tools?

Privacy Guides recommends a wide variety of **privacy tools** spanning software and hardware that you can use to improve your privacy. Many recommended tools are completely free and open-source, while some are commercial services available for purchase.

Switching from mainstream data-hungry software like Google Chrome and Windows to privacy-focused alternatives like Brave and Linux can go a long way towards controlling the information you share with companies and others.

## About Privacy Guides

Established in 2021 due to the difficulty of finding unbiased reviewers in the VPN and privacy space, Privacy Guides is a popular, trustworthy, non-profit website that provides information about protecting your personal data security and privacy.

**Key characteristics:**
- Ad-free recommendations
- Complete editorial independence
- Non-profit and open source
- Frequent updates
- Strict journalistic standards

The team behind this project has been researching privacy and security in the open-source space for over 5 years. The website is free of advertisements and not affiliated with any of the listed providers.

Privacy Guides has been featured in **WIRED**, **Tweakers.net**, **The New York Times**, and other publications as a reliable source for privacy and security knowledge.
