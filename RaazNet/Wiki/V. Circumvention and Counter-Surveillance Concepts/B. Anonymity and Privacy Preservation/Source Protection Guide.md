# Source Protection for Journalists

Journalists working on sensitive subjects must establish source protection practices *before* sources make contact. This guide provides essential resources for creating secure processes that protect both you and your sources from surveillance and legal threats.

## Core Guides

### 1. Preventing First Contact Exposure

**[The First Time They Reach Out: Protect Sources from Themselves](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/first-time-they-reach-out-protect-sources-themselves/)**

The "first contact problem" occurs when a source reaches out over an insecure channel, immediately compromising their confidentiality. This guide covers:
- Proactively advertising secure tip channels
- Helping sources reach out safely from the start

### 2. Secure Messaging with Signal

**[Signal, the Secure Messaging App: A Guide for Beginners](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/signal-beginners/)**

A walkthrough for setting up and using Signal for encrypted communication with sources.

### 3. Building Effective Tip Pages

**[Security Considerations for Confidential Tip Pages](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/security-confidential-tip-pages/)**

Not all tip pages offer equal protection. This guide explains:
- Security properties of different tip channels (email, phone, Signal, SecureDrop)
- How to communicate security trade-offs to sources
- Pitfalls to avoid when designing tip pages

### 4. Working with Whistleblowers

**[Working with Whistleblowers: A Guide for Journalists](https://whistleblower.org/resources/guides/working-with-whistleblowers-a-guide-for-journalists/)**

From the Government Accountability Project, this guide covers:
- Common trends among whistleblowers
- Legal threats they face
- Best practices for secure and effective engagement

### 5. Protecting Your Address Book

**[To Protect Your Sources, Protect Your Address Book](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/to-protect-your-sources-protect-your-address-book/)**

Contact lists can expose source relationships. Learn about:
- How address books leak through cloud backups
- Vulnerabilities to law enforcement requests
- Risk minimization strategies

### 6. Sensitive Reporting Contexts

**[Taking Care with Source Security When Reporting on Abortion](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/abortion-reporting/)**

Since *Roe v. Wade* was overturned in 2022, journalists covering reproductive health face heightened risks. This guide examines:
- Types of data at issue
- Minimizing risk when speaking to sources in targeted spaces

### 7. Guidance for Potential Sources

**[Here's How to Share Sensitive Leaks with the Press](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/sharing-sensitive-leaks-press/)**

Language and resources to help sources understand:
- Security strategy for reaching journalists
- Legal considerations before making contact
- How to use secure tip channels

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Start early**: Establish secure channels before sources need them
- **Advertise clearly**: Make secure contact methods visible and accessible
- **Minimize data**: Protect address books and reduce stored information
- **Know the context**: Adapt practices to specific reporting areas and legal environments
