# Signal's Secure Backup Feature

Losing your phone or deleting the Signal app used to mean saying goodbye to your important messages. Signal has announced a new feature called **Secure Backups** that changes this.

This optional feature allows you to back up and recover your messages if you lose your phone or need to reinstall Signal. As always, backups are end-to-end encrypted, meaning the service operator cannot read your messages.

## Storage Options

| Tier | Storage | Details |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Free** | 100MB | Text messages + last 45 days of messages and media |
| **Paid** | 100GB | $1.99/month for expanded storage |
| **Local** | Device storage | Password-protected backup stored directly on your device |

Signal suggests the free tier will cover the needs of "even heavy Signal users."

### Enabling Backups

> **Note:** This feature is currently rolling out on Android first. iOS and desktop support is coming soon.

**For Android users:**
1. Open Signal
2. Tap the circle icon at the top left to access settings
3. Navigate to **Settings > Backups**
4. Choose between Secure Backup or on-device backup

To access the feature immediately, [sign up for the Signal beta](https://support.signal.org/hc/en-us/articles/360007318471-Signal-Beta).

### Protecting Your Recovery Key

Signal does not retain a copy of the keys to unlock your message backups—only you have access. When you create a backup, Signal provides a **recovery key** that unlocks your backup archive.

**Important:** Store your recovery key somewhere safe, such as a [password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/). If you lose this key, you will not be able to access your backup.
