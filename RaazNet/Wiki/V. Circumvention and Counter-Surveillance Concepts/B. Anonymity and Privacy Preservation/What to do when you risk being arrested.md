# What to Do When You Risk Being Arrested

In some regions, prominent human rights defenders, activists, journalists, and lawyers face a difficult choice: going into exile or risking arrest and unfair legal prosecution. This guide, developed by [the Front Line Defenders Digital Protection team](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/en/programme/digital-protection), provides guidelines to help protect your activities, information, and contact networks by making it harder to extract data from your devices.

> **Note:** If you are preparing to go into exile or travel in risky situations, also read the [guide on protecting your information and devices while traveling](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel).

---

## Assess Your Situation

### Talk to a Lawyer

- **Assess your legal situation:** How likely is detention?
- **Know your rights:** Understand what you may be compelled to disclose and your legal protections.

> **Important:** If violent interrogation or torture is possible, it's better to have empty devices. This allows you to provide passwords without exposing yourself or others to higher risks.

### Map Your Data

Take an inventory of important information across all devices (phones, computers, external hard drives, USB sticks, SD cards) and online services (cloud storage, email accounts).

- Follow recommendations in [the Holistic Security Manual](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/exercises/explore/information-ecosystem.html)
- Document all sensitive information: pictures, documents, PDFs, chats, attachments, login credentials

---

## Prepare for Potential Detention

Follow these steps in order:

1. **Delete** what you don't need
2. **Back up** what you want to keep
3. **Delete** backed-up data from your devices
4. **Disconnect** devices from online storage and email containing sensitive information
5. **Uninstall** apps for online services (if possible); access them only through a browser

### Back Up Important Information

- [Learn secure backup practices](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/)
- Use an encrypted external storage device and/or secure online storage platform
- If you continue producing content, repeat the backup process regularly and delete information afterward

### Minimize Data in Your Devices

- After backup, delete all data—or at least the most sensitive items—from your devices
- Review the [safer travel guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel/) for device cleanup recommendations
- Consider keeping all data in a [secure cloud storage service](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/#encrypted-cloud-services)
- If you must keep files locally, store them in a secure, encrypted app

### Protect Against Malware

If you suspect an ongoing investigation, protect your devices from spyware:

- [Read the malware prevention guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/malware/)

---

## Prepare Your Phone

### For All Phones

- Avoid linking your phone to personal Google or iCloud accounts; create a new account if necessary
- **Disable biometrics**—use at least a 6-digit PIN or an 8+ character password
- Enable automatic updates
- Remove apps that contain private information or connect to sensitive online services
- Use your phone only for less sensitive purposes
- Read guides for [Android](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/android/) or [iOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/ios/) security
- **iPhone:** [Enable Lockdown Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/guide/iphone/iph049680987/ios)
- **Android:** Enroll in [Google's Advanced Protection program](https://support.google.com/android/answer/16339980)

### Recommended: Install GrapheneOS

For enhanced security on Android:

- Acquire a Google Pixel ([8a](https://store.google.com/us/product/pixel_8a) or newer recommended)
- Have a trusted technologist install [GrapheneOS](https://grapheneos.org/)
- Create separate profiles (admin, work, banking, social media)
- Use GrapheneOS's built-in browser and PDF reader

---

## Prepare Your Computer

- Follow security guides for [macOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/mac/), [Windows](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/windows/), or [Linux](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/linux/)
- If storing information locally, [encrypt your hard drive](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/secure-file-storage/#consider-encrypting-your-whole-device)

### Recommended: Use Tails

[Tails](https://tails.net/about/index.en.html) is an operating system that runs from a USB stick or SD card. It leaves no traces on your hard drive or RAM after shutdown and routes all internet traffic through [the Tor network](http://torproject.org/).

- [Install Tails](https://tails.net/install/index.en.html)
- [Get started with Tails](https://tails.net/doc/first_steps/index.en.html)
- [Create persistent storage](https://tails.net/doc/persistent_storage/index.en.html) for encrypted file storage

### Optional: Install Qubes OS

For protection against online attacks, consider [Qubes OS](https://www.qubes-os.org/), which isolates different compartments ("qubes") on your computer. Configure it using [the SecureDrop guide for journalists and human rights workers](https://github.com/freedomofpress/securedrop-workstation).

> **Note:** Qubes OS protects against remote hacking but may not fully protect against physical forensic analysis.

## Review Your Password Strategy

Weak passwords are the weakest point of any security measure.

- [Use strong, unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
- [Use a secure password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- [Enable 2-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) for all accounts
- Back up your 2FA codes securely (e.g., [Proton Authenticator backup guide](https://proton.me/support/back-up-2fa-codes))

---

## Secure Your Communications

### Messaging

- [Read secure messaging recommendations](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat)
- Use apps with end-to-end encryption by default, such as [Signal](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications)

### Internet Connections

- [Browse the web more securely](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)
- Connect only through [a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/circumvention/#choose-a-vpn-service)

### Accessing Online Services

- Use a browser to log in to sensitive accounts, then log out when finished
- Avoid using apps for online accounts to minimize traces on your devices

---

## Prepare Emergency Contacts and Protocols

### Trusted Contacts

- Securely share passwords for your most sensitive accounts (including Google/iCloud) with trusted contacts
- Ask them to suspend your social media and remotely wipe your phone if you're arrested
- Compile a list of digital security helplines to assist your contacts

### Emergency Apps

- **Android:** Configure the [Personal Safety app](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9319337) for emergency actions (calling for help, sharing location, recording video)
- **iPhone:** Use [Emergency SOS](https://support.apple.com/guide/personal-safety/emergency-call-text-iphone-apple-watch-ips4f0cd709b) to alert contacts and share live video or photos

---

## During Detention

- Keep all devices **off** if possible
- Turn on your phone only to call trusted contacts (if permitted), then switch it off without unlocking it

---

## After Release

- **Do not use returned devices** until a security expert confirms they're free of malware
- Be prepared to replace devices entirely if infection cannot be ruled out
