# Reporting in the Field

Journalism happens beyond the desk. Whether covering protests, meeting sources in person, or crossing international borders, field reporting presents unique digital security challenges. This guide collects essential resources for staying safe while on the go.

## Essential Field Security Guides

### Getting Started

**[First Steps for Online Safety](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/first-steps-for-online-safety/)**
Foundation guide covering risk assessment, account security, malware defense, and encrypted communications. Start here if you're new to digital security.

### Mobile Photography & Media

**[Redacting Photos on the Go](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/redacting-photos-on-the-go/)**
Learn to safely redact sensitive information from photos directly on your mobile device before sharing or publishing.

**[How Secure Are Journalists' Favorite Transcription Tools?](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/how-secure-are-journalists-favorite-transcription-tools/)**
Security analysis of five popular transcription services (including Otter.ai), examining how they handle transcript and audio privacy.

### Protecting Your Data

**[Encryption Toolkit for Media Makers](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/introduction/)**
Step-by-step guides for encrypting external storage using BitLocker To Go (Windows), Disk Utility (macOS), or VeraCrypt.

**[Digital Security for Filmmakers: Protecting Your Footage](https://www.digitalsecurity.film/protecting-your-footage)**
Methods for encrypting and password-protecting footage on storage devices, computers, and cloud services. Created in collaboration with Field of Vision.

### Protest Coverage

**[How Journalists Can Protect Themselves While Covering Protests](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/how-journalists-can-protect-themselves-while-covering-protests/)**
Quick guide for U.S. journalists on device security and protecting reporting materials during protest coverage. Addresses digital security alongside physical safety and legal rights.
