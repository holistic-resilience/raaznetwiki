# Lock Down Firefox Add-ons

## Firefox Add-on Developers Targeted in Phishing Attacks

If you use Firefox, Mozilla has [issued a warning](https://blog.mozilla.org/addons/2025/08/01/warning-phishing-campaign-detected/) to add-on developers about an active phishing campaign. 

Browser extensions often have wide-reaching access to users' browsing activity, making it especially important that developers maintain control over their accounts to protect extension users. Attackers are targeting developer accounts through AMO ([addons.mozilla.org](http://addons.mozilla.org/)).

According to Mozilla, these phishing emails are variants on the message:

> "Your Mozilla Add-ons account requires an update to continue accessing developer features."

Mozilla has not yet shared details about the scale of the attacks or how many developers may have been compromised. [Read more at The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2025/08/04/mozilla_add_on_phishing/).

## What You Can Do

### Remove Unneeded Extensions

This is a good reminder to review your browser extensions and minimize risk by uninstalling any you don't need.

**Firefox users:**
1. Click the hamburger menu (three lines) at the top right corner
2. Navigate to "Extensions and themes"
3. Select "Extensions"

**Chrome users:**
1. Click the three-dot menu at the top right
2. Navigate to "Extensions"

### Keep Privacy-Preserving Extensions

We recommend a few browser extensions for boosting your safety when browsing:

- **[Privacy Badger](https://privacybadger.org/)** (Electronic Frontier Foundation) - Blocks many trackers embedded in websites you browse
- **[uBlock Origin](https://ublockorigin.com/)** - Blocks ads that may contain tracking code. You can disable it on websites you'd like to support—or even better, become a paid subscriber!
