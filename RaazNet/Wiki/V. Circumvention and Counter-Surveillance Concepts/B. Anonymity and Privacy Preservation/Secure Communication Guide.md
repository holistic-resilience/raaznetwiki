# Secure Communication

We'd probably object if someone put a microphone and camera in our room and could listen to our conversations at any time. Yet, whether through ordinary phone calls or messages on social media, we often use chat tools that enable the service provider to eavesdrop on our conversations.

For added privacy and security, we recommend using chat tools that are **end-to-end encrypted**—meaning only the participants in the conversation can read the calls and messages. No uninvited guests, not even the service provider, should be able to listen in.

## Encrypted Messaging Guides

### Signal

#### Signal for Beginners
Signal is one of the best end-to-end encrypted messengers available, enabling voice, video, and messaging with other Signal users. Signal differentiates itself by minimizing the amount of information it stores about conversations, such as who spoke to whom and when. This guide walks you through setting up and using Signal for Android and iOS, along with tips on how to use it safely.

#### Locking Down Signal
This guide covers advanced settings that Signal users can leverage to harden the app further, as well as considerations for minimizing risk.

#### Taking Private Photos with Signal
Your phone may back up or store photos to your camera roll, which may not preserve your privacy if you have automatic backups enabled on Google Drive or iCloud. This guide explains how to use Signal to take ephemeral and private photos within the app, so they are never saved to the camera roll unless you choose.

### WhatsApp

#### Upgrading WhatsApp Security
With over two billion users, WhatsApp is one of the most popular chat services in the world. The good news: WhatsApp offers end-to-end encryption. The bad news: Its parent company, Meta, collects a great deal of metadata about users and their contacts, and many of its most useful security features are not enabled by default. This guide describes how to enable stronger security settings within WhatsApp and how to minimize risk when using it.

### Keybase

#### Keybase for Beginners
Keybase is a powerful yet fairly simple end-to-end encrypted file-sharing and chat service. This guide describes how to set up and use Keybase, as well as tips on using it safely.

## Encrypted Email Guides

### Proton Mail

#### Proton Mail like a Pro
Since the 1990s, PGP has been the standard for encrypting messages and files, including over email. However, it is infamously tricky to use, and even experienced users make mistakes. Proton Mail is an email client designed to make PGP easier to work with, particularly when sending encrypted messages with other Proton Mail users. This guide walks you through how to get started.

### Mailvelope

#### Encrypting Email with Mailvelope
Mailvelope offers PGP email encryption and decryption through a web browser add-on, enabling users to send encrypted messages using popular email clients including Gmail and Microsoft Outlook. This guide explains how to set up and use Mailvelope.
