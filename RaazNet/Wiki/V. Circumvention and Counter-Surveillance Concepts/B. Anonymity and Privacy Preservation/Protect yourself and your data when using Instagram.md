# Protect Yourself and Your Data When Using Instagram

Read this guide to learn how to secure your Instagram account, protect your privacy, and handle harassment or abuse on the platform.

> **Note:** Also see guides on [Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook) and [WhatsApp](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/whatsapp) to increase your privacy and security when using other Meta platforms.

Consider whether relying on commercial, corporate products is secure enough to protect your information. Commercial corporations might not share your values and may remove or filter content that they believe violates their policies. It may also be difficult for them to understand your local context, particularly if they do not operate in your language.

## Understand Instagram's Policies

### How Instagram Uses Your Information

Before using any platform, understand what happens to your data:

- Review Instagram's terms of service on [Terms of Service; Didn't Read](https://tosdr.org/en/service/219) for a plain-language summary
- Read [Instagram's privacy policy](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/policy)
- Review their [terms of use](https://help.instagram.com/581066165581870/) and [community standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/community-standards/)

> **Why this matters:** It's often unclear what service providers do with your information. Is your data combined with other information to make inferences about you? Is it sold to other companies? Reading their policies helps you make informed decisions.

**Tip:** Install the [Terms of Service; Didn't Read browser extension](https://tosdr.org/en/sites/download) to see service ratings as you browse.

### What Instagram Shares with Governments

Social media sites may provide your information to governments or law enforcement when requested:

- [Meta's law enforcement request policy](https://about.meta.com/actions/safety/audiences/law/guidelines)
- [Transparency report on government data requests](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [Content restrictions based on local law](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/government-data-requests/)
- [All Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/reports/)

> **Why this matters:** Understanding when and how your data might be shared with authorities helps you make informed decisions about what to post.

## Create a New Account Securely

You can [create an Instagram account](https://help.instagram.com/155940534568753) for free. Take these precautions when setting up:

### Choose Your Identity Carefully

**Consider using a pseudonym if appropriate:**
- Even with a fake name, you may be identifiable by your IP address unless you [use a VPN or Tor](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Consider maintaining separate accounts for personal and professional use
- If using pseudonyms, be consistent—your network needs to verify who they're connecting with

> **Why this matters:** Some people maintain accounts with fake names to organize and connect with others while protecting their free speech, safety, or liberty.

### Set Up with a Fresh Email Address

- Create a new email address on a privacy-friendly server while using [Tor Browser or a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- See suggested [privacy-friendly email services](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-email-providers)

### Avoid Linking Your Phone Number

If possible, set up your account using only an email address. Your phone number can easily be used to identify you and look up your activities.

> **Why this matters:** Law enforcement can request information from service providers using your phone number. Someone seeking to harass or locate you might also exploit this connection.

### Skip "Find Friends" Features

- Dismiss requests to access your contacts, import contacts, or connect other social media accounts
- Only connect to people you know and trust
- Don't share your employer or educational background—platforms use this to suggest your profile to others

> **Why this matters:** Social media uses your contact lists to find and recommend connections. This can be dangerous if you need to keep your contacts hidden. Law enforcement might use these lists to build cases, or your associations could be revealed publicly.

### Designate an Account Manager

Plan for emergencies:
- Instagram allows family members to [memorialize an account](https://help.instagram.com/231764660354188) rather than granting access
- Verified immediate family can [request removal of a deceased user's account](https://help.instagram.com/264154560391256#removing-the-account)
- Share login information with trusted contacts using [secure communication](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/private-communication)
- If someone you know is arrested, contact [Access Now Digital Security Helpline](https://www.accessnow.org/help/) or [Front Line Defenders](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/emergency-contact) for assistance

### Know How to Export Your Data

Before storing data on any platform, understand how to move it out:
- Test the download process for your Instagram data
- Review what data is provided and in what format
- Consider how long complete deletion might take

## Protect Your Account

### Verify Recovery Information

- [Check your recovery email and phone number](https://help.instagram.com/583107688369069) regularly
- Change this information immediately if you lose access to your email or phone
- [Learn more about keeping your account secure](https://help.instagram.com/369001149843369/)

> **Why this matters:** Attackers may change your recovery information to gain control of your account later. Also secure your recovery email and phone—an adversary could hack them to reset your password.

### Use Strong Passwords

Anyone who accesses your Instagram account gains information about you and everyone you're connected to.

- See the guide on [creating and maintaining strong passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/password/passwords)

### Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Recommended methods (in order of security):**
1. Security key (most secure)
2. Authenticator app
3. Backup codes

**Avoid if possible:** SMS or phone calls—your mobile carrier has access to these, and they're easier to compromise.

- [Set up 2FA on Instagram](https://help.instagram.com/566810106808145)
- [Learn more about two-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa)

### Save Backup Codes

- [Get recovery codes](https://help.instagram.com/1006568999411025) for when you can't use your usual 2FA method
- Print codes and store them somewhere secure, like a locked safe

### Consider Advanced Protection

If Instagram identifies your account as vulnerable, you may be invited to enroll in their Advanced Protection program:
- [Learn about Advanced Protection](https://help.instagram.com/945221763907783)
- Respond to enrollment prompts promptly—you may be locked out if you don't enroll in time

### Recognize Phishing Attempts

- [Learn to identify phishing emails and messages](https://help.instagram.com/670309656726033)
- [Verify emails are actually from Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/emails/emails_sent/) in your account settings
- Never click links in security emails—log into your account directly to verify

## Use Instagram More Securely

**Browser vs. App:**
- Consider using a browser instead of the app for browsing profiles, posting, and chatting
- The app is required for some features like Stories

**For Anonymous Use:**
- Create your account through a VPN or [Tor Browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/tools/#tor-browser)
- [Never connect with your actual IP](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/multiple-identities/#hide-your-ip-address)
- Use [an email address created through Tor or VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#exchange-anonymous-emails)

## Monitor for Suspicious Access

### Review Active Sessions

Check which devices have recently logged into your account:
- Look for unfamiliar logins in the Password and security section of your Accounts center
- Run security checks across apps, devices, and emails from Meta services

**If you see suspicious activity:**
1. Immediately change your password to [a new, long, random passphrase](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
2. Use a unique password not used for any other accounts
3. Save it in your [password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)

### Enable Login Alerts

Set up notifications for login attempts from unusual devices or locations—this alerts you immediately if your account may be compromised.

## Control What Information You Share

### Make Your Account Private

- [Learn how to make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/517073653436611)
- [Explore Instagram's privacy tools](https://help.instagram.com/811572406418223/)

### Information to Avoid Sharing

Consider avoiding:
- **Personally identifying information**
- **Location clues** (photos of your home, workplace, regular locations)
- **Family details** (depending on your threat assessment)
- **Sexual orientation or activities**
- **Email addresses** (consider separate sensitive and non-sensitive accounts)

**Best practices:**
- Get consent before posting about others
- Establish guidelines with your network about what's safe to share
- Consider what you reveal about friends that they may not want shared

> **Why this matters:** The more information you reveal online, the easier it becomes for others to identify and monitor you. In some countries, "liking" posts opposing government positions has led to surveillance or persecution.

### Prevent Cross-Posting

- [Turn off automatic sharing to Facebook](https://help.instagram.com/797182937605779/#sharing-your-posts-automatically-to-facebook)
- [Unlink Instagram from other social networks](https://help.instagram.com/536741816349927)

> **Why this matters:** You may be anonymous on one platform but exposed on another. Connected accounts also create additional security vulnerabilities.

### Disable Location Sharing

- [Understand how location works on Instagram](https://privacycenter.instagram.com/guide/location/)
- [Turn off location on iPhone/iPad](https://help.instagram.com/171821142968851)
- For Android, see ["Stop an app from using your device's location"](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6179507)

### Share Photos and Videos Safely

**Before posting, check for:**
- Identifying information in the background
- Location clues
- Other people who haven't consented

**Protect your media:**
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555) for private photos
- [Remove metadata](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/destroy-identifying-information/#remove-metadata-from-pictures-videos-and-other-files) before posting

> **Why this matters:** Photos and videos can reveal information unintentionally. Many cameras embed hidden data about location, date, time, and camera type.

### Understand "Likes" Visibility

[Your likes are always visible](https://help.instagram.com/281388201973414), even on private accounts. In some countries, "likes" have been used as evidence in legal cases.

### Protect Your Birthday

You can register with a different birthday and change it later. Instagram doesn't share your birthday publicly—it's used for age verification. Keep track of what you enter in case you need it for account recovery.

## Manage Who Can See Your Content

### Control Your Audience

- [Remove unwanted followers](https://help.instagram.com/413012278753813) (they won't be notified)
- [Block accounts](https://help.instagram.com/426700567389543)
- [Make your account private](https://help.instagram.com/448523408565555)
- [Remove public content from search engines](https://help.instagram.com/147542625391305)

**Note:** If you share private posts to other platforms, [anyone with that link can see the content](https://help.instagram.com/442837725762581).

### Manage Comments

- [Turn off comments](https://help.instagram.com/1766818986917552)
- [Remove comments](https://help.instagram.com/289098941190483)
- [Delete multiple comments at once](https://help.instagram.com/289376615404536) (app only)

> **Why this matters:** Replies have been used to build false claims about activists' associations. Disabling comments can reduce harassment and stress.

### Curate Your Connections

- [Unfollow people](https://help.instagram.com/286340048138725)
- [Deny follow requests](https://help.instagram.com/207917546007234)

> **Why this matters:** Your connections reveal information about you. In some countries, social media connections have been used as evidence in court cases.

### Control Tags and Mentions

[Manage who can tag and mention you](https://help.instagram.com/345505572238391)—tags could be used to make false accusations about your activities.

### Limit Who Can Contact You

- [Choose who can send direct messages](https://help.instagram.com/296884305019942/)
- [Block accounts](https://help.instagram.com/454180787965921)

### Manage Advertising

- [De-select all ad topics](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/245100253430454)
- [Prevent Instagram from using partner data for ads](https://www.facebook.com/help/instagram/2885653514995517)
- Review [Facebook advertising settings](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook/#manage-advertising)

> **Why this matters:** Governments or police might purchase advertising data to target you with disinformation or locate you.

## Leave No Trace

### On Public or Shared Devices

- Avoid logging into Instagram from shared devices (internet cafes, others' devices)
- Never save passwords on public machines
- Delete browsing history after use
- Change passwords for any accounts accessed from shared devices as soon as possible

### Clear Search History

[Clear your Instagram search history](https://help.instagram.com/354860134605952) regularly.

> **Why this matters:** If your account is compromised or device seized, search history could be used against you.
