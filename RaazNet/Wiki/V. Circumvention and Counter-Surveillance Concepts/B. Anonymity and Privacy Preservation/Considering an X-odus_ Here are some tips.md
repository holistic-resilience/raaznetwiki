# Considering an X-odus? Here Are Some Tips

*By Security in a Box | Published March 18, 2025*

Human rights defenders and civil society members have used Twitter since its creation. [Security in a Box first published a guide on secure Twitter usage in 2013](https://web.archive.org/web/20131114102313/https://securityinabox.org/en/twitter_main), and [that guide still exists](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/x-twitter/) today—though the platform has since been renamed to X.

## Why People Are Leaving X

After Elon Musk purchased Twitter in 2022, significant changes occurred:

- **Restricted access**: X is now [mostly accessible only to account holders](https://xcancel.com/about)
- **Reduced moderation**: The platform [hardly moderates abuse or disinformation](https://www.reuters.com/technology/twitter-exec-says-moving-fast-moderation-harmful-content-surges-2022-12-03/)
- **Dissolved oversight**: The [Trust and Safety Council was disbanded](https://www.reuters.com/technology/twitter-dissolves-trust-safety-council-2022-12-13/)—the advisory group that addressed hate speech, child exploitation, and other platform problems
- **Suppressed external links**: Posts containing links, [especially to news websites, receive reduced reach](https://techround.co.uk/news/musk-limiting-external-links-x/)
- **Algorithmic shifts**: [Increased visibility for right-leaning users](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/10/29/elon-musk-x-republican-democrat-twitter-election/)

As a result, X has become a more toxic environment where harassers have the upper hand. Notable organizations that have left include [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2024/nov/13/why-the-guardian-is-no-longer-posting-on-x), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2023/04/12/1169269161/npr-leaves-twitter-government-funded-media-label), [EDRi](https://edri.org/our-work/why-edri-is-leaving-x-and-where-to-find-us/), and [Statewatch](https://www.statewatch.org/news/2025/january/why-statewatch-is-leaving-x/).

A [campaign to kickstart a massive exodus from X](https://www.escape-x.org/) has begun, and various tools now exist to help you migrate while staying connected with your network.

## What to Consider Before You Leave

Think about how you currently use X:

- Distributing updates on your activities
- Getting news and current events
- Participating in political discourse
- Joining discussions on specific topics
- Sharing videos
- Networking with like-minded people
- Brand promotion

Your primary use case will help determine the best alternative platform for your needs.

## Alternatives to X

### Bluesky

[Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) is currently the most popular X alternative.

**Pros:**
- Founded by Twitter co-creator Jack Dorsey
- Customizable user experience and conversation moderation
- Familiar interface for former Twitter users

**Cons:**
- Still owned by a company—policies could change if sold
- While designed for decentralization, currently operates as a single network (though [efforts are underway to change this](https://freeourfeeds.com/))

### Mastodon

[Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/about) is a federated social networking platform built on free and open-source software.

**Getting started:**
1. Choose a server based on its community, code of conduct, and moderation rules
2. Browse available servers at [joinmastodon.org/servers](https://joinmastodon.org/servers)
3. Check where people you follow on X have migrated
4. Read [Fedi.Tips](https://fedi.tips/) for a non-technical usage guide

### Threads and Other Meta Platforms

[Threads](https://www.threads.net/) is Meta's microblogging platform. Consider it if:
- You focus on brand advertising
- You already use other Meta platforms
- Your X followers are active there

For secure usage, see Security in a Box guides for [Instagram](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/instagram) and [Facebook](https://securityinabox.org/en/tools/facebook).

### Social Media Management Tools

Use a tool that posts to multiple platforms simultaneously without leaving X entirely. Browse options at [topsocialtools.com](https://www.topsocialtools.com/).

### Reddit

[Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/) works well if you primarily follow and join conversations on specific topics. Its upvote system surfaces relevant content, and many discussions are accessible without logging in.

### LinkedIn

[LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/) suits professional networking, job searching, and following industry news.

### RSS Feed Readers

If you use X mainly for news, RSS readers offer a direct connection to content sources:

- [Find RSS feeds for almost any site](https://help.socialbee.com/hc/en-us/articles/29979142036759-How-Can-I-Find-the-RSS-Feed-of-a-Website) (SocialBee guide)
- [Set up RSS in Thunderbird](https://blog.thunderbird.net/2022/05/thunderbird-rss-feeds-guide-favorite-content-to-the-inbox/)
- Use Firefox add-ons like [Feedbro](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/feedbroreader/)

### Your Own Blog or Website

If you have the capacity, maintain your own site to:
- Control your content completely
- Publish an RSS feed for subscribers
- Accept comments for conversation and feedback

### Newsletter or Telegram Channel

Keep your audience updated through:
- Newsletter platforms like [Substack](https://substack.com/) or [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/)
- A [Telegram channel](https://telegram.org/faq_premium) (premium accounts offer additional features)

## Useful Migration Tools

### Cyd
[Cyd](https://cyd.social/) helps you:
- Create a local, private backup of tweets, retweets, likes, bookmarks, and direct messages
- Delete all your tweets after backup
- Migrate data from X to Bluesky (premium feature)

### OpenPortability
[OpenPortability](https://openportability.org/en/auth/signin) migrates your data and contacts from X to Bluesky and Mastodon.

### BlueNotify
[BlueNotify](https://bluenotify.app/) provides notifications for new posts from accounts you follow on Bluesky—a feature the platform doesn't offer natively.

### Xcancel
[Xcancel](https://xcancel.com/about) lets you read X posts without logging in.
