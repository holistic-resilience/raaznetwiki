# Protests: Affinity Groups - Care for Yourself and Others

Coordinating with a group of trusted friends or colleagues can make protest participation more effective and secure. This guide covers tactics for organizing collective action while keeping yourself and others safe.

## Create an Affinity Group

An **affinity group** is a small group of fellow protesters who prepare, carry out, and leave the protest together, interacting with the larger protest as a unit. Members create their security plan together and divide roles for action and emergency response.

### Key Characteristics

- **Size**: 2-15 people (small enough for personal trust and direct care)
- **Duration**: Can form for a specific action or as an ongoing part of a movement
- **Structure**: Can operate autonomously or coordinate with other affinity groups

### Setting Up Your Group

1. **Assign roles** such as:
   - Facilitator
   - Representative/spokesperson
   - Media contact
   - Medical support
   - "Good vibe watch" (morale and emotional support)

2. **Create a decision-making structure** that works for your group

3. **Plan together**: Take time to discuss the action and check everyone's perceptions on security issues

4. **Ensure at least one or two members** have basic first-aid and psycho-social care skills

### During the Action

- Stay in communication throughout
- Respond to emergencies together
- Leave together as a group
- Debrief in a safe location afterward

## Manage Your Well-being

### Before the Protest

- Identify how your body and mind react to unhealthy stress levels
- Determine what practices or resources help restore your mental and physical balance
- Integrate these into your action plan

### Within Your Group

- Foster a culture where members can share fears openly
- Discuss stress and traumatic experiences without judgment
- Practice [active listening](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_listening)
- Build solidarity and care for one another

### During the Protest

- Ensure access to adequate food and water
- Prepare for various weather conditions

### After the Protest

- Debrief with your affinity group in a safe location
- Allow time for group sharing and support
- Take personal time to process the experience and transition back to daily life

## Build an Inclusive Movement

Security extends beyond physical protection—it includes a sense of belonging, solidarity, and mutual respect, especially for those who face discrimination based on gender identity, race, sexual orientation, appearance, or other factors.

### Examine Privilege Within Your Movement

- Notice who holds positions of influence
- Consider whether responsibilities are distributed equitably
- Challenge power imbalances when they emerge
- Create opportunities for those traditionally excluded

### Establish Clear Standards

- Maintain **zero tolerance** for discrimination, sexual harassment, or violence
- Make consequences clear for those who engage in harmful behavior
- Never treat these issues as material for jokes

### Plan Inclusively

- Recognize that people facing discrimination may need specific protection measures
- Actively include marginalized members in security planning
- Consider how your tactics affect all participants
