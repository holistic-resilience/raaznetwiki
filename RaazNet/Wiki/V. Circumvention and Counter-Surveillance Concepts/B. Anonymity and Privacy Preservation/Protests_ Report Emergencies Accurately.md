# Protests: Report Emergencies Accurately

At protests or demonstrations, witnesses may observe human rights abuses by authorities or opposing groups. Coordinating an effective response requires accurate reporting—a task that becomes difficult when experiencing fear or distress.

## Essential Information to Report

When reporting an emergency, gather and communicate the following details:

- **Your identity and location** – Who are you, and where are you?
- **What happened** – Describe the incident clearly
- **Location of incident** – Where exactly did it occur?
- **Time of incident** – When did it happen?
- **Affected individuals** – Who was impacted?
- **Perpetrator identification** – Who was responsible, and how do you know?
- **Immediate needs** – What assistance is required?
- **Contact information** – How and when can you be reached?

> **Tip:** Practice these reporting scenarios before the protest if emergencies seem likely.

## Responding to Arrests

If someone is arrested during the protest:

1. **Document the arrest details:**
   - Time and location of arrest
   - Arresting officer's name or ID number (usually printed on uniforms)
   - Vehicle descriptions and license plates

2. **Disseminate information** to affinity groups, human rights organizations, and media contacts

3. **Conduct a roll call** if the arrested person was part of your group to confirm everyone else's safety

4. **Follow up on custody details:**
   - Confirm the detained person's location
   - Obtain information about their medical condition
   - Learn what charges have been filed

5. **Coordinate release efforts** – If arrest was anticipated, activate your pre-planned strategy involving national or international human rights groups

6. **Regroup and adapt** – Reassign duties and adjust plans if continuing the action
