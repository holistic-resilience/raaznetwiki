# Should You Try Google Advanced Protection?

Journalists and other at-risk users sometimes need stronger protections than those in a phone's default settings. Starting with iOS 16 in 2022, Apple offered users its hardened [Lockdown Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-us/105120), which limits security risks by suspending certain device features known to be exploited in previous attacks—such as link previews in Messages—and restricting certain web technologies when browsing.

Now, with Android 16 released this month, Google is expanding its long-standing Advanced Protection program to include hardened security settings as well.

## Key Features of Advanced Protection

You can see [the full list of features](https://security.googleblog.com/2025/05/advanced-protection-mobile-devices.html) affected by Advanced Protection. Notable highlights include:

- **End-to-end encrypted security logging** to help facilitate investigations of potential device breaches
- **USB connection blocking** for unknown devices (useful if your device is stolen or seized)
- **Automatic reboot after 72 hours** which also encrypts the device
- **Technology restrictions** preventing certain features from activating in apps, web browsing, messages, calls, and network connections

For a detailed analysis, read [this write-up from Thorin Klosowski](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/06/googles-advanced-protection-arrives-android-should-you-use-it) at the Electronic Frontier Foundation.

## What You Can Do

### Decide If It's Right for You

Advanced Protection may break certain websites and change how you use some apps, including messaging apps. However, this trade-off might be worthwhile for the peace of mind that comes with upgraded device security.

### Turn On Advanced Protection (Android)

Not all Android devices have access to Android 16 yet. The update is being rolled out in phases across manufacturers, starting with Google Pixel phones.

**To enable Advanced Protection on Pixel devices:**
1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Navigate to **Security and Privacy**
3. Select **Advanced Protection**

### Consider Lockdown Mode (iPhone)

[Learn more about Lockdown Mode](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2022/07/apple-expands-commitment-to-protect-users-from-mercenary-spyware/) before deciding. From experience, it does break certain websites and has blocked messages from people not previously contacted (particularly Android users). Otherwise, it's been a surprisingly normal experience and a decent security compromise.

**To enable Lockdown Mode:**
1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Navigate to **Privacy & Security**
3. Select **Lockdown Mode**
4. Tap **Turn On Lockdown Mode**
