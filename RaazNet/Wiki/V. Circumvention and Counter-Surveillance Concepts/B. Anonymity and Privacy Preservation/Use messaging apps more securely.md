# Use Messaging Apps More Securely

Messaging apps are widely used for real-time communications, enabling immediate exchange of text, voice messages, files, and video calls. However, many popular apps have privacy and security issues that can expose your communications to risks—messages could be tampered with or accessed by unauthorized third parties.

This guide helps you make informed decisions about which tools work best for your needs and how to use them safely.

> **Related Reading:** For assessing communication sensitivity, see the [guide on protecting the privacy of your online communications](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/private-communication/).

---

## Assess Your Needs

Answer these questions to determine which messaging apps suit your requirements:

### Basic Communication Needs

1. **What do you need to exchange?**
   - Text only, or also voice messages, files, images, and video?
   - Do you need audio/video calling capabilities?

2. **Individual or group communication?**
   - For sensitive information with individuals, most messaging tools work
   - For groups, verify the app supports group messaging and check size limits

3. **Technical requirements?**
   - Can you access the app on all your devices and operating systems?
   - Can you install software, or do you need browser-based access?
   - If browser-based, use a [secure browser](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)

### Registration and Identity

4. **Registration requirements—are you comfortable with them?**
   - If you need to hide your identity, choose tools that don't require phone numbers or email addresses
   - If contact info is required, prefer email over phone number registration
   - Consider decentralized tools for better anonymity
   - [Learn more about anonymous chatting](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

### Security Features

5. **Do you need end-to-end encryption?**
   - Essential for sensitive information shared in real-time
   - Verify encryption is enabled by default or can be activated
   - Confirm group chats and voice/video calls can also be encrypted

6. **Can messages automatically disappear?**
   - Critical for sensitive exchanges—ensures data is deleted from all devices after a set period
   - Protects against device loss, theft, or seizure

### Evaluating App Trustworthiness

7. **Can you trust the messaging app?** Consider these factors:

| Factor | What to Check |
|--------|---------------|
| **Mission** | Was it created for secure communications or commercial purposes? What's the business model? |
| **Data Storage** | What data is stored, and for how long? Check privacy policy and terms of service for logging practices |
| **Legal History** | How have they responded to government data requests? |
| **Location** | Where are headquarters and servers? Will they comply with your country's authorities? |
| **Encryption Software** | What encryption protocols are used? Is the software mature? |
| **Open Source** | Are both app and server open-source? |
| **Audits** | Has it undergone independent security audits? When? Were servers included? |

### Group Chat Considerations

8. **For group communications, consider:**
   - Maximum group size supported
   - Membership control (registration required? invitation-only?)
   - Administration and moderation features
   - Announcement-only vs. full conversation modes
   - End-to-end encryption for groups
   - Video call capabilities
   - Topic-based channel organization

## Use Messaging Tools Safely

Follow these practices regardless of which app you use:

### Register with a Separate Identity

- **Avoid using your phone number** for account creation when possible
- Create separate email addresses for messaging app registration
- Keep messaging apps separate from your usual email and social media accounts

> **Why:** Your phone number is typically connected to your official identity. Separating it from sensitive communications prevents activities from being traced back to you.

**Further reading:**
- [How to choose a username](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/#choose-a-name)
- [Creating multiple online identities](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/multiple-identities/)
- [Anonymous chatting guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/anonymity/#chat-anonymously)

### Enable End-to-End Encryption

- Use apps that protect conversations with end-to-end encryption
- If not enabled by default, manually activate it for sensitive communications

> **Why:** Anyone with access to messaging servers can read unencrypted communications. End-to-end encryption prevents unauthorized access.

### Verify Contact Identities

- Always verify contacts through a second, safer channel
- Options: face-to-face meeting, encrypted email, or different secure platform

### Manage Message Retention

- **Enable disappearing messages** for sensitive conversations
- Set automatic deletion timeframes appropriate to your needs
- **Regularly delete chat history** manually if automatic deletion isn't available

> **Why:** Chat apps keep records of everything shared by default. Disappearing messages limit stored information, protecting you if devices are lost, stolen, or seized.

### Handle Links and Files Carefully

- **Suspicious links:** Don't open unexpected links
- **Unexpected files:** Contact the sender through a different secure channel to verify before opening
- **Sharing files:** Consider uploading to secure file storage and sharing the download link instead

> **Why:** Many malware and spyware infections occur through malicious links or files. Verification prevents phishing attacks.

## Use Group Chats Safely

### Control Group Membership

- Assign admin rights only to [verified contacts](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat/#verify-your-contacts-identities)
- Know everyone you invite to join
- If you can't remove a problematic member, create an entirely new group
- Double-check contact information before sending invitations
- Verify each person's identity through a different trusted channel

### Configure Member Privileges

Review group settings to control who can:
- Write messages and send voice messages
- Add or remove members
- See other members' names and contact information
- Access administrative functions

> **Why:** Proper privilege settings let you use groups as peer discussion spaces or controlled announcement channels, depending on your needs.
