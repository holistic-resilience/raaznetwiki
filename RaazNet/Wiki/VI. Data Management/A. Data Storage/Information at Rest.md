# Information at Rest

Information "at rest" refers to digital files and hard-copies that are being *stored* on devices, paper, or other formats—as opposed to information actively being transmitted.

## Digital Information at Rest

Digital information at rest includes all files stored on devices whenever they are not being transferred or communicated. This encompasses:

- **Documents**: Reports, evidence, proposals, contact information, accounting records
- **Communications**: Emails, attachments, text messages
- **Metadata**: Hidden information embedded within files

### Common Storage Locations

- Computers and laptops
- Phones and tablets
- External hard drives
- USB drives and SD cards
- CDs and DVDs
- Cloud servers
- Email provider servers

> **Note**: Portable devices like smartphones contain data "at rest" even while the device itself is in motion.

## Physical Information at Rest

Hard copies of sensitive data may be stored in:

- Filing cabinets and vaults
- Office desks and shelves
- Wallets and purses
- Books and ledgers

## Mapping Your Information

To protect your information effectively, you must first understand what you have. For each piece of stored information, consider:

- **Location**: Where is it stored?
- **Access**: Who can access it?
- **Sensitivity**: How sensitive is the content to you, your organisation, or people mentioned (e.g., witness or victim statements)?
- **Necessity**: How important is it to keep?
- **Retention**: How long should it be kept?

### Information Mapping Table

Use a table like the one below to catalogue your most important information at rest:

| Information Type | Location | Who Has Access | Sensitivity Level | Retention Period |

Once you have mapped your critical information, you can identify concrete ways to protect it based on its sensitivity and storage location.

## Next Steps

After cataloguing your information at rest, proceed to assess your **information in motion**—data actively being transferred or communicated—to identify vulnerabilities and appropriate protections.
