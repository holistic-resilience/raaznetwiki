# Signal's Secure Backup Feature

Signal has introduced **Secure Backups**, an optional feature that allows you to back up and recover your messages if you lose your phone or need to reinstall the app. As with all Signal communications, backups are end-to-end encrypted—the service operator cannot read your messages.

## Storage Options

| Tier | Storage | Details |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Free** | 100MB | Text messages + last 45 days of media |
| **Paid** | 100GB | $1.99/month |
| **Local** | Device storage | Password-protected backup on your device |

Signal suggests the free tier will cover the needs of "even heavy Signal users."

## How to Enable Secure Backups (Android)

1. Open Signal
2. Tap the circle icon at the top left to access **Settings**
3. Navigate to **Backups**
4. Choose either **Secure Backup** (cloud) or **On-device backup** (local)

> **Note:** This feature is currently available on Android (beta). iOS and desktop support is coming soon.

## Important: Protect Your Recovery Key

Signal does not store a copy of your backup encryption keys. When you create a backup, you receive a **recovery key** that unlocks your archive.

**Store your recovery key safely:**
- Use a [password manager](https://freedom.press/digisec/blog/choosing-a-password-manager/)
- Keep a secure physical copy
- **If you lose this key, you cannot recover your backup**
