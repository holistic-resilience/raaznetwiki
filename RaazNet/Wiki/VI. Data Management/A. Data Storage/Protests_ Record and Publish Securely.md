# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing documentation without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Physical Safety First

Before you start taking pictures or filming:

- Ensure you are in as physically safe a space as possible
- Ideally, have another person recording you while you film (and vice versa) in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries
- Plan this buddy system in advance with your affinity group

## Secure Recording Tools

When using a smartphone, several apps can help you record safely:

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **ObscuraCam** | Blur faces to protect identities |
| **CameraV** | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| **eyeWitness to Atrocities** | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| **Mobile Martus** | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |

## Secure Transmission

When sending recorded files:

- **Avoid MMS messages** — they are not encrypted
- Use end-to-end encrypted messengers like **Signal**
- Consider **GPG-encrypted email** for file transfers
- Use **Orbot** to upload content while protecting your identity

If storing content on an SD card, either encrypt your phone or create an exit strategy — give the card to someone who will take it to safety.

## Publishing Securely

Before publishing or sharing recorded violations, protect identities:

### Remove Identifying Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data that can reveal where, when, and by whom content was captured. Remove this using:

- **Metanull**
- **Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)**
- **CameraV** (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Cover Your Tracks

- Use **Tor Browser** (or **Orbot** on Android) when uploading
- Consider a **VPN service** to access blocked websites and mask your location

### Seek Expert Guidance

If you're concerned about safety and legal implications of sharing gathered information:

- Consult with lawyers familiar with protest documentation
- Connect with human rights organizations for support
- Maintain secure backups of all materials
- Carefully consider whether to retain non-anonymized originals (and how to store them securely)
