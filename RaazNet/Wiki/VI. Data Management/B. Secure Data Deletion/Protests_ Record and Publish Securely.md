# Protests: Record and Publish Securely

If you witness a human rights violation during a protest or demonstration, your first instinct may be to reach for your mobile phone or camera to record it. However, recording and sharing abuses without proper precautions could endanger you or others. Consider the following before you hit record and share.

## Before Recording

**Ensure physical safety first.** Position yourself in as safe a space as possible before taking pictures or filming. Ideally, have another person recording you while you film (and vice versa) in case you become the target of aggression or harassment by police or adversaries. Plan this tactic in advance with your affinity group.

## Secure Recording Tools

If you're using a smartphone, several apps can help you record safely:

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **[ObscuraCam](https://guardianproject.info/apps/obscuracam/)** | Blur faces to protect identities |
| **[CameraV](https://guardianproject.info/apps/camerav)** | Control and manage image/video metadata |
| **[eyeWitness to Atrocities](http://www.eyewitnessproject.org/)** | Record legally verifiable footage and images |
| **[Mobile Martus](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.martus.android)** | Document human rights abuses with encrypted transfer |

## Secure Transmission

**Avoid MMS messages**—they are not encrypted. Instead, use:

- **[Signal](https://whispersystems.org/)** for end-to-end encrypted messaging
- **[GPG-encrypted email](https://securityinabox.org/en/guide/thunderbird/windows)** for secure file transfer
- **[Orbot](https://guardianproject.info/apps/orbot)** to upload content while protecting your identity

**For SD card storage:** Consider encrypting your phone, or implement a "runner" strategy—give the card to someone who will take it to safety.

## Publishing Safely

Before sharing recorded material, take steps to protect identities:

### Remove Identifying Metadata

Pictures and videos contain EXIF data that can reveal where, when, and by whom content was captured. Remove this metadata using:

- **[Metanull](https://securityinabox.org/en/lgbti-mena/metanull/windows)**
- **[Metadata Anonymisation Toolkit (MAT)](https://mat.boum.org/)**
- **CameraV** (can also add verification metadata when needed)

### Anonymize Your Connection

When uploading or sharing material:

- Use the **[Tor Browser](https://torproject.org/)** (or Orbot on Android)
- Use a **VPN service** to mask your location and access blocked websites

## Legal and Safety Considerations

If you're concerned about the safety and legal implications of sharing gathered information:

1. Seek expert advice from lawyers and human rights organizations
2. Maintain secure backups of all documentation
3. Decide whether to retain non-anonymized materials—and if so, store them securely
