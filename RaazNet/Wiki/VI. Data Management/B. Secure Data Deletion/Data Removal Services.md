# Data Removal Services

People search sites operated by data brokers pose significant privacy risks, particularly for Americans. Sensitive personal information—including your address, phone number, email, and age—is often just a simple internet search away.

> **Note for US Residents:** While there is no federal regulation protecting your data from these brokers, many companies will remove your information from their public databases upon request. In other regions, regulations like GDPR already provide stronger protections.

## Why This Matters

Removing your personal data from these sites generally requires *providing* these companies with your personal information to comply with the request. While counterintuitive, this trade-off is usually worthwhile to minimize publicly accessible information about you.

**Quick Test:** Search for your name in quotes plus your general location (e.g., `"Jane Smith" Chicago IL`) using any search engine. You may find your personal information prominently displayed in the results.

---

## Option 1: Manual Opt-Outs (Free)

The most private approach is submitting opt-out requests manually. While hundreds of people search sites exist, most are operated by a small number of companies—so removing your data from major providers often cascades to smaller sites.

### Priority Data Brokers to Check

Search for your information on each site and submit an opt-out request if found:

| Provider | Search | Opt-Out |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Advanced Background Checks | [Search](https://advancedbackgroundchecks.com/) | [Opt-Out](https://advancedbackgroundchecks.com/removal) |
| BeenVerified | [Search](https://beenverified.com/app/optout/search) | [Opt-Out](https://beenverified.com/app/optout/address-search) |
| Intelius | [Search](https://intelius.com/) | [Opt-Out](https://suppression.peopleconnect.us/login) |

For a comprehensive list, see the [Big Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List](https://github.com/yaelwrites/Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List).

### Sustainable Strategy

To avoid burnout:
- **Weekly approach:** Handle one site per week
- **Wait period:** After initial opt-outs, wait 1-2 weeks for propagation
- **Re-check cycle:** Review every 3-4 months, as data often reappears

---

## Option 2: EasyOptOuts (Paid - $20/year)

An automated service that searches data broker sites and submits opt-out requests on your behalf. Re-runs the process every 4 months.

**Key Points:**
- Independent testing indicates high effectiveness and best value
- Consumer Reports ranks it among top-performing removal services
- Does not cover all sites (notably Intelius and PeekYou require manual opt-out)

---

## Option 3: Google "Results About You" (Free)

Google offers a free monitoring tool that alerts you when your personal contact information appears in search results.

**Limitations:**
- Requires sharing personal data with Google
- Only removes listings from Google search—not from source websites
- Limited to 3 addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses
- Available only in select markets (US, UK) for users 18+

**Best Use:** Combine with manual opt-outs or other services to address source websites directly.

---

## Recommended Approach

1. **Start with manual opt-outs** for the major providers listed above
2. **Consider EasyOptOuts** if you need faster, automated coverage
3. **Use Google's tool** as a supplementary monitoring system
4. **Set calendar reminders** to re-check every 3-6 months

For those facing immediate threats from stalkers or harassment, prioritize automated tools and seek professional help from law enforcement or support organizations.
