# What to Do When You Risk Being Arrested

In some regions, human rights defenders, activists, journalists, and lawyers must choose between going into exile or risking arrest and prosecution—often under unfair conditions. This guide, developed by [Front Line Defenders Digital Protection team](https://www.frontlinedefenders.org/en/programme/digital-protection), provides recommendations to protect your activities, information, and contact networks by making it harder to extract data from your devices.

> **Related:** If you are preparing to go into exile or travel in risky situations, see the [guide on protecting information and devices while traveling](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel).

---

## Assess Your Situation

### Talk to a Lawyer

- Assess your legal situation: How likely is detention?
- Know your rights, particularly regarding:
  - Whether you can be compelled to provide passwords
  - What authorities can legally access
  - Your rights during interrogation

> **Important:** If violent interrogation or torture is possible, it is better to have empty devices so you can provide passwords without exposing yourself or others.

### Map Your Data

Take an inventory of important information across all your devices (phones, computers, external hard drives, USB sticks, SD cards) and online services (cloud storage, email accounts).

- Follow recommendations in [the Holistic Security Manual](https://holistic-security.tacticaltech.org/exercises/explore/information-ecosystem.html)
- Create a digital inventory of sensitive information: pictures, documents, PDFs, chats, attachments, login credentials

---

## Prepare for Potential Detention

Follow these steps in order:

1. **Delete** what you don't need
2. **Back up** what you want to keep
3. **Delete** backed-up data from your devices
4. **Disconnect** devices from online storage and email containing sensitive information
5. **Uninstall** apps for online services (if possible); access them only through a browser

### Back Up Important Information

- [Learn secure backup practices](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/)
- Back up to an encrypted external storage device and/or secure online storage platform
- If you continue producing content, repeat the backup process regularly and delete information afterward

### Minimize Data on Your Devices

- After backing up, delete all data—or at least the most sensitive—from your devices
- Review the [safer travel guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/assess-plan/safer-travel/) for device cleanup recommendations
- Consider keeping all data in a [secure cloud storage service](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/backup/#encrypted-cloud-services)
- If you must keep files locally, store them in a secure, encrypted app

### Protect Against Malware

If you suspect an ongoing investigation, protect your devices against spyware and surveillance.

- [Read the malware prevention guide](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/malware/)

## Prepare Your Phone

### For All Phones

- Avoid linking your phone to personal Google or iCloud accounts; create a new account if needed
- **Disable biometrics**—use at least a 6-digit PIN or an 8+ character password
- Enable automatic updates
- Remove apps containing private/sensitive information or connecting to sensitive online services
- Use your phone only for less sensitive purposes
- Follow platform-specific guides: [Android](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/android/) | [iOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/ios/)
- **iPhone:** [Enable Lockdown Mode](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/guide/iphone/iph049680987/ios)
- **Android:** Enroll in [Google's Advanced Protection Program](https://support.google.com/android/answer/16339980)

### Recommended: Install GrapheneOS

For enhanced security on Android:

- Purchase a Google Pixel (ideally [8a](https://store.google.com/us/product/pixel_8a) or newer)
- Have a trusted technologist install [GrapheneOS](https://grapheneos.org/)
- Create separate profiles (admin, work, banking, social media)
- Use GrapheneOS's built-in browser and PDF reader

---

## Prepare Your Computer

Follow platform-specific security guides: [macOS](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/mac/) | [Windows](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/windows/) | [Linux](https://securityinabox.org/en/phones-and-computers/linux/)

If you must keep information on your computer, [encrypt your hard drive](https://securityinabox.org/en/files/secure-file-storage/#consider-encrypting-your-whole-device).

### Recommended: Use Tails

[Tails](https://tails.net/about/index.en.html) is an operating system that runs from a USB stick or SD card. When shut down, no traces remain on the hard drive or RAM. All internet traffic routes through [the Tor network](http://torproject.org/).

- [Install Tails](https://tails.net/install/index.en.html)
- [Getting started with Tails](https://tails.net/doc/first_steps/index.en.html)
- [Set up persistent storage](https://tails.net/doc/persistent_storage/index.en.html) for encrypted file storage

### Optional: Install Qubes OS

For protection against online attacks, consider [Qubes OS](https://www.qubes-os.org/), which isolates different compartments ("qubes") on your computer. Configure using [the SecureDrop guide for journalists and human rights workers](https://github.com/freedomofpress/securedrop-workstation).

> **Note:** Qubes OS protects against remote hacking but may not protect data from forensic tools during physical device seizure.

## Review Your Password Strategy

Weak passwords are the weakest point of any encryption or secure service.

- [Use strong, unique passwords](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/passwords)
- [Use a secure password manager](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/password-managers)
- [Enable 2-factor authentication](https://securityinabox.org/en/passwords/2fa) on all accounts
- Back up 2FA codes securely—consider [Proton Authenticator](https://proton.me/support/back-up-2fa-codes)

---

## Secure Your Communications

### Messaging

- [Read secure messaging recommendations](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/secure-chat)
- Use apps with end-to-end encryption by default, ideally [Signal](https://securityinabox.org/en/communication/tools/#more-secure-text-voice-and-video-chat-applications)

### Internet Connections

- [Browse the web more securely](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/safer-browsing)
- Connect only through [a trusted VPN](https://securityinabox.org/en/internet-connection/circumvention/#choose-a-vpn-service)

### Accessing Online Services

When accessing sensitive email or cloud services, use a browser and log out when finished. Avoid using dedicated apps—this reduces traces of your activities on your devices.

---

## Emergency Planning

### Choose Trusted Contacts

- Securely share passwords for critical accounts (including Google/iCloud) with trusted contacts
- Ask them to suspend your social media accounts and wipe your phone remotely if you're arrested
- Compile a list of digital security helplines for your contacts

### Set Up Emergency Apps

- **Android:** Configure the [Personal Safety app](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9319337) for emergency actions (calling for help, sharing location, recording video)
- **iPhone:** Use [Emergency SOS](https://support.apple.com/guide/personal-safety/emergency-call-text-iphone-apple-watch-ips4f0cd709b) to alert emergency contacts and share live video or photos

---

## During Detention

- Keep all devices **off** if possible
- Only turn on your phone to call trusted contacts (if permitted), then switch it off again
- **Do not unlock your devices**

---

## After Release

- **Do not use returned devices** until an expert runs forensic analysis to check for malware
- Be prepared to replace devices entirely—confirming they are clean may be impossible
